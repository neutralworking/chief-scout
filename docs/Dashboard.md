CORE ELEMENTS
Reports
A chronological list of reports shown on the dashboard homepage with all scouts reports in list order of when they were uploaded to the system. The initial info shown will be ‘Report Name - Date of Fixture - Scout - Date Uploaded’. If you click on the Report Name this will go to that report. If you click on the Scout name, this will go to their historical list of reports completed.
￼
Reporting Structure
A consistent reporting structure with ease of use and ability to complete on platform, as well as App (see below). Reports broken down into specific reports relating to whether it was a Match Report, or a position specific Player Report.
￼
 
￼
 
Each report will be position specific and based on current reporting structure as seen below. Within each report there will be a set scoring system with a total of 12 scores on every position (score on each attribute plus a score on gut instinct), with a total score out of 48.

Match Reports
When scouts attend matches, a Match Report must be submitted, complete with a diagram view of the starting line ups and substitutes (see below), as well as relevant information on each player (see below). At the top of the report the scout will be able to enter the basic match details (formations, scores, conditions and report type - live/video). Ideally, the reports will be already updated to a basic level with the fundamental information (players, scorers etc) already in the system. *to be discussed.

 The report structure will be fully editable and ideally have the basic information already in the system, with the ease of use and functionality as a priority.

Individual Player Reports
Individual player reports, as mentioned above, will be position specific and relate to the clubs positional requirements (see external document for detailed breakdown of Positional Requirements). Again, the individual reports will still have the basic match information as seen above in the Match Report, including the starting line ups diagram. Each position specific report will be broken down into four primary areas (Physical, With The Ball, Without The Ball, Personality) with specific characteristics within those categories to be scored and commented on, along with a final Gut Instinct score. As mentioned, each report with have a total of 12 categories to score, with each attribute being scored out of four (1 = poor, 2 = didn’t show enough quality to fit our profile, 3 = shows potential to fit the profile, 4 = fits the profile).
￼
    
As within the Match Report, the scout selects the Report Type (Position Specific), the Player (by searching) and then the fixture it relates to (see below left). Similarly to the Match Report, the individual Player Report will have an ‘Overview’ where scouts can include a brief description of the game, individual performance and any other relevant information they see fit.
 
￼
 
The player reports are the broken down into the four categories with attributes based on the specific position, with drop down options to mark a score out of four (as mentioned above) with an additional text box for the scout to enter supporting text for the grade/score given.

Playing Styles Insights 
A Playing Styles tool will provide objective definitions of tactical preferences of teams, enabling us to compare and contrast similar playing styles when identifying suitable targets. This will be a industry leading tool that can provide detailed analysis of teams playing styles (to be defined), across different leagues, where we can highlight similar teams or players to delve further into or to filter before acting further. 
Example/draft definitions can be seen below, but are subject to change/define: 
• Direct Play• Counter Attack • Maintenance• Build-Up 
• Crossing• High Press • Fast Tempo 
The specific definitions of each playing style will need to be clearly defined in order for teams to be objectively analysed.

Snapshot Tool 
As part of the recruitment strategy a numeric grading system has been created (see below) to highlight and shortlist players based upon a set criteria. Primarily used to highlight up-and-coming/young prospects, this tool is used to provide weekly insights to UK based players playing in the Premier League, Championship, League 1 and league 2.

Furthermore, as seen above, the players are recorded weekly with trend data compiled to provide weekly, monthly and seasonal averages which highlight key performing players to then view live. Additionally it is used to support our current live scouting structure. If this system can be also built into the platform it will be invaluable in not only providing relevant insights but to also save time and resources elsewhere. 

A tab or widget can be added to the dashboard for the Snapshot Tool to have a separate role in the system where scouts can manipulate and use to their benefit, as well as using it to highlight specific needs. Ultimately, this will be a fully customisable and automate trend data and snapshot section where each week the selected leagues get published for viewing. Additionally, a list of every player at 18 or below involved in match day squads for selected leagues are also highlighted to prompt scouting procedures for U23s/U18s.

Audit Tool 
An Audit Tool will be essential to keep track of, and log platform activity for historical reference and review. By storing historical activity the department can refer back to any changes that have been made such as players being deleted from shortlists, players added to shortlists, notes added, teams/players watched/reported, reports uploaded etc. 
￼
 
￼
 
The tool can be viewed by date, in chronological order or in categories that specify certain actions (i.e. players removed, players added, report added, note added etc). 
￼
Furthermore the Audit Tool can be used in a review process to critically review the recruitment processes over a season or specific transfer window (e.g. reviewing a summer transfer window as to players watched/ discarded, players highlighted, signed and actions that have been taken). By having a critical review, the department can highlight any specific needs or areas for improvement going forward (i.e. positional biases) and providing context to why certain players were targeted or not.

	⁃	Compact graph on profile, clicking on it navigates to fuller statistical overview
	⁃	Check training data (PLAYER PROFILE - TRAINING)
	⁃	View player’s medical notes/injury history (PLAYER PROFILE - HEALTH)
	⁃	Assess current squad situation and how his departure would affect squad makeup (SQUAD - DEPTH)
	⁃	View future depth charts and see if there will be a solution in the position in future years (SQUAD - FUTURE DEPTH)
	⁃	Compare player to other’s in the squad (PLAYER PROFILE - COMPARISON)
	⁃	Check contractual information (PLAYER PROFILE - CONTRACT)
	⁃	Speak to player’s agent (PLAYER PROFILE - CONTRACT)
	⁃	View player’s playing history for club and country (PLAYER PROFILE - HISTORY)
	⁃	Check recent news about their player and their social media accounts (PLAYER PROFILE - MEDIA)
	⁃	View player’s FM biography (PLAYER PROFILE - BIO)
	⁃	Link to Wikipedia in language of user’s choosing

Club Profiles - Each club will also have a variety of useful tabs displaying everything that the user could possibly wish to know about the organisation.
