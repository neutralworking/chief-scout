
# Next Generation 2021: 60 of the best young talents in world football

From ‘the next Alphonso Davies’ to Dortmund’s Moukoko, we pick 60 of the most talented players born in 2004

![](https://media.guim.co.uk/e3bc745a291f72c4429d7b06923513065033f7e9/0_113_3200_1921/500.jpg)

Valentín Barco

ClubBoca Juniors

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born23 July 2004

Plays in the same position as Roberto Carlos and wants to continue in defence despite his coaches preferring him to play in midfield because of his technical ability. At the time of writing he has already played three times for Boca’s first team and is part of the Argentina under-17 set-up. He is extremely good going forward and has a mean free-kick, capable of scoring dead-ball goals with his left foot. Stands out with his bright red hair and promises to be a future star. **[Juan Carlos Pasman](https://twitter.com/TotiPasman?lang=en)**  
[Watch Valentín Barco in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RNpDnwSBbz0)  
  
**September 2022 update** Named player of the Under-20 L’Alcudia friendly tournament for Argentina, but then picked up an unfortunate injury with the Boca reserves just as he was about to return to the first team. “My dream of playing in Europe is always there and it’s what I’m aiming for,” claims the flame-haired youngster, who reportedly has a £14m release clause.. “But first I have to play and win titles at Boca.”  
  
**October 2023 update** “He does everything well. He’s got so much skill, he strikes the ball so well and he’s growing alongside the bigger stars at the club,” Boca legend Juan Román Riquelme claimed of the midfielder recently. With interest from Brighton and Manchester City mounting in recent weeks, a new improved contract is set to be offered to the 19-year-old star that would make him one of the highest paid players in the Argentinian league.

Luka Romero

ClubLazio

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born18 November 2004

Born in Mexico to Argentinian parents, he moved to Spain at the age of three. Not only that, his physical resemblance to Leo Messi is quite remarkable (although he is right-footed). He can play across all positions in attacking midfield. Picked up by Mallorca, he was – inevitably – dubbed the new Messi when he made his debut for the club in La Liga at the age of 15 in June 2020. A summer move to Lazio followed and he promptly played his first Serie A game in the second league match of the season, against Spezia. Has represented Argentina at under-15 level but can also represent Spain or Mexico. What will his choice be? **[Juan Carlos Pasman](https://twitter.com/TotiPasman?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Called up to the senior Argentina side for their World Cup qualifiers in March, dealing another blow to the country of his birth, Mexico, who had also hoped to win his services. Lazio are now desperately waiting for the right-wing starlet to turn 18 in November in order to tie him down a new five-year contract.  
  
**October 2023 update** Scored his first Serie A goal at the end of last year to help Lazio beat Monza 1-0, before walking out of the Rome club on a free in the summer. Played for Argentina at the Under-20 World Cup held on home soil in May, and his wonderful solo effort against New Zealand, which saw him pick the ball up in his own half before rifling a shot past the Kiwis keeper from outside the box, drew obvious comparisons. “I’d prefer it if people didn’t call me the new Messi,” Romero told TyC Sports. “There’s only one Messi and I want to make my own name in football as Luka Romero.” Signed for Milan in the summer.

![](https://media.guim.co.uk/fc187533245384927110f2934a653fd32d4c5e96/0_110_3088_1853/500.jpg)

Joshua Rawlins

ClubPerth Glory

PositionDefender

Born23 April 2004

An intelligent full-back with enough versatility to play anywhere across the backline and in defensive midfield, Rawlins is a graduate of Perth Glory’s academy and was the youngest player to start in Australia’s top flight last season aged 16 years and 272 days. The swift promotion – he started eight of his 12 games – under manager Richard Garcia was little surprise given he went to the 2019 Fifa Under-17 World Cup in Brazil while still a member of the under-15 squad and in the same year came off the bench in a friendly with Manchester United. Last year Rawlins became the youngest player overall in the rebooted Asian Champions League when he debuted against Shanghai Shenhua – the first of four group-stage appearances. **[Emma Kemp](https://twitter.com/emmavkemp)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Was a bright spark in a dark season for Perth Glory in 2021-22; starting 15 games and making seven substitute appearances while also representing Australia at the AFC Under-23 Asian Cup. Earned a move to FC Utrecht on a long-term deal and is now starting regularly in Domstedelingen’s youth setup.  
  
**October 2023 update** Playing for the under-23 Utrecht side and still yet to make his senior debut, but has impressed in his youth national team appearances. Captained the Young Socceroos to the quarter-finals at the Under-20 Asian Cup in March where they lost on penalties to Uzbekistan. Started in central defence for the Olyroos in June where he helped keep two clean sheets and impressed as one of the squad’s youngest players.

Mohamed Toure

ClubAdelaide United

PositionForward

Born26 March 2004

Adelaide manager Carl Veart has described him as a “player who can go all the way to the top”, which is why the Guinea-born Australia striker is staying put for now. As other young local talents with little match experience rush abroad at the first chance, Toure is taking the time to develop and condition his body to utilise his speed – he can run up to 36km/h but has only two senior starts. In February 2020 he became the A-League’s youngest-ever goal scorer at 15 years and 326 days, having been drafted into the senior squad as injury cover. Since then the younger brother of fellow Reds forward Al Hassan, and older sibling of youth player Musa, has scored a further three goals over 15 appearances, mainly off the bench. **[Emma Kemp](https://twitter.com/emmavkemp)**  
[Watch Mohamed Toure in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8pMyzehGbkM)  
  
**September 2022 update** Battled injury during Adelaide United’s 2021-22 A-League Men season but still earned a move, alongside teammate Yaya Dukuly, to Ligue 1 side Stade de Reims. Has already begun scoring for the club’s youth team.  
  
**October 2023 update** After transferring to Reims, Touré impressed for the reserve team, scoring eight goals in 14 appearances, before making his Ligue 1 debut as a substitute in May. But in search of more senior game time the forward made the move to Ligue 2 club Paris FC on loan. Touré got his first national team call-up for the AFC Under-23 Asian Cup qualifiers but had to withdraw because of injury.

Luka Reischl

ClubRed Bull Salzburg/Liefering

PositionForward

Born10 February 2004

Red Bull Salzburg are famous for nurturing young players, and Reischl, who joined the club at the age of 11, has long been considered the best Salzburg prospect born in 2004, having scored goals for fun at all levels. It is hardly surprising that Manchester City and Tottenham were linked to him last year, but equally unsurprising that Salzburg refused to sell. Instead, Reischl made his debut for Liefering – who act as Salzburg’s reserve team in the second division – a year ago, and scored two goals and made three assists, mainly coming off the bench. This term he has two goals already and the club expects him to be ready for promotion to the first team by 2022. “It is clear that Luka has a special talent. If he is handled in the right way we will hear a lot about him in the future,” says the former Liefering coach Bo Svensson, who is now at Mainz. **[Michael Yokhin](https://twitter.com/Yokhin)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Still at Liefering but Salzburg showed that they believe in him by tying him down to 2026 with a new contract in February. Has five goals in 53 second division matches, and will hope to improve that ratio this season.  
  
**October 2023 update** The bad news is that the goals have not started to flow as expected for the youngster but the good news is that he has made his debut for the under-21s at international level and has a goal in three games for them. Began the season in Liefering's starting XI but suffered a shoulder injury in the first game and has not been able to return since.

Pierre Dwomoh

ClubRoyal Antwerp

PositionMidfielder

Born21 June 2004

Every top talent comes with a manual – but not all of them are easy to read. One and a half years ago Pierre Dwomoh was promoted to Genk’s first-team squad, with the announcement that he was one of the biggest talents in their youth ranks. Last season he made his official debut, but then things went wrong. His ambition is his worst sin it seems. The gifted midfielder constantly needs challenges. He is fast, technically strong, tall, but also has an explosive edge. A feisty row with under-21 manager Hans Somers was the beginning of the end at Genk. He was sent to the B-squad but it didn’t work out. He pushed for a move to Milan but finally signed for Antwerp last summer. The ambitious Belgian club paid €2m in the hope they will sell him on for a bigger fee in a few years. In his manual there is a warning sign and no guarantee that he will deliver but all the tools are there. **[Kristof Terreur](https://twitter.com/HLNinEngeland)**  
  
  
**September 2022 update** His first season at Antwerp did not go quite according to plan and he has been loaned out to Braga in Portugal for the 2022-23 campaign.  
  
**October 2023 update** The temporary deal with Braga was made even more temporary when his loan was cut short in January. A move to Oostende followed, where he saw more action and he is now on loan at RWD Molenbeek, where he has began this season in a central midfield position. Could this be his breakthrough year?

Roméo Lavia

ClubManchester City

PositionMidfielder

Born6 January 2004

At Anderlecht they were sad and no wonder why. It was the summer of 2020 and they had lost their biggest talent, Roméo Lavia, on a free to Manchester City. In the Belgian Pro League he would already have been a regular, but Lavia chose the difficult way. “As a young player you have one chance out of a million to break through at City”, says Vincent Kompany, the former Manchester City captain and now Anderlecht manager. “But Romeo can do it. He’s a rare talent.” Lavia is a strong, mature and ball-playing defensive midfielder. His role models are Fernandinho and Sergio Busquets. He likes defending, playing the ball out with both feet and making forward runs. Two years ago he played in the KDB Cup, Kevin De Bruyne’s tournament in his hometown Drongen and last month he made his City debut alongside Phil Foden and De Bruyne. **[Kristof Terreur](https://twitter.com/HLNinEngeland)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Got further minutes for City in the FA and EFL Cups and was on the bench for several Premier League games before being signed by Southampton for a whopping £10.5m. Began the season in Ralph Hassenhüttl’s starting XI and was linked with a move to Chelsea on deadline day before he suffered a hamstring injury.  
  
**October 2023 update** After a hugely impressive season at Southampton, without being able to prevent them from being relegated, Lavia became the subject of a tug-of-war between Chelsea and Liverpool in the summer of 2023. The west London paid an initial fee of £53m for the 19-year-old and co-sporting directors Laurence Stewart and Paul Winstanley said: "He demonstrated his quality in the Premier League last season at Southampton, showing maturity despite his young age, and is a player we have monitored for some time. We believe he is ready to make an impact at Chelsea throughout the current campaign, and in the coming years."

Aleksandar Kahvic

ClubRed Star Belgrade

PositionForward

Born02 January 2004

The 192cm tall striker is all about goals. Yes, some of it may be down to his imposing physical presence in the youth categories but it is definitely not just that and he is very eager not to get typecast. “I work hard to be as mobile a centre-forward as I can, because that’s the most important thing for a striker,” he says. “I try my best not to rely just on my height and robust build.” Kahvic made an immediate impression when he was signed by Red Star and loaned out to Graficar, their feeder club, scoring no less than eight - eight! - goals on his under-17 debut there. Has continued to impress in the Red Star’s under-19 team, waiting for a chance with the first team. **[Aleksandar Holiga](https://twitter.com/AlexHoliga)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Still without a senior appearance for Red Star, he was loaned out – with an option to buy – to Maccabi Haifa.  
  
**October 2023 update** Only played for Haifa’s youth team and they passed on the option to buy him on a permanent basis but things have turned out OK for Kahvic anyway. He has joined second-tier Serbian side OFK Beograd on loan and, at the time of writing, he has already scored eight goals in seven appearances, including two hat-tricks. “It all comes down to commitment in training,” he says. “It all depends on me and my hard work.”

Ângelo

ClubSantos

PositionForward

Born21 December 2004

The best way to explain how good Ângelo is probably through the stats and records. He is the youngest goalscorer in the history of Copa Libertadores, at the age of 16 years, four months and 16 days, breaking a record which had been held by Juan Carlos Cardena since 1962. At the age of 15 years, 10 months and four days, he also became the second youngest player to make his Santos debut, beating even the king Pelé. Santos have a history of producing stars as the careers of Neymar, Robinho and Rodrygo have proved. Born in Brasília, Ângelo was picked up by the club when he was nine. His style is similar to Rodrygo, mixing technique and a great power when dribbling. “He is a guy that breathes football and I think we have an atmosphere that will help this development,” says the Santos under-17 assistant coach, Bruno Silva. “It’s easy to find legends of our club like Lima and Clodoaldo visiting Vila Belmiro. The Neymar effect is still present here.” **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Progress has stalled somewhat, with Santos' six managers in two years making it impossible to have a settled team. After a hugely positive 2021 Ângelo has been struggling to get into the starting XI recently.  
  
**October 2023 update** Struggled for goals with Santos – scoring only two in the first half of the season – but did enough to catch the attention of Chelsea, who signed him for €15m in August. Will spend the 2023-24 season on loan at Strasbourg, the French club Chelsea bought a majority stake in in June who are managed by Patrick Vieira.

Matheus Nascimento

ClubBotafogo

PositionStriker

Born03 March 2004

The talented striker is known as “Little Cavani” or Cavaninho because of his style of play and long hair. He is the most promising striker for years in a country that has had problems up front in the last two World Cups. He has been at Botafogo since 2015, when he caught the attention of the club coaches who saw his potential. “I was the coach of under-13s and I needed a striker,” remembers Vinicius Durante, the current under-15 coach at Botafogo. Some work friends of mine had told me about this player in the the under-12 team. So I decided to test him in my squad. In his first action, he turned an opponent and shot. He didn’t score but it was incredible. There are some players who you don’t need to watch too much to see how good they are.” Made his first team debut in September 2020 and it is only a question of time before he moves to Europe. **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
[Watch Matheus Nascimento in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J8uOXAKciBo)  
  
**September 2022 update** The “new Botafogo”, with John Textor as owner and with big investment, has not helped Matheus Nascimento’s development. Despite very good stats playing for Brazil at youth level, he has not yet managed to deliver the goods for Botafoga with no goal in the first 17 games in Brasileirão.  
  
**October 2023 update** 2023 has been frustrating for Nascimento so far. Botafogo are fighting for the title but the youngster has been given very few chances to shine.

Savinho

ClubAtlético Mineiro

PositionWinger

Born10 April 2004

“Football these days is a little bit boring. I want to mess it up, bring more joy to the game.” And with those remarks Savinho makes clear what he wants to achieve in football. One of the most skilful youngsters of his generation, he is a promising player from Atlético Mineiro who is inspired by a club legend. “My idol is Ronaldinho. I like him because he is bold. He was different, I like everything about him,” he says. Dalla Dea, coach of Brazil’s Under-17 World Cup winners in 2019, is just one of many who is impressed. “Savinho has the joy of football in him and has the movements of Brazilian football in him,” he says. Playing for Atlético Mineiro since 2014, Savinho caught the attention of Jorge Sampaoli and made his professional debut in September 2020 aged 16 years, five months and nice days, breaking Reinaldo’s record of being the youngest player to play in Brasileirão for the club. **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** The past 12 months could hardly have gone any better for Savinho, won won both the Brasileirão and Copa do Brasil before being sold to Troyes – part of the City Football Group – for €6m. He is spending this season on loan at PSV Eindhoven.  
  
**October 2023 update** It is funny how one tournament can change a player's life for ever. That was certainly the case for Savinho. He was not pulling up any trees at PSV but then had an outstanding Under-20 World Cup in Argentina, where he was one of the best players on show, despite the frustrating defeat against Israel in the quarter-finals. Girona liked what they saw and arranged for a loan deal from Troyes for the 2023-24 campaign and he got off to the best possible start with goals and assists for the league's surprise package.

Vinicius Tobias

ClubInternacional (on loan from Shakhtar)

PositionDefender

Born23 April 2004

Vinicius Tobias has a typical background of a Brazilian footballer from a poor background who saw in sports a way to have a better life. Born in São Paulo and raised in Buraco Quente Favela with five brothers, he did not have an easy upbringing, as Renato Silva, who has run a social project called RSoccer in the city since 2006, helping more than 600 children, tells the Guardian. “He was playing normally and suddenly fainted. So I asked him what happened? He answered he was hungry because hadn’t eaten anything.” One year later, in 2016, he moved to Internacional and his life changed immediately. In the first season, he put on eight kilos and the development in his football was clear to see. “He is a No 10 playing as right-back,” said Jos Boto, the scout at Shakhtar Donetsk who was responsible for the club signing the player in July for €5m. Many experts believe he is a Brazil international in the making. **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** The war in Ukraine stopped Vinicius Tobias’ progress and after a period of not playing he was loaned to Real Madrid Castilla. His first few months in Spain were impressive and Carlo Ancelloti, said that Vinicius may well be the future at Real Madrid. His deal expires next summer, when Real have to decide whether to sign him permanently.  
  
**October 2023 update** The loan contract with Real Madrid was renewed for one more season, but he is still waiting for a chance with the first team. There is hope though as he was included in the squad for the recent Champions League game against Hertha Berlin and sat on the bench, but did not come on.

Nikola Iliev

ClubInter

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born06 June 2004

A tremendously gifted player with great technique, he made his Bulgarian top-flight debut for Botev Plovdiv aged just 15. Having also scored 15 goals in 11 games for Botev’s under-17 side, he drew Ajax’s attention and the Dutch giants were close to signing him at the start of 2020. Iliev decided to wait as there were reports that Manchester United were interested too but when Inter came calling last summer he decided to move abroad. "I've always dreamed of playing for a big European club and this dream is now a reality," he said after the deal was confirmed. The _Nerazzurri_ reportedly paid €500,000 for the youngster, whose dribbling skills and playmaking abilities are truly impressive. **[Metodi Shumanov](https://twitter.com/shumanskoo)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Iliev scored the title-winning goal that won Inter U19’s the Primavera trophy and there have already been talks over a potential contract extension with the Nerazzurri. He has also just been called up to Bulgaria’s senior team for the Nations League games against Gibraltar and North Macedonia.  
  
**October 2023 update** Great progress for the attacking midfielder who made his debut for Bulgaria in 2022 and already has eight caps for the national team. At club level he has joined Bulgarian top-flight side CSKA 1948 on loan from Inter’s Primavera hoping to make a triumphant return to the Nerazzurri next summer.

Jahkeele Marshall-Rutty

ClubToronto FC

PositionWinger

Born16 June 2004

Most young players emerging from Canada are likely to be likened to a certain Alphonso Davies, especially if they beat the Bayern Munich player’s record as the youngest player to have been called up for the national team (even though it was only by 10 days). A fast right winger, his crosses are exceptionally accurate and he can also turn inside and set up the forwards. Toronto FC’s general manager, Alis Curtis, has called him one of “the top young players in all North America”, adding that “he is an incredibly talented young man. While he’s only 15 years old, he plays the game in very mature way.” **[Jaime F Macias](https://twitter.com/Jaimefmacias?lang=en)**  
[Watch Jahkeele Marshall-Rutty in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6bXo7JeUf8Y)  
  
**September 2022 update** Held talks with Club Brugge in the summer after training with Liverpool and Arsenal last winter. Has clocked up various appearances for Toronto FC and was named in the MLS Team of the Week back in February.  
  
**October 2023 update** Remains at Toronto and has racked up nearly 50 MLS appearances at the time of writing. Asked in April what it was like being compared to Alphonso Davies and other players on the Guardian Next Generation list, he told TFC notebook: “I’ve said this from when I was a young age, it’s an honour to be compared to these players, but I don’t like to read those things. When I go home, that’s not what we talk about. It’s just how are you getting better every single day. When I come in, I just want to be the best version of myself and keep learning from these players."

Miguel Monsalve

ClubDeportivo Independiente Medellín

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born27 February 2004

Made his first-team debut in Colombia’s top division in October last year for Medellín, the team he joined aged four. Back then he was told he was still too young to join the club’s youngest age category the Under-7s, but one look at him convinced coaches to turn a blind eye. Ever since, local lad Monsalve has played with age groups too old for him. And in every category he has excelled, turning defences inside out, scoring goals for fun. In 2016, the right-footed midfielder leapt into national headlines after captaining Medellín to the final of the famed Pony Futbol tournament, just as James Rodríguez and Radamel Falcao had done in previous editions. Identifying as a No 10, he is fleet footed, technically gifted and with an eye for goal, yet mature enough to recognise where he needs to improve. “He knows he needs to take things step by step,” explains Medellín coach, Wilmar Valencia, who has worked with 17-year-old Miguel for the last seven years. “First, he needs to consolidate his place at Medellín and with the Colombia youth teams. But if he can do that, he has all the conditions to one day play in Europe.” **[Carl Worswick](https://twitter.com/cworswick)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Made a blistering start to the new Colombian season bagging his first professional goals and establishing himself as Medellín’s No 10, before picking up an untimely injury that ruled him out for a month. Big things are expected from him on his return.  
  
**October 2023 update** Showed flashes of his quality in helping Colombia qualify for the Under-20 World Cup, but then lost his place in the starting XI that went on to reach the quarter-finals at this summer’s Argentina finals. “He is very intelligent,” club manager Alfredo Arias enthused about his playmaker upon taking over at Medellín in June. “He stays behind every day to keep on training and I know he’s only going to get better.”

Marko Brkljaca

ClubHajduk Split

PositionMidfielder

Born15 July 2004

A left-footed box-to-box midfielder in the vein of, say, Georginio Wijnaldum. Regarded the biggest national prospect of his generation by some distance, Brkljaca attracts immediate attention with a smooth first touch and impressive physical capacity. His coaches particularly praise his ability to withstand challenges against older and bigger opponents, but also his sense for timing runs from behind, which regularly gets him into goal-scoring positions. Juventus tried to sign him last winter but they are not the only big club interested. Hajduk will want him to establish himself in the first team before considering any offers and he will get his chance soon enough – he made his senior debut in a cup game in September. **[Aleksandar Holiga](https://twitter.com/AlexHoliga)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Looked very promising last season in the Uefa Youth League and in a couple of off-the-bench senior appearances. This was supposed to be his break-through season but instead things turned ugly when — on the advice of his father, who later spoke about it at length in the media — he turned down a professional contract with Hajduk. The club said they “refused to be blackmailed” and the youngster was removed from the squad, signing instead for bitter rivals Dinamo. He is currently with their Under-19 squad and is still a major prospect.  
  
**October 2023 update** As his former Hajduk teammates reached the Uefa Youth League final, Brkljaca had to settle for game time in the second division, on loan to Dubrava, although he did make one senior appearance for Dinamo in the Cup. Now on loan at Aluminij in the Slovenian top division.

Jeppe Kjær Jensen

ClubAjax

PositionAttacking midfielder/forward

Born1 March 2004

There will be more to come, but Jensen has already set one record that will be impossible to beat (unless the Danish Super League change their rules). On his 16th birthday Jensen became the youngest player to appear in the top flight, coming off the bench for AC Horsens against Randers. He would have played his first game long before that but the rules stipulate that you cannot be younger than 16 to play in the SuperLiga. He soon scored his first goal and was invited to several clubs, visiting some of them before signing for Ajax in August 2020. “He has a unique vision and awareness. He makes the right decision nearly every time. He may lack some strength and physical power because of his young age, but his intelligence makes up for that,” says Bo Henriksen, his coach at AC Horsens, who is now at FC Midtjylland. **[Jesper Haue Hansen](https://twitter.com/jesperhaue)**  
[Watch Jeppe Kjær Jensen in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NBDjNOpu8f8#t=3m55s)  
  
**September 2022 update** The Dane is currently not close to the Ajax first team but he is still very young and time is on his side.

The Swiss FA were not aware of the striker and he never played for youth national teams at any level until then, but his top-division debut proved to be sensational. The 17-year-old scored a very well taken goal in the 2-1 win at Servette, thus becoming the first player born in 2004 to score in the Swiss top division, and never looked back. Physically strong, Besio is an imposing figure in the penalty area, but is also very quick and agile, and possesses a delicate touch. He proved himself with the St Gallen first team in pre-season, and is now a starter, contributing two assists in the first eight matches. His mother used to play handball for the Swiss national team and Alessio looks destined to follow suit in football. **[Michael Yokhin](https://twitter.com/Yokhin)**  
  
  
**September 2022 update** Has made 32 Swiss Super League appearances at the time of writing, although has only managed two goals so far. Is clearly at the right club for the time being with his manager, Peter Zeidler, saying: “Alessio is our biggest talent – and we give young players a chance here.”  
  
**October 2023 update** Had the 2022-23 season completely ruined by injuries and left St Gallen in the summer upon the end of his contract. He had knee surgery in August before signing for Freiburg, where he will start with the club's reserve team in the German third division when back to full fitness.

The latest star in the making to emerge from the Altinordu academy. The official Bundesliga website picked Ince as one of the top 10 players to watch in the 2. Bundesliga this season.

Emre Demir

ClubKayserispor

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born15 January 2004







****************

Became the youngest player to score in the Süper Lig aged just 15, nine months and 25 days. In total Demir scored twice and provided one assist in 16 appearances for Kayserispor last season. Barcelona were so impressed they decided to snap him up before any rivals got involved. The left-footed attacking midfielder will join the La Liga giants next season and will be under contract until 2027 with a €400m release clause. Former Kayserispor sporting director Suleyman Hurma first spotted Demir aged just eight years old. “I am not surprised by his transfer to Barcelona, in fact, I’m surprised it took so long,” he says. “If he keeps developing at the same rate over there you are going to see the rise of a huge star”. **[Emre Sarigul](https://twitter.com/Turkish_Futbol1?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Joined Barcelona this summer after a season with a few league and cup appearances for Kayserispor. He is currently part of the Barcelona B setup where he will still benefit from the advice of the first-team manager, Xavi. “Xavi was one of the best football players in the world and I am very thrilled to work with him and think I will learn a lot from him,” he said.  
  
**October 2023 update** In a bizarre turn of events Demir ended up leaving Barcelona after just six months to join Fenerbahce. Demir cited a change of personnel at Barça and struggling to settle behind his hasty exit. Fenerbahce loaned the youngster to second-tier side Umraniyespor where he will play first team football this season. Demir definitely has potential and is getting much-needed experience under his belt. The Yellow Canaries have nurtured some of Turkish football’s brightest talents recently so Demir is in a good place.

Yegor Yarmolyuk

ClubDnipro-1

PositionMidfielder

Born1 March 2004







****************

Yarmolyuk is often compared to his compatriot, the West Ham player Andriy Yarmolenko, and not only because of the similarity of their names. The 17-year-old is as strong and powerful as the Ukrainian international with the ball and can also play on the wing. There’s much more on offer from the young prospect, though. He is a hardworking, tireless midfieler who can be trusted on the ball with his great vision and outstanding footballing brain as well as out of possession. Yarmolyuk’s best position seems to be centrally as a box-to-box midfielder, as he can demonstrate most of his skills there, one of them being his ability in one-on-one situations, his desire to dribble at high speed and his ability to make key passes. Yarmolyuk made his debut for the Ukraine Under-21 team when he was only 16 years and 146 days old, becoming the second youngest to do so after the former Barcelona defender Dmytro Chygrynskiy. He is the youngest outfield player in Ukraine’s top division to start a match, which he did six days before his debut for the under-21s. **[Artur Petrosyan](https://twitter.com/arturpetrosyan)  
  
**September 2022 update** After scoring four goals and making three assists in his past five games for Ukraine Under-19 side, Yarmolyuk signed a three-year contract with Brentford this July. Had a couple of appearances for the first team in pre-season before being placed with the B team.  
  
**October 2023 update** Continues to progress after his move to Brentford last year. Is now a regular squad member for Thomas Frank’s team in the Premier League and his debut in the English top-flight is surely not far away. Speaking to zoryalondonsk.com about his time in England he said: "One thing I have noticed is that people do like to spend a lot of time in pubs here."**
