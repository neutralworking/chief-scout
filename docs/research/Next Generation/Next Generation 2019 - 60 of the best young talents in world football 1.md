Adel Belkacem Bouzida

ClubParadou AC

PositionWinger

Born28 February 2002

Paradou has become one of Africa’s most productive academies and in the last decade several players have moved over to Europe to start promising careers in the top leagues. Last summer, Haithem Loucif (Angers), Zakaria Naidji (Gil Vicente) and Hicham Boudaoui (Nice) followed that footpath and although Bouzida is still to make his league debut for Paradou, scouts and agents are already monitoring his progress. Blessed with blistering pace and a remarkable dribbling ability, Bouzida is a full-back’s nightmare. With Paradou’s policy of exporting their brightest and best, it seems only a matter of time before you see him playing in Europe. **[Maher Mezahi](https://twitter.com/mezahimaher)**  
  
**October 2020 update** The past 12 months have been quiet for Bouzida. He was rarely called up to the Algerian youth national teams and he did not play senior football, although he did reportedly draw the eye of Southampton last January.  
  
**October 2021 update** His progress has been severely hampered by consecutive injuries and is now falling behind. Needs to stay fit and make first-team appearances to live up to the hype.  
  
**September 2022 update** Injuries delayed Bouzida’s progress as Paradou teammates such as Adem Zorgane and Nadir Benbouali left for Europe. He’s finally healthy, playing regular football, and producing with goals and assists. Should he keep it up for an entire season, you can expect the hype to lurch forward once more.  
  
**October 2023 update** Bouzida is finally playing to his potential. Last season he was one of the best players in the Algerian league, and it would not surprise anyone if he was playing in Europe in the next 12 months.

Matías Palacios

ClubSan Lorenzo

PositionMidfielder

He is a naturally very talented player with a brilliant shot and has scored many goals in his career already. “When I play I try to play the way Riquelme, Andrés Iniesta or Aimar would do,” he told Olé last year. **[Juan Carlos Pasman](https://twitter.com/TotiPasman?lang=en)**

He also turned down an approach from Francesco Totti, who wanted to help him seal a move. Has since signed for Al Ain in the UAE after two seasons with Basel where he played 45 games, scoring three goals.  
  
**October 2023 update** Remains in the UAE’s Pro-League with Al Ain after his $3.3m move from Basel, and they finished league runners-up in 2022-23 under Serhiy Rebrov before the former Spurs striker left to take charge of the Ukraine national team this summer.

PositionWinger

Velasco is the new jewel of Avellaneda, a port city in the Buenos Aires province. **October 2023 update** Called up to the Argentina squad for the start of the South American World Cup qualifiers in September after again performing admirably for FC Dallas in the MLS this season in a more central role. “We put him outside more at the start because he has the right characteristics to play there,” the manager Nico Estévez explained. “But as he’s become more used to what we do here, we’ve played him more inside and seen his qualities and skills there.” Flashed his back tattoo of Diego Maradona to Lionel Messi when the two exchanged shirts after the Dallas-Inter Miami Leagues Cup match in August.
