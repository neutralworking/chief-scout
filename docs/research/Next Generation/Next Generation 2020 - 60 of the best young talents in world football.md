Federico Redondo

ClubArgentinos Juniors

PositionDefensive midfielder

Born18 January 2003

Federico Redondo, son of the former Real Madrid midfielder Fernando, has been with Argentinos Juniors since the age of 10, but his gentle rise through the club’s youth system has accelerated in recent months, with Diego Dabove adding him to the first-team training group in August. In April his father confirmed that there had been interest from English clubs in the deep-lying midfielder but that he needed to “continue maturing and working – he has the ability, the rest depends on him”. **[Juan Carlos Pasman](https://twitter.com/TotiPasman?lang=en)**  
[Watch Federico Redondo in action](https://twitter.com/sc_espn/status/1172265917314740232?lang=en)  
  
**October 2021 update** Scored the winner for the reserves after coming on for the last few minutes of the clásico between Argentinos Juniors and Platense at the start of the year, and has since found the net against both Central Córdoba and San Lorenzo. The avowed Peaky Blinders fan spent pre-season training with the first team in June and was named on the first-team bench by senior team coach Gabriel Milito in July. “I was one year old when my dad retired so I never got to see him play,” the midfielder admits. “But he’ll always be an example to follow, he watches all my games and afterwards we sit down and tells me where I need to improve.”  
  
**September 2022 update** “Little by little he’s becoming a very important player for us,” glowed Argentinos Juniors' manager Gabriel Milito after young Fede shone in only his sixth game for the team in their 1-0 win over Estudiantes in August. “He just understands things so clearly.” With midfielder Fausto Vera leaving for Corinthians a month earlier, the path is now clear for the 19-year-old to solidify his hold on the special No 5 shirt that his father once wore for El Bicho in the late 1980s.  
  
**October 2023 update** Firmly established in the first team at Argentinos Juniors, his impressive performances over the last year have pricked European attention. Linked to Barcelona with comparisons offered to Sergio Busquets, the right-footer nonetheless insists he wants to follow in the footsteps of his dad, Fernando. “First I want to be champions with Argentinos Juniors and then I want to aim for the very top. I would love to play for Real Madrid and be part of the Argentina team."

Darío Sarmiento

ClubEstudiantes de La Plata

PositionWinger

Born29 March 2003

Alejandro Sabella, the former Sheffield United and Leeds midfielder who coached Argentina to the 2014 World Cup final and previously played for and managed Estudiantes, has a nickname for Darío Sarmiento: Hallelujah. “He fills me with hope for his future and for all of us who love the club so much,” Sabella says. Sarmiento, a diminutive left-footer who under Gabriel Milito, the current Estudiantes coach, has moved from No10 to the right wing, became the second-youngest first-team player in the club’s history when he made his debut last October and his first start followed in March, three weeks before his 17th birthday. There have been persistent rumours of interest from Manchester City in recent months. “This is just the start, I have to be calm and I don’t have to hurry,” Sarmiento says. “I still have so many things to learn.” **[Juan Carlos Pasman](https://twitter.com/TotiPasman?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Finally snapped up by City, for a fee that could rise to $12m. “As soon as I heard about the possibility of joining Manchester City, I didn’t think twice,” exclaimed Sarmiento. “I was so happy, I didn’t want to hear from anyone else.” In June, the winger was loaned to City Football Group partner, Girona, to gain first-team experience playing in the Spanish Segunda División.  
  
**September 2022 update** Provided two assists in the nine games he played for promotion-winning Girona in the Spanish second division last season, before joining another City Group club, Montevideo City Torque, on another year-loan in July. Still awaiting his debut for the relegation-threatened Uruguayan club.  
  
**October 2023 update** Dogged by muscular injuries over the last year, Sarmiento failed to play a single minute while on loan at sister club City Torque before returning to Manchester City to build up his fitness. Had pondered a move back to former club Estudiantes, but instead City once again sent him back to Uruguay with City Torque until the end of the year.

Yusuf Demir

ClubRapid Vienna

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born2 June 2003

They say he could be the "Austrian Messi" and although such comparisons are extremely unhelpful, Demir does catch your imagination. The left-footed playmaker possesses remarkable dribbling skills and a phenomenal passing range – and he is also a brilliant free-kick specialist. At 15, he was named player of the tournament at the Under-19 Mercedes Benz Junior Cup. At 16, he became the youngest Rapid Vienna player to play a Bundesliga game. In September he scored his first goal and is expected to play a major role this season. Demir has already represented Austria at under-21 level, and there is pressure to call him up for the senior squad so that he does not switch to Turkey, for whom he can also play. A lot of top clubs have been linked with him too, including Manchester United, Manchester City, Bayern Munich, Borussia Dortmund, Real Madrid and Barcelona. **[Michael Yokhin](https://twitter.com/Yokhin)**  
[Watch Yusuf Demir in action](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GHZKGtJHJRc)  
  
**October 2021 update** Sensationally joined Barcelona on loan in July 2021, with the Catalan club having an option to sign him permanently for €10m. He has already become the youngest foreigner since Lionel Messi to play for the first team and has three caps for Austria. A dizzying 12 months for the youngster.  
  
**September 2022 update** Having made nine appearances for Barcelona, he returned to Rapid in January before being sold to Galatasaray for around €6m in September. He has four Austria caps at the time of writing.  
  
**October 2023 update** Failed to get playing time at Galatasaray in the beginning of 2023 and was loaned out to Basel in the summer. The Swiss side are going through a turbulent time at the moment but Demir hopes to shine through the chaos and put his career back on track. There have been reports that Roma have an option to sign him from Galatasaray as part of the Nicolò Zaniolo deal.

Jhon Jáder Durán

ClubEnvigado

PositionStriker

Born13 December 2003

Aged 16 and already a towering 6ft tall, Jhon Jáder has made a habit of being ahead of schedule so far in his fledgling career. A revelation in youth football after the Medellin-born left footer was converted from winger to striker by the Under-15s coach Wilberth Perea, Durán went on to play for the Colombia Under-17s while still 15, before being promoted to the Under-20s national team that is currently preparing for the South American Youth Championships on home soil in 2021. In 2019, he became the second youngest goalscorer in Colombian top-flight history at the tender age of 15 years and 263 days with a goal after nine minutes. Strong, versatile and with a devilish burst of pace, he emerged from the same famed Envigado academy as James Rodríguez and Juan Fernando Quintero, then aged 11. Nephew to an ex-player who is now a football manager, Durán’s development has since been in good hands with several observers comparing his robust style to that of Romelu Lukaku. “Both are similar players, but for me Durán could develop to be even more complete,” coach Perea explains. “At the age of 15 he’d already broken all the limits. He has this strong character to always try risks – a bit like Tino Asprilla would do.” **[Carl Worswick](https://twitter.com/cworswick)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Became the youngest signing in MLS history when Chicago Fire swooped this year, thus stealing a headstart on several European clubs that had been sniffing around the young striker. “One of the best talents in world football is coming to Chicago,” the Fire tweeted upon announcing the deal. “Jhon is a very special and highly rated young talent,” sporting director Georg Heitz said. Unable to officially join the club until his 18th birthday at the start of next year he’s been progressing nicely as tiny Envigado punch above their weight at the top of the Colombian first division.  
  
**September 2022 update** Recently called up to the first Colombia squad under new manager Nestor Lorenzo and nicely settling in the MLS where he’s picking up regular minutes for Chicago Fire.  
  
**October 2023 update** Signed for Aston Villa in an £18m deal at the start of the year, becoming Unai Emery’s back-up striker behind Ollie Watkins following Danny Ings’ departure to West Ham. "He will need to play matches, gain minutes and experiences and confidence," Emery gushed after the 18-year-old scored a late stunner to set Villa on their way to a 3-1 September win over Crystal Palace. "Scoring like he did today, it is coming quick. But the most important thing is his quality, his capacity, his skill, his big potential.” A regular in the Colombia squad, he also scored his first international goal against Japan in March.
