Trae Coyle

ClubArsenal

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born11 January 2001

Coyle is that rare player who is not only brave enough to try the things that others would not risk but has the talent to pull them off. He shot to attention when he scored from the halfway line as a 14-year-old in an Arsenal youth game against Barcelona and there have been other moments of impudence, such as a Messi-style assist from a penalty. Comfortable on the left or right, as the No10 (his favourite role) or even as a No9, Coyle brings the ‘wow’ factor. He left school a year early and trained full-time at London Colney last season, effectively as a scholar, when he starred for the club’s under-18s until a knee injury forced him out. **[David Hytner](https://twitter.com/DaveHytner)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Has been set back by injury, particularly at the start of last season, but still scored goals for Arsenal’s under-18s. Also made his Under-23 breakthrough last season.  
  
**October 2019 update** Played a key role as the Under-18s won the Premier League South last season. Expected to kick on now with the Under-23s.  
  
**October 2020 update** Coyle was an eyebrow-raising addition to the prestigious “Golden Boy” longlist for 2020, given he had never played a first-team game at the time. But his talent is little secret and he has shown it early in a season-long loan to Gillingham in League One, scoring twice in his first four senior appearances.  
  
**October 2021 update** That promising start at Gillingham fizzled out and Arsenal recalled Coyle from his loan in January. With first-team football at the Emirates a distant prospect he took the bold move of joining Lausanne-Sport, from the Swiss top flight, in a permanent deal during the summer. He was involved in four of their first seven league games, starting once.  
  
**September 2022 update** The move to Lausanne has been broadly successful on a personal level for Coyle, even if the club finished bottom of Switzerland’s top flight in 2021-22. A regular on the left wing, he began second-tier life with two goals and looks likely to be a key player in their efforts to secure a quick return.

Brennan Camp

ClubBournemouth

PositionDefender

Born12 October 2000

The 16-year-old centre-back has witnessed Bournemouth’s rapid rise since joining the club’s under-nines. “He’s an out-and-out defender who wants to keep clean sheets,” said Alan Connell, who was appointed Bournemouth’s under-18s manager in July. “He’s a good listener, he’s competitive, he wants to learn and he can play the ball out from the back, which we demand of all of them.” Camp, from Portland, joined the Cherries after being scouted playing for Weymouth as an eight-year-old. He was part of Bournemouth’s youth team that beat Wigan Athletic in the national EFL Youth Alliance Cup final in May. Camp’s grandfather is the former Bristol City and Manchester City midfielder Gerry Gow. **[Ben Fisher](https://twitter.com/benfisherJ)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Now captain of the Under-18s, and has also featured for the Under-23s.  
  
**October 2019 update** Signed a two-year contract this summer after captaining the Under-18s last season. Has earned international recognition with Scotland Under-19s.  
  
**October 2020 update** Has returned to Weymouth on a season-long loan after playing an integral role in helping the club win promotion to the National League last season.  
  
**October 2021 update** The Under-21s captain made his Bournemouth debut as a young team were dismantled 6-0 by Norwich in the League Cup in August. His contract was extended until the end of the season in the summer.  
  
**September 2022 update** Joined Eastleigh on a permanent deal in the summer after spending last season on loan at the National League club.

Danny Cashman

ClubBrighton

PositionForward

Born08 January 2001

The forward, who can also play as a No 10, has been recognised by England at youth level, most recently at under-17s. The Crawley-born first-year scholar has trained with the Brighton’s first team on occasions this season. “He’s intelligent, creative and has an eye for goal,” said Ian Buckman, Brighton’s under-18s manager. Cashman, who helped Brighton to the fifth round of the FA Youth Cup in February, scored a hat-trick against Newcastle United last month and, according to Buckman, has “shown improvements in the defensive side of his game”. Brighton’s impressive Falmer training base is home to the Category One academy, as well as to Chris Hughton’s side. “We are all conscious that with the first team doing so well, it raises the bar for us,” Buckman said. **[Ben Fisher](https://twitter.com/benfisherJ)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Cashman offered flashes of his quality through his first year as a scholar, enduring the ups and downs expected in his debut full season at that level, but finished strongly and has carried that form into the current campaign. He is still a striker for whom Brighton have high hopes.  
  
**October 2019 update** The forward is competing for game time in a highly talented Under-23s group with Aaron Connolly, last season’s Premier League 2 player of the season, who made his senior debut against Manchester City in August.  
  
**October 2020 update** Signed a new one-year contract, with the option of a further 12 months, in August and remains a regular for Simon Rusk’s under-23 side. Could leave on loan to gain first-team minutes.  
  
**October 2021 update** The striker joined Coventry City on a three-year deal in the summer and was immediately loaned to Rochdale, for whom he scored three goals in his first dozen appearances.  
  
**September 2022 update** Joined League Two Walsall on loan in September from Coventry. Last season at Rochdale was moderately successful with 23 League Two appearances but only two goals.

Michael Fowler

ClubBurnley

PositionForward

Born13 May 2001

Fowler made a big impression the day he signed a professional scholarship with Burnley, turning up in his school-prom tuxedo. “Why not?” he said. “It’s the biggest day of my life so far.” The striker released by Sunderland has not taken long to get noticed on the pitch since his move to Lancashire either. He has three goals this season, as well as three in the CEE Youth Cup staged in Prague over summer, where Burnley reached the final but were beaten by Everton. **[Paul Wilson](https://twitter.com/paulwilsongnm)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Led the scoring charts for Burnley U18s with goals in his first four games, but unable to add to that sequence since being sent out on loan to local side Padiham of the North West Counties Premier League. “The youth team will miss him,” coach Danny Cadamarteri explained. “But we have a strategy of introducing players to men’s football at a certain stage to help their development.”  
  
**October 2019 update** The striker was released this year after loans at Padiham and Radcliffe, with the Under-23s coach Steve Stone admitting Fowler was a victim of the club’s rising standards, arguing he would have a better chance of first-team action outside the Premier League. Soon picked up by Fleetwood Town, where he has yet to make a league appearance but scored the winner in a pre-season friendly against Wrexham.  
  
**October 2020 update** Released by Burnley, picked up by Fleetwood, now out on loan at lower league level.  
  
**October 2021 update** Sent out on loan to FC United of Manchester during 2020-21. Released by Fleetwood at the end of the season and has since moved to the US to join Coastal Carolina University’s football programme.  
  
**September 2022 update** After a spell in America where he completed a scholarship, Fowler returned to the UK and is currently playing for Dunston UTS in the Northern Premier League.

Callum Hudson-Odoi

ClubChelsea

PositionForward

Born7 November, 2000

Hudson-Odoi caught the eye as a schoolboy last season when he was outstanding in Chelsea’s under-18s, scoring eight goals in 25 appearances and netting in the fifth round, semi-final and final of the FA Youth Cup, before starring for England at the under-17s European championship. He excelled in partnership with Jadon Sancho, who joined Borussia Dortmund from Manchester City in August, and scored his third goal of the tournament in the final, when England were beaten on penalties by Spain. At 16 he is already ensconced in Chelsea’s under-23s set-up and, with Juventus and Benfica having scouted him in the hope of luring him away, is committed to a three-year deal at Stamford Bridge. One to watch at the under-17s World Cup in India in October, Hudson-Odoi is likely to gain senior experience – most likely on loan – in the next 18 months. **[Dominic Fifield](https://twitter.com/domfifield)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The winger was a key member of the England U17s who won the World Cup in India last October, and a regular scorer for Chelsea in the EFL Trophy last season before breaking into Antonio Conte's first-team with Premier League and FA Cup appearances. He was the club's outstanding performer in pre-season under Maurizio Sarri, who has resisted attempts by rivals to take the 17-year-old on loan so Hudson-Odoi could be properly integrated, and utilised, within the first-team group.  
  
**October 2019 update** The 18-year-old was heavily courted by Bayern Munich last season as he grew frustrated at life on the periphery of Maurizio Sarri's side, and was so inclined to follow the path trodden by Jadon Sancho to the Bundesliga that he even handed in a transfer request in January. Chelsea resisted and the winger was eventually granted his chance, duly excelling to the extent that he earned two caps for England in March. A ruptured achilles ended his campaign prematurely, but has now signed a new contract at Stamford Bridge and is back to full fitness.  
  
**October 2020 update** A year of frustration after the bonus of recovering from his injury quickly, with Frank Lampard only selecting him to start seven Premier League matches. Could be allowed to leave on loan this season.  
  
**October 2021 update** Has stayed at Chelsea and played a fair amount at the start of the 20-21 season but found himself increasingly marginalised under Thomas Tuchel. He was included in match day squads, and still is, but was kept on the bench for Chelsea's final five Champions League games without playing a minute as they won it.  
  
**September 2022 update** More out than in for Chelsea last season and suffered with injury over the closing months. Did start the European Super Cup and Club World Cup wins. Has gone to Bayer Leverkusen on loan this season.

Sean Robertson

ClubCrystal Palace

PositionWinger

Born6 June, 2001

Sean Robertson is similar in style and stature to Wayne Routledge, another who graduated through Palace’s youth ranks, and has stood out among a group of under-16s elevated prematurely to under-18 level towards the end of last season before starting their scholarships. He scored on debut as Palace eased beyond Hull, then netted again to help check Coventry City’s 11-match winning streak. There were seven appearances last term, when Robertson was 15, and he has featured regularly this season, most notably scoring a brace in a 4-0 thrashing of Huddersfield. Skilful, quick and direct, the winger was at school in Camberwell and has been at Palace since the age of 12. He has even been granted a taste of training with the first team of late, particularly while senior players have been absent on international duty.**[Dominic Fifield](https://twitter.com/domfifield)**  
  
**September 2018 update** Has excelled for Palace’s U18s and is now a permanent fixture in the U23s having impressed the coach, Richard Shaw, over his nine appearances last season. He scored seven goals in 23 appearances for the U18s last term from right wing and, still under 17, is expected to sign a professional contract at the end of the season.  
  
**September 2019 update** Featured predominantly on the right wing of Palace’s Under-23s side before dropping back into Paddy McCarthy’s Under-18 squad towards the end of the season and netted a fine hat-trick in a 4-1 victory over Watford towards the end of the campaign. As a result he was one of only two second-year scholars to be awarded a one-year professional contract at the end of the season.  
  
**September 2020 update** One of five players in Palace’s development squad who signed contract extensions in July and is tipped to head out on loan this season.  
  
**September 2021 update** Signed another new contract at Palace last month after playing regularly for the club’s Under-23 side having featured in a pre-season friendly against Brentford B.  
  
**September 2022 update** Joined Forest Green Rovers on a permanent deal in the summer after leaving Palace.

Anthony Gordon

ClubEverton

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born24 February 2001

Everton’s under-18s endured a painful night in the first ’mini-derby’ of the season recently – losing 3-1 to Steven Gerrard’s Liverpool at Kirkby, where Curtis Jones scored twice – but not before Gordon gave another demonstration of his potential. The attacking midfielder is developing a reputation for his ability to beat an opponent and clinical finishing, and both attributes were in evidence as he put Everton ahead against their local rivals. The 16-year-old was one of 12 academy graduates taken on full-time by Everton this summer. **[Andy Hunter](https://twitter.com/AHunterGuardian)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Became the first person born in the 21st century to play for Everton’s first team when – aged 16 years and 286 days - he appeared as a substitute in the Europa League win over Apollon Limassol last December. Impressed for the under-18s throughout last season.  
  
**October 2019 update** Given a new four-year contract in March in recognition of his excellent progress and part in the Under-23s’ double-winning campaign. Scored a fine goal in this season’s EFL Trophy win over Mansfield and has started training with the first team.  
  
**October 2020 update** Made a major impression on Carlo Ancelotti during lockdown and was rewarded with a first Premier League start against Liverpool when the season resumed. Made 11 Premier League appearances last season in total and started this campaign with what Ancelotti called an “inspired” display against Salford in the Carabao Cup.  
  
**October 2021 update** Signed a new five-year contract last September and joined Preston on loan in February, where he initially impressed before his game-time reduced with a change of manager. Part of Rafael Benítez’s squad and has been a regular on the bench in the Premier League so far this season.  
  
**September 2022 update** Has blossomed into a vital part of the Everton attack, to the extent the club rejected several huge bids from Chelsea for his services this summer as Frank Lampard looks to build around the homegrown talent.

Jordan Thomas

ClubHuddersfield Town

PositionDefender

Born2 January 2001

Last month Huddersfield restructured their academy, downgrading their programme for players below under-18s because it has been a long time since the club brought a player from a younger age group through to the first team. “The climate has proven difficult for this club considering EPPP rules and the number of big clubs on our doorstep, which offers strong competition for the best local players,” explained the chairman, Dean Hoyle. But that does not mean the club has given up on youngsters – and around the time that they announced the restructuring, they signed Thomas from London’s XYZ academy. An adventurous and powerful right-back he has already made a couple of appearances for the club’s under-23s.**[Paul Doyle](https://twitter.com/Paul_Doyle)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Left Huddersfield and signed his first professional contract with Norwich in January and has already played for the club’s under-23s.  
  
**October 2019 update** Continuing his progress and made his debut in the EFL Trophy in September, playing the full 90 minutes in the 2-1 defeat by Oxford at right-back.  
  
**October 2020 update** Loaned by Norwich to Leyton Orient in September. “We’ve signed a player with huge potential,” said the Os head coach, Ross Embleton.  
  
**October 2021 update** Made only nine appearances for Orient, and only one of those was a league match, and has now moved to Barnet on a permanent deal.  
  
**September 2022 update** Thomas is halfway through a two-year deal at Barnet in the National League though has struggled for minutes so far this season.

Lukas Husek

ClubLeicester City

PositionDefender

Born25 October 2000

The first thing that strikes anyone about Husek is his size. Still only 16, the left-sided central defender is 6ft 7in. Signed from FK Jablonec, who play in the top flight in the Czech Republic, Husek has represented his country at under-15, under-16 and under-17 level and he attracted interest from Manchester City before signing for Leicester. Husek, however, had given his word to Leicester and, as well not wanting to go back on that agreement, believes he is in the right place for his development. Comfortable with both feet and eager to learn, he has quickly made a big impression on the academy staff at Leicester with his attitude as well as his ability and, not surprisingly, is quite a threat in the opposition penalty area. **[Stuart James](https://twitter.com/StuartJamesGNM)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The towering central defender continues to make good progress. He has been on the bench for the club’s under-23 team this season and has already been called up twice for the Czech Republic’s under-19s.  
  
**October 2019 update** Named in the Czech Republic’s squad for the European Under-19 Championship finals this summer but only got off the bench for a few minutes. Progressed has stalled a little at Leicester, too, with Husek unable to get into the Under-23 team at the time of writing. His one-year professional contract is up at the end of the season.  
  
**October 2020 update** Signed a permanent deal with Sparta Prague in his homeland until 2023 and is now on loan at fellow top-flight side Pardubice.  
  
**October 2021 update** Made only three top-flight appearances for Pardubice and is now on loan again, this time at second-tier FK Varnsdorf.  
  
**September 2022 update** Contracted to Sparta Prague B he will spend the 2022-23 season on loan at third-tier Loko Vltavin. Last season he started brightly at FK Varnsdorf but was mainly on the bench towards the end of the season.

Curtis Jones

ClubLiverpool

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born30 January 2001

A Liverpool-born, Liverpool-supporting midfielder with an eye for goal might feel inhibited with Steven Gerrard studying his every move from the sidelines. But not the confident teenager from Toxteth, who has continued to impress for Liverpool’s and Gerrard’s under-18s despite being only 16. Jones, whose attacking instincts and penetrating runs into the penalty area are matched by excellent footwork, made his debut for the under-18s and trained with Jürgen Klopp’s senior squad at the age of 15. He scored five goals for the under-18s last season and marked his Uefa Youth League debut last month with two goals in a 4-0 defeat of Sevilla. **[Andy Hunter](https://twitter.com/AHunterGuardian)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Already part of Jürgen Klopp’s first team squad having scored 18 goals for the under-18s and 19s last season, debuted for the under-23s in January and signed his first professional contract in February. Made a big impression on the pre-season tour of the US this summer.  
  
**October 2019 update** Made his first start for Jürgen Klopp’s team in last season’s FA Cup defeat at Wolves and signed a new long-term contract with his boyhood club in August. Featured regularly for the first team in pre-season and currently for the Under-23s. Also impressed for England Under-18s last term.  
  
**October 2020 update** Signed a new five-year contract with his boyhood club in July and enjoyed a breakthrough season with Jürgen Klopp’s champions that included a sensational winner against Everton in the FA Cup.  
  
**October 2021 update** Maintained his impressive development with 34 appearances for Jurgen Klopp’s team last season. Missed the start of this campaign with concussion and was also linked with a deadline day move to Aston Villa, although Liverpool had no intention of selling. Returned as a substitute in the Champions League group win over Milan.  
  
**September 2022 update** Featured 27 times and made valuable contributions last season as Jürgen Klopp’s team won the FA Cup and Carabao Cup, although injuries have hindered his efforts to secure a regular starting role.

Ivan Ilic

ClubManchester City

PositionDefensive midfielder

Born17 March 2001

The defensive midfielder became Red Star Belgrade’s youngest debutant in April before Manchester City signed him and his elder brother Luka in August in a deal worth £5m. Illic’s debut was only a minute long, at the end of a SuperLiga game, but after being loaned back to Red Star the expectation is the 16-year-old will be given considerably more match time this season. Illic has represented Serbia at under-14, under-15, under-16 and under-17 level. **[Jamie Jackson](https://twitter.com/JamieJackson___)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The midfielder was loaned back to Red Star Belgrade but made only one appearance – in a cup game – last season.  
  
**October 2019 update** Is out on loan again – at NAC Breda in the Dutch second tier, having spent the second half of 2018-19 at FK Zemun in his native Serbia. Made his debut for the Serbia Under-21 side in a No 10 role but was sent off for two yellow cards.  
  
**October 2020 update** Was loaned to Italian side Hellas Verona in September for the season, with an option to buy, and made his Serie A debut against Roma on 19 September. Not bad for someone who is still only 19.  
  
**October 2021 update** Made an incredible impact in Italy, playing 1,831 minutes of Serie A football in his season on loan before signing a permanent deal in August, reportedly for £7m. Also made his Serbia debut.  
  
**September 2022 update** Firmly established at Verona in Serie A where he made 32 Serie A appearances last season. Has three Serbia caps at the time of writing.

Arnau Puigmal

ClubManchester United

PositionMidfielder

Born10 January 2001

Puigmal, a Spain youth international, signed last summer after impressing for Espanyol against Manchester United in the 2017 Med Cup in April. The midfielder is rated among Europe’s best prospects and was voted Med Cup player of the tournament. Puigmal coolly netted a Panenka penalty during a shootout against England's under-16s at Leek Town’s Harrison Park in February. "He's a really dynamic, technical midfield player who likes to drive forward and carry the ball," says the United coach Kieran McKenna. **[Jamie Jackson](https://twitter.com/JamieJackson___)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Made four Under-18 appearances but was not selected for the summer tour.  
  
**October 2019 update** Has made good progress and played his first games for Spain Under-18s and Under-19s but, at United, he has been overtaken by James Garner, who is two months younger, and in Ole Gunnar Solskjær’s senior squad.  
  
**October 2020 update** The 19-year-old was in Ole Gunnar Solskjær’s squad for last November’s Europa League trip to Astana and made his senior debut in the Carabao Cup against Salford in September 2020. Will hope for more chances this season.  
  
**October 2021 update** Had another decent season at Under-23 level but decided to leave when his contract ran out in the summer. Then signed a five-year deal with Almería in the Spanish second division.  
  
**September 2022 update** The first season at Almería in Spain’s second tier ended with 33 league games and promotion, leading to a La Liga debut against Real Madrid in August.

Deese Kasinga-Madia

ClubNewcastle United

PositionWinger

Born12 December 2000

A pacey, technically gifted left-sider, comfortable on the wing or in a more central attacking midfield role, Kasinga-Madia is in the first year of a two-year scholarship on Tyneside. Already making his mark on Dave Watson’s under-18 side, he was born in DR Congo but grew up in London, where he became part of Brentford’s junior system. As befits a player who started out as a goal-hungry central striker, Kasinga possesses a decent shot. **[Louise Taylor](https://www.theguardian.com/profile/louisetaylor)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Now a second-year scholar at Newcastle United and playing for the club’s under-18s.  
  
**October 2019 update** Released by Newcastle, the Congo-born winger is without a club. Had trials with Ipswich and Norwich last spring.  
  
**October 2020 update** After assorted trials with Premier League and EFL clubs, the right winger is now back in east London, playing for Tower Hamlets FC.  
  
**October 2021 update** Appalling luck with knee injuries prefaced Kasinga-Madia this year revealed suffering depression ahead of his release by Newcastle. This summer he starred in an inspirational YouTube film “Made on the Concrete” about his comeback. Still with Tower Hamlets and hoping to return to the professional game.  
  
**September 2022 update** The gifted winger saw his top tier career de-railed by serious knee injuries. After being released by Newcastle, Kasinga-Madia struggled with depression before beginning to rebuild his career with non league Tower Hamlets and, most recently, Chesham United.

Kameron Ledwidge

ClubSouthampton

PositionDefender

Born7 April 2001

A new face at Southampton's Staplewood campus, having joined the club's academy in June of this year, the Republic of Ireland under-17 international ticks all of the boxes for the modern attacking left-back. Strong, athletic and quick across the ground, he is comfortable coming forward with the ball at this feet. Formerly of St Kevin's Boys in his native Dublin, he starred for his country in last season's Victory Shield. He can also play in central defence and his versatility is considered as a further plus point. **[David Hytner](https://twitter.com/DaveHytner)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Played at both left-back and centre-half for the club's under-18s last season. The club want to give him time to establish himself.  
  
**October 2019 update** Star has waned a little and he has not been spoken about recently in terms of a first-team breakthrough.  
  
**October 2020 update** A regular for Southampton's under-23s last season but remains down the pecking order for the first team.  
  
**October 2021 update** Gained more Premier League 2 experience before moving back to Ireland and signing for Shelbourne on a free in July.  
  
**September 2022 update** A key figure for Shelbourne in the 2022 League of Ireland Premier Division. Has signed a deal to stay with them for 2023.

Nathan Collins

ClubStoke City

PositionDefender

Born30 April 2001

The teenager’s father and uncle, David and Eamonn, captained the Republic of Ireland in youth World Cups (where David, formerly a youth-team player at Liverpool, marked Luis Figo in one match) and Nathan kept up a proud family tradition when he appeared for his country in last summer’s under-17 European championship. Born in Dublin, he played for the celebrated junior club Cherry Orchard before joining Stoke as a first-year scholar in June. Already 6ft 4in, he is a commanding centre-back who is very comfortable on the ball. **[Paul Doyle](https://twitter.com/Paul_Doyle)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Still at Stoke. Captained Republic of Ireland to quarter-finals of this year’s Under-17 European Championship before losing on a penalty shootout to eventual winners Netherlands.  
  
**October 2019 update** Still highly regarded at Stoke, who have loaned him to Accrington Stanley this season to enable him to continue his development.  
  
**October 2020 update** Linked with a move to Arsenal and Manchester United during the summer after some mature performances in defence and starred in Stoke’s Carabao Cup win over Wolves in September.  
  
**October 2021 update** After making 49 senior appearances for Stoke and becoming the club’s youngest ever captain, he was bought for £12m for Burnley in the summer.  
  
**September 2022 update** Having been a shining light within Burnley’s relegation season, Collins was cashed out for £20.5m to Wolves, a record for an Irish player. His signing led to Bruno Lage deciding he could allow Conor Coady to be loaned out to Everton. Back home in Ireland, Collins hit the headlines in the summer for a mazy, dribbling solo goal against Ukraine in the Nations League.

Mason Jones-Thomas

ClubSwansea City

PositionWinger

Born05 December 2000

A local lad who has been with Swansea since the age of eight, Jones-Thomas is a talented winger who likes dribbling and taking on full-backs. He is a boy in a hurry, having made his debut for Swansea’s under-18s at the age of 14 and been named on the bench for the under-23s while still at school. Jones-Thomas, whose father was a scholar at Swansea in the mid-90s but never quite made the grade, has also played well above his age group for Wales, including starting against Iceland for the under-19s last month. Reasonably quick with a bright football brain and willing to put in the extra hours after training, the 16-year-old is highly regarded within Swansea’s academy. **[Stuart James](https://twitter.com/StuartJamesGNM)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Progressing well as he starts his second year as a scholar, having finished last season as Swansea’s top scorer for the under-18s despite playing on the wing.  
  
**October 2019 update** Made 10 appearances for Swansea’s Under-23 side towards the end of last season – five during the run to the Premier League Cup semi-finals – and signed his first professional contract in July, tying him to his hometown club until 2020. “I feel a lot of pride and happiness but, at the same time, I realise the hard work starts now,” Jones-Thomas said.  
  
**October 2020 update** Released after making eight Premier League 2 appearances last season. Recently spent a week on trial at Portsmouth under the former Swansea manager Kenny Jackett.  
  
**October 2021 update** Joined Haverfordwest County of the Cymru Premier League on a one-year deal in July, reuniting with former Swansea academy coach Gary Richards, who is assistant manager.  
  
**September 2022 update** After a free transfer from Haverfordwest, in the Cymru Premier, Jones-Thomas is playing this season for Trefelin Boys & Girls Club in the Cymru South division.

Oliver Skipp

ClubTottenham Hotspur

PositionDefensive midfielder

Born16 September 2000

Skipp played 18 games for Tottenham’s under-18s last season and he even got on as a substitute for the under-23s in February – all this while he was still a schoolboy. This season he has stepped again up to become a regular at under-23 level and the overall impression is of a player who is mature beyond his years. A firm favourite of the club’s head of player development, John McDermott, Skipp is a tenacious defensive midfielder with composure, an eye for a pass and an excellent attitude. **[David Hytner](https://twitter.com/DaveHytner)**  
  
**October 2018 update** A regular for the under-23s last season, despite being a first-year scholar, he impressed on tour with the first-team over the summer. Christian Eriksen described him as an “all-round midfielder”, adding: “What I like about him is his courage.”  
  
**October 2019 update** Has established himself as a fixture in Mauricio Pochettino’s first-team squad, playing 12 times last season – a remarkable achievement that felt as though it drifted under the radar somewhat.  
  
**October 2020 update** Played only 73 minutes of Premier League football - including being substituted at half-time in his only start against Burnley - last season so it was no surprise that he opted to join Norwich on loan for this campaign.  
  
**October 2021 update** Helped Norwich win promotion to the Premier League and was included in the 2021 PFA team of the year for the Championship. Back at Spurs, he has regularly been trusted with a starting place in midfield by Nuno Esperíto Santo so far this season.  
  
**September 2022 update** Nuno Esperíto Santo counted on him in the early weeks of last season and so did Antonio Conte when he took over. However, a pelvic injury ruled him out from late January [to the annoyance of the Italian](https://www.theguardian.com/football/2022/mar/04/antonio-conte-calls-on-spurs-doctors-to-publicly-explain-oliver-skipp-injury).

![](https://interactive.guim.co.uk/atoms/2017/09/interactive-next-gen-footballers-2017-premiership/v/1507032273544/assets/placeholder.png)

Lewis Gordon

ClubWatford

PositionDefender

Born12 February 2001

It is a measure of the faith placed in Lewis Gordon that he trained with the first team in pre-season and was one of only two teenagers – and the youngest by three years – to play in an experienced team picked for a pre-season friendly against AFC Wimbledon in July. Coaches admire his technique in possession, his reading of the game and his defending of one-v-one situations, as well as his willingness to get forward. “It’s clear he has a real love for the game,” says Watford’s head of academy, Chris McGuane. “He takes information on board very well and he’s always desperate to improve and progress.” **[Simon Burnton](https://twitter.com/Simon_Burnton)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Gordon is still at Watford, having signed a professional contract in March. He remains part of the under-18 squad but is playing regularly for the under-23s.  
  
**October 2019 update** The left-back suffered a serious injury in January, from which he finally returned in August. Was called up by Scotland’s Under-19s in September, making one substitute appearance, and a regular in Watford’s Under-23 team.  
  
**October 2020 update** Released by Watford in the summer, Gordon had interest from several Championship sides and eventually joined Brentford, where he will initially play in the B team. He was capped by Scotland’s under-19s last November. “Athletically he’s really good. We’ve also seen his quality on the ball, and we think that he’ll be a fantastic addition,” said Neil MacFarlane, the Brentford B head coach.  
  
**October 2021 update** Remains at Brentford, who exercised an option to extend his contract by a further year at the end of last season.  
  
**September 2022 update** Now 21, Gordon made his senior first-team debut in January, when he started Brentford’s 2-1 victory over Middlesbrough in the FA Cup third round. Released by the Bees at the end of the season, he rebuffed interest from Crystal Palace to join League One Bristol Rovers, reasoning that “it was time for me to kick on and start playing professional football”, and has started the new season strongly.

Rayhaan Tulloch

ClubWest Bromwich Albion

PositionStriker

Born20 January 2001

Tulloch’s rapid progress stands out amid a richly promising generation of English talent at West Brom. He has been a regular for the under-23s in the early stages of the season and, on 19 September, started against the League One side Walsall in the Checkatrade Trophy. Tulloch opened the scoring with a sharp finish from close range; West Brom went on to lose but the bigger picture is of a forward who already looks at home in more senior company. With two goals in recent friendlies he is making a difference at the sharp end for England under-17s, too; at his current rate it is little stretch to imagine him progressing quickly through the age groups. **[Nick Ames](https://twitter.com/NickAmes82)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Tulloch signed his first professional contract in March, having impressed West Brom’s then-manager Alan Pardew enough to earn a place on the bench at Swansea three months previously. Featured for England in the Under-17 European Championship but awaits his first-team bow.  
  
**October 2019 update** Made his first-team debut for West Brom in January when coming off the bench in an FA Cup fourth-round tie with Brighton and appeared again in the replay. He featured in pre-season under new manager Slaven Bilic but is yet to step up from the Under-23s this term. Premier League clubs were reported to be monitoring him during the summer.  
  
**October 2020 update** Made his Football League debut in September after signing on loan for Doncaster, where he has reunited with former West Brom manager Darren Moore. Signed a new four-year contract in the summer after impressing Slaven Bilic off the bench in the FA Cup. Trained with first team for majority of last season and caught the eye for the under-23s, scoring nine goals.  
  
**October 2021 update** There was rotten luck for Tulloch at Doncaster, where he picked up a serious hamstring injury 13 minutes into his second appearance. That cut the loan short but things have looked up this season and he made his Championship debut for West Brom at Blackburn in August. A groin issue meant Valerian Ishmael could not call on him for the subsequent month but Tulloch is now back in training and a firm part of the manager’s plans.  
  
**September 2022 update** Tulloch made a further appearance from the bench for West Brom in February but otherwise it was a fallow season after returning from injury. He badly needs a run of first-team football and was sent on loan to League Two side Rochdale in August. So far he has been in and out of Jim Bentley’s side and will hope this is the year his potential blossoms.

Anouar El Mhassani

ClubWest Ham United

PositionMidfielder

BornApr 18, 2001

West Ham have started looking to the continent for young players and signed the 16-year-old from Ajax in the summer. El Mhassani, a left-footed attacking midfielder, was not offered a new contract by Ajax after suffering injury problems, but there are high hopes for him. “He’s a talented, technical player and he’s now got to develop other the three years to the physical, tactical sides of the game,” West Ham’s academy director, Terry Westley, says. “In terms of ability with the ball, he’ll be right up there. He’s got that bravery to really take people on.” **[Jacob Steinberg](https://twitter.com/JacobSteinberg)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The Dutch midfielder is yet to make his first team breakthrough.  
  
**October 2019 update** The midfielder is yet to push for first-team inclusion.  
  
**October 2020 update** Released when his contract expired in the summer and said: “I’m currently open to all options. A return to the Netherlands is definitely an alternative.”  
  
**October 2021 update** Joined Ajman Club in the UAE but only played 23 minutes during the 2020-21 season, according to Transfermarkt, and is without a club at the time of writing.  
  
**September 2022 update** Appears to be still without a club.
