The 2023-2024 Premier League registration rules stipulate that each club must submit a squad of up to 25 players. Key points include:

1.	Squad Size & Composition
The maximum squad size is 25 players. This must include a minimum of eight ‘Home Grown’ players.

2.	Home Grown Players
A ‘Home Grown’ player is defined as one who, irrespective of nationality or age, has been registered with any club affiliated to the Football Association (FA) or the Football Association of Wales (FAW) for a period, continuous or not, of three entire seasons or 36 months prior to their 21st birthday (or the end of the season during which they turn 21).

3.	Under-21 Players
Players who are under 21 (born on or after January 1, 2002, for the 2023-2024 season) are eligible over and above the limit of 25 players per squad. For these players, registration with the Premier League is not required.	

4.	Mid-Season Changes
Changes to the squad list can only be made during the transfer windows or if special circumstances arise, subject to Premier League Board approval.

5.	Non-Home Grown Players
There’s no specific limit on the number of non-home grown players in the squad, but clubs must adhere to the minimum number of home-grown players, effectively limiting the number of non-home grown players to 17.

These rules aim to encourage the development of local talent while maintaining the competitive balance and quality of the Premier League.
