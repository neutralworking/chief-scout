The Governing Body Endorsement (GBE) system, introduced post-Brexit for football transfers in the UK, is a points-based system used to determine whether overseas players are eligible to work in the UK. Here's more detail on how it operates:

1. **Points Allocation**: Points are awarded based on:
   - **International Appearances**: Players earn points for playing a set percentage of senior international matches for countries ranked in the FIFA rankings. The higher the country's ranking, the fewer appearances are needed.
   - **Club Appearances**: Points are given based on the proportion of domestic league and continental competition matches a player participates in for their club. The quality of the player's current league and their club's performance in continental competitions factor into this assessment.
   - **Final League Position**: The final league position of a player's previous club can earn them points.
   - **Continental Competition Progress**: Players can earn points if their club has progressed to the group stages or further in competitions like the UEFA Champions League or Europa League.

2. **Threshold for Approval**: To automatically qualify for a GBE, a player typically needs to accumulate a specific number of points. If they don't meet this threshold, they may still be considered for a GBE, but this would go to an Exceptions Panel.

3. **Youth Players and Talented Prospects**: Special provisions are made for young players and high-potential talents who may not meet the standard criteria. These cases are also reviewed by the Exceptions Panel.

4. **Review Process**: If a player does not meet the automatic criteria, their application is reviewed on a case-by-case basis by the Exceptions Panel. They consider the player's experience and value to assess whether they should be granted a GBE despite not meeting the points threshold.

5. **Impact on Transfers**: This system has made the recruitment of players from EU countries more complex, aligning it more closely with the process already in place for non-EU players. Clubs must be more strategic in their international signings, considering these regulations.

The GBE system reflects an attempt to balance the need for clubs to access international talent with the desire to foster domestic player development in the wake of Brexit.
