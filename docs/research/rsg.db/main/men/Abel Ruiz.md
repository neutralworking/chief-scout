All-time top scorer in the European under-17 championship with 16, eight of them at the finals, Abel Ruiz was the captain of the Spain side who won the competition this summer – scoring against France, Italy (2) and Turkey. He had been in the team that finished runners-up the previous season, getting goals against Germany, Italy, Holland and Serbia. An out-and-out goalscorer, finishing from inside the area, he stands out for his movement, work and positioning. A fan of Zlatan Ibrahimovic, he was signed by Barcelona from Valencia at the age of 12 – much to the chagrin of the club from Mestalla. Scored on his infantil debut and continued to progress. Attracted interest from the Premier League but signed a three-year deal in 2016 and has played, and scored, for Barcelona B in Spain’s second division this season. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**
 
**October 2018 update** In 2017 Ruiz top-scored for Spain in the Under-17 European Championship and Under-17 World Cup. Earlier this year, shortly after he made his first-team debut as a substitute in the Supercopa de Catalunya – scoring in the penalty shootout – Ruiz signed a new three-year contract, complete with €100m release clause. He is a regular in the Barcelona B team, relegated last season to Spain’s Segunda Division B.
 
**October 2019 update** Made his senior debut against Getafe in May, after the league title had been secured, coming on with 20 minutes remaining. Went on the US tour with the first team and won the Under-19 Euros with Spain. He wanted to go out on loan but Barcelona turned down offers, including a two-year stint at Sporting.
 
**October 2020 update** In January Ruiz moved to Sporting Braga on loan, with a mandatory purchase clause set at €8m (Barcelona can bring him back for €40m). Despite not starting a game – there were six substitute appearances, the longest of 25 minutes, and one goal – the move became permanent in the summer, making him the Portuguese club’s record signing. Carlos Carvalhal, who was appointed Braga’s coach in July, described him as “a slightly introverted boy” who had struggled during the peak of the pandemic but is evolving nicely. “Now he is adapting to his team and his teammates,” he said. “Abel Ruiz in his first week and Abel Ruiz five weeks later are totally different players.”
 
**October 2021 update** Ruiz’s career is going from strength to strength since joining Braga, where he has been a regular in Carvalhal’s starting lineup and has even caught the eye of Spain’s national team manager Luis Enrique. Ruiz made his La Roja debut in a friendly against Lithuania before the Euros, when the whole first squad was quarantined due to a Covid-19 outbreak in the squad. But instead of returning to the U21s when the big guns returned, he was again called up by Enrique for the September qualifiers and played his first full 90 minutes in a 4-0 victory over Georgia.
 
**September 2022 update** Ruíz’s second season at SC Braga did not go as well as his first in Portugal. He started just 14 league games, while coming off the bench another 14 times in 2021-22. “He is a good striker, with a lot of mobility and I love working with him,” former manager Carlos Carvalhal commented. “He has the ability to play at the highest level.” This season Ruíz started with a bang scoring two goals in three games as a sub which sparked interest from other La Liga teams, but Braga would not budge on their €40m minimum-release clause, while Ruíz himself has since struggled to find a regular rhythm. Still an important player for the Spain Under-21s, the striker has not returned to Luis Enrique’s national squad and is unlikely to make the cut for Qatar.
