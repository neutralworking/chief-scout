If you happen to be in Kraków during winter time there’s big chance you will order “Grzaniec galicyjski” (hot mulled wine) to warm yourself up. Adam Buksa, Aleksander’s father, is behind this drink. Footballers often come from poor backgrounds but not in this case. As a child Buksa and his brothers had everything they could wish for. Their old man built a football field with artificial turf in the basement. He let Aleksander paint the walls himself (he used Wisła club colours). Aleksander Buksa is an educated young man who speaks fluently and confidently, and attacks opposition goals likewise. When he scored his first senior goal against Jagiellonia Białystok in August 2019 he was 16 years and 220 days years old, and became the youngest ever scorer in Wisła’s 114-year history. His playing style is similar to his older brother Adam, who currently plays for New England Revolution. Both Buksa brothers are tall, technically proficient, left-footed, blonde strikers. Barcelona are said to have scouted him but Buksa has committed to Wisła until 2023. **[Maciej Slominski](https://twitter.com/maciejslominski?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Did not sign a contract extension with Wisla, which led open hostility from White Star fans, who had adored him before. Buksa then moved to Italian Serie A side Genoa in the summer and has played 45 minutes of Serie A football at the time of writing.  
  
**September 2022 update** The remainder of the 2021-22 season was a topsy-turvy experience for the young Pole. He ended making four Serie A appearances, play for the Primavera team but also suffered injuries. He will spend the 2022-23 season on loan at OH Leuven in Belgium.  
  
**October 2023 update** Spent the 2022-23 season with the reserve teams of Leuven and Standard Liège, but got a much more promising loan deal in the summer by joining WSG Tirol. Is now a starter in the Austrian top division and has the opportunity to really prove himself.
