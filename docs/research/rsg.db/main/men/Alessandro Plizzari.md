Not content with having the most exciting teenage talent on the peninsula as their first-choice goalkeeper, Milan appear to have already discovered Gianluigi Donnarumma’s heir as well. Separated in age by only 11 months, the pair are close friends from their time together in the club’s academy and continue to stay in touch by phone now that Plizzari is on loan with Ternana in Serie B. Although three-and-a-half inches shorter than Donnarumma, the younger man is light enough on his feet, and quick enough with his reflexes, to dominate between the sticks. Manchester City were reported to have had a bid turned down in January. **[Nicky Bandini](https://twitter.com/nickybandini?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Played 19 times for Ternana in Serie B, gaining valuable experience, but lost his place midway through the season after fracturing a finger. Now fourth-choice at Milan as well as a member of the Italy Under-20 squad.
 
**October 2019 update** Made seven Serie A matchday squads without getting any playing time and has been loaned to Livorno in Serie B, where he has started most games on the bench.
 
**October 2020 update** Became first-choice at Livorno and played 21 league games in 2019-20. He also made his debut for the Italian Under-21 side. This summer he joined another Serie B side, Reggina, on loan.
 
**October 2021 update** Spent most of the season on the bench at Reggina, making only 10 Serie B appearances. Back at Milan as third-choice goalkeeper but has just had knee surgery.
 
**September 2022 update** The knee injury kept him out until January and, after three appearances for Lecce in Serie B while on loan, he has join Pescara in Serie C, where he has started the season as the club’s No 1.
