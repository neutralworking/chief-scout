Besio seemingly came from nowhere when the St Gallen coach, Peter Zeidler, decided to give him a chance on the last matchday of the 2020-21 season in May. The Swiss FA were not aware of the striker and he never played for youth national teams at any level until then, but his top-division debut proved to be sensational. The 17-year-old scored a very well taken goal in the 2-1 win at Servette, thus becoming the first player born in 2004 to score in the Swiss top division, and never looked back. Physically strong, Besio is an imposing figure in the penalty area, but is also very quick and agile, and possesses a delicate touch. He proved himself with the St Gallen first team in pre-season, and is now a starter, contributing two assists in the first eight matches. His mother used to play handball for the Swiss national team and Alessio looks destined to follow suit in football. **[Michael Yokhin](https://twitter.com/Yokhin)**  
  
  
**September 2022 update** Has made 32 Swiss Super League appearances at the time of writing, although has only managed two goals so far. Is clearly at the right club for the time being with his manager, Peter Zeidler, saying: “Alessio is our biggest talent – and we give young players a chance here.”  
  
**October 2023 update** Had the 2022-23 season completely ruined by injuries and left St Gallen in the summer upon the end of his contract. He had knee surgery in August before signing for Freiburg, where he will start with the club's reserve team in the German third division when back to full fitness.
