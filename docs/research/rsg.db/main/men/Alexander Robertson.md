A two-footed box-to-box midfielder with an eye for goal - in the mould of his idol Steven Gerrard - Alex Robertson will soon graduate from Manchester City’s high-yielding academy. Injuries have tempered his transition from age-group to men’s football but first-team experience is not far away, most likely on loan from City, where he is contracted until 2023. Robertson’s father and grandfather both represented Australia, and he learned the game in the beachside suburbs of Sydney, but he is no certainty to play for the Socceroos. Robertson has already found the back of the net for England Under-17s while family ties make Scotland and Peru further options. **[Jonathan Howcroft](https://twitter.com/jphowcroft)**

**October 2021 update** Scored for Manchester City’s under-23 side in February against Leicester and again against Liverpool in April, before being sent on a season-long loan to Ross County. Played 45 minutes for England’s under-18s in a friendly with Wales.  
  
**September 2022 update** Had a frustrating time in Dingwall; making just one start and five substitute appearances before his deal was cut short and he has since returned to Manchester City’s youth setup.  
  
**October 2023 update** Despite interest from England, Scotland and Peru, Robertson has pledged himself to Australia and made his first appearance for the senior national team off the bench against Ecuador in March – becoming a third-generation Socceroo in the process. Now on loan to Portsmouth, the striker has impressed in League One and even received a standing ovation from fans after his first start.
