At just 16, Alphonso Davies has achieved some remarkable things at senior level. In July he became the youngest player to score at the Gold Cup at 16 years, eight months and five days old. The hard-working, delightfully technical and scarily fast Canadian was also named the Best Young Player at the tournament, where he picked up the Golden Boot for his three goals. Capable of playing across the attacking line, Davies excels at moving into pockets of space between the full-back and centre-back, where his direct running towards goal or his willingness to stretch the play wide can be factors in developing dangerous attacks. **[Stephen Ganavas](https://twitter.com/scoutedftbl?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2018 update** After a stellar year for club and country, Davies earned a move to Bayern Munich. The reported fee of $13m plus bonuses is the record paid for a Canadian footballer and for an MLS player.  
  
**October 2019 update** Since arriving at Bayern in January he has made a decent impact from the bench, scoring his first Bundesliga goal against Mainz in March and adding another against the same opponents five months later. Occasionally tried at left-back, he is yet to start a game but that appears a matter of time.  
  
**October 2020 update** It's remarkable to think that this time last year the teenager had yet to make his full debut for Bayern and is now a European champion, having flourished at left-back under coach Hansi Flick. Who knows what might happen next? "Football comes first, but I want to be an actor eventually," Davies admitted to Gary Lineker in April in an article published **[by the Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/football/2020/apr/24/bayern-munich-alphonso-davies-meets-gary-lineker-i-want-to-be-an-actor). “I like entertaining people and TikTok gives me the platform to do so.”  
  
**October 2021 update** Now has three Bundesliga titles and has played more than 60 Bundesliga games and 15 Champions League games. Continues to improve.  
  
**September 2022 update** His 2021-22 season was interrupted because a mild inflammatory heart condition was discovered after he had covid. He missed three months but remains a key player for Bayern, with whom he won another league title.**
