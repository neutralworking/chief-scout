Amadou Onana's footballing background is marked by his development across various European clubs. Born in Dakar, Senegal, Onana moved to Brussels, Belgium, at a young age. He played his youth football with Anderlecht, RWS Bruxelles, and Zulte Waregem. His professional career began with German club Hoffenheim in July 2018. After three years there, Onana signed for Hamburger SV ahead of the 2020/21 season, where he made a significant impact in Bundesliga 2, making 26 appearances.

Onana's performances at Hamburg earned him a move to Ligue 1 club Lille in August 2021. He quickly became a regular for Lille, making 42 appearances across all competitions, including eight in the Champions League. His form at Lille led to his call-up to the Belgium senior squad in May 2022, earning his first cap against the Netherlands in June 2022.

Everton signed Onana in August 2022 for a reported £33 million including add-ons. His move to Everton was seen as part of the club's strategy to strengthen their midfield, and he has since been an active player for the team, making his first Premier League goal in January 2023.
