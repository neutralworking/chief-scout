Whereas many of the US's young talents – Christian Pulisic is a notable example – have chosen to move abroad to develop their careers, Carleton is a true homegrown talent. Born and raised in Georgia, he made his debut for his local MLS team Atlanta United in front of 45,000 fans this season at the age of 16. Carleton models his attacking, positive game on his hero Cristiano Ronaldo, and scored 20 goals in 32 games for the USA under-17s. He is also keen to show that the US can produce world-class players without help from Europe: "[I want] to be able to show that you don't necessarily have to go to Europe at 15, 16, 17 years old to be a top-of-the-line player," he told Bleacher Report this year. **[Tom Lutz](https://twitter.com/tom_lutz?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Unlike many young US stars, Carleton has stayed in MLS where he is under the care of the former Barcelona coach Tata Martino at Atlanta United. Has had some first-team action but, at 18, is seen as an ongoing project.
 
**October 2019 update** Won the 2018 MLS title with Atlanta United but has clashed this season with manager Frank de Boer, who has questioned his work ethic and professionalism. Carleton forgot his passport for a trip to Toronto FC in June, and he was dropped to the reserves. Has since returned to the first team, and De Boer has noted an improvement in the player's attitude.
 
**October 2020 update** Was loaned out to Indy Eleven in the USL Championship, the second tier, until the end of the year. "God’s work has perfect timing and my job is to make sure that whenever the opportunity does come that I’ll be ready," he told [the Athletic](https://theathletic.com/2102522/2020/09/29/andrew-carleton-indy-eleven-atlanta-united-usl-mls/). “I’m really just waiting for that opportunity to really show what I can do and show everybody that I’m still the player that I was when I was 16, 17,18 years old.”
 
**October 2021 update** His contract with Atlanta cancelled, he surprisingly joined ADR Jicaral in Costa Rica in September.
 
**September 2022 update** After a short spell in Costa Rica, the winger is back in the US with San Diego Loyal of the USL Championship
