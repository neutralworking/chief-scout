The captain of the Serbia under-17 side is a wonderfully crafty new-age attacking midfielder. You won’t see him dwelling on the ball for too long or taking on two or three opponents like the iconic Balkan No 10’s of yesteryear – instead, he likes to keep it fast and simple, providing first-touch key passes or well-timed, well-placed shots. While some see him as ‘the next Adem Ljajic’, others notice a bit of Chelsea’s Eden Hazard in his moves. He is with Partizan’s under-19 team but it shouldn’t take too long before he makes a transition to senior football. **[Aleksandar Holiga](https://twitter.com/AlexHoliga)**
 
**October 2018 update** Made his debut for Partizan in February and has since played more than 20 games in all competitions for the club.
 
**October 2019 update** Played on and off for Partizan before the change of coach in March; the new man, the former Aston Villa striker Savo Milosevic, made clear he did not count on the youngster. Signed by Turkish Süper Lig side Sivasspor this summer but as of mid-September he had not made an appearance.
 
**October 2020 update** Little more than a fringe player at Sivasspor for now.
 
**October 2021 update** Made his debut for Serbia Under-21s in March, but has since ended up at Aluminij, a small club in Slovenia’s top flight.
 
**September 2022 update** A promising season in Slovenia was cut short because of a serious injury. He is now back at Sivasspor, who seem uncertain about the best way forward with him.
