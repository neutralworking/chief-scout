
ClubLille

PositionWinger

Born8 December 2004

One of the youngest players on the list – he won’t turn 17 until December – Bouanani impressed for France’s under-18 side this year with a goal and two assists in a tournament that also featured Spain, Portugal and Switzerland. Usually deployed as a second striker, he joined Lille at the age of 11 and along with fellow youth internationals Nathan Buayi-Kiala and Kaïlé Auvray – who has also represented the United States – has regularly represented Lille’s under-19s despite his tender years. Bouanani exploded on to the scene in 2019 when he scored four times and provided five assists in his first two matches for Lille’s under-17s and will hope to be given an opportunity to continue his progress this season. **[Ed Aarons](https://twitter.com/ed_aarons)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Signed his first professional deal with Nice in July amid interest from Lyon and Bordeaux. Highly-rated and still a regular for France’s youth sides.  
  
**October 2023 update** Caused controversy when he scored for France’s Under-18s against Algeria and then said afterwards: “Je suis Algérien" ("I am Algerian"). He has since been called up by two-time African champions and made his senior debut in March having played regularly for Nice in Ligue 1 in the past 12 months.
