After making his senior Cameroon debut as a substitute in a 2-2 friendly draw with Mexico in June this year, the Chelsea academy product is finally getting first-team action at Reading.

He joined the League One outfit on a three-year deal in the summer, and turned 21 in November. His mother is English but his father is from Cameroon, and Elliott attaches the surname Njongoue when on national team duty for the Indomitable Lions.

He may be slight in stature, but the midfielder has already made a big impression on coach Rigobert Song, who gave him more time as a substitute in friendlies against Russia and Senegal, where he was brimming with confidence and did not look out of place in the rigours of the international game.
