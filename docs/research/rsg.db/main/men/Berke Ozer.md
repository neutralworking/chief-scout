To describe Ozer as a hot prospect would be underplaying where he is at because he has already emerged as a star player for Altinordu’s first team. He enjoyed a meteoric rise from playing youth football to breaking into the first team last season and getting called up to the senior national team against Macedonia in June. The 6ft 3in goalkeeper has two league starts this season and is preparing for the under-17 World Cup in India. With several major European clubs, including Manchester City, sniffing around, Ozer could well end up joining Roma’s Cengiz Under and the Freiburg defender Caglar Soyuncu as the latest talent from the Izmir side to move to one of Europe’s top five leagues. **[Emre Sarigul](https://twitter.com/Turkish_Futbol1?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Got a high-profile move to Fenerbahce after breaking through at Altinordu. Berke is the latest player to emerge from the highly rated Altinordu academy, alongside the Roma winger Cengiz Under and the Leicester City defender Caglar Soyuncu.
 
**October 2019 update** Joined Fenerbahçe in a high-profile move from Altinordu last summer but did not get many chances. The 19-year-old has now been sent on loan to the Belgian side Westerlo - as first-choice keeper - and kept two clean sheets in his first four appearances.
 
**October 2020 update** Last season’s loan spell at Westerlo got off to a promising start but was cut short due to a season-ending injury. Ozer is back with Westerlo in the Proximus League and has already kept three clean sheets in his first three games.
 
**October 2021 update** The young keeper made 23 appearances in the Proximus League in 2020-21, keeping eight clean sheets and gaining valuable first team experience. His path to the No 1 position at Fenerbahce is currently blocked by Altay Bayindir and with just 12 months left on his contract Ozer has a decision to make on where he sees his future. His dream is to play in England.
 
**September 2022 update** Made 19 appearances for the Yellow Canaries last season as the No1 Altay Bayindir had some injury problems, but then joined Portuguese side Portimonense in the summer as a replacement for Samuel Portugal, who moved to Porto. “Samuel is a great example for me to follow, it shows the progress goalkeepers can make at this club,” Ozer said.
