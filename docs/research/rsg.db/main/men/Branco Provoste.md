A speedy attacking midfielder who specialises in assists. Provoste made his professional debut for Colo Colo under Pablo Guade’s guidance as a 16-year-old and his style can be compared to Isco or James Rodríguez as he is very comfortable on the ball and prefers playing behind the strikers. Provoste has always been ahead of his age-group: in 2015 he played at the under-17 South American championship at the age of 14 and was part of the under-17 team that qualified for the 2017 World Cup. In 2015 he was invited by Manuel Pellegrini for 10 days to the Manchester City academy where he played two matches for the under-16s. It seems it is only a question of time before he moves to Europe. **[Jaime F. Macias](https://twitter.com/Jaimefmacias?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Last year Provoste was a member of Chile’s squad at the Under-17 World Cup, where they finished bottom of England’s Group F, and shortly afterwards made his first-team debut in the Chilean Superclasico, against Universidad de Chile. He also took part in a friendly between Colo Colo’s first team and their women’s team, which pitted him against his girlfriend, Fernanda Shlomit.  
  
**October 2019 update** The Colo-Colo manager, Marcelo Espina, says that by 2025 60% of the club’s squad should be homegrown, with Provoste hoping to be among the players to benefit from this commitment. For now he is a regular member of matchday squads but rarely makes it off the bench.  
  
**October 2020 update** The Colo Colo manager, Marcelo Espina, says that by 2025 60% of the club’s squad should be homegrown, with Provoste hoping to be among the players to benefit from this commitment. For now he is a regular member of matchday squads, but only rarely makes it off the bench.  
  
**October 2021 update**Joined Ñublense at the start of 2021 after never fully breaking through at Colo Colo. “They offered me a better contract,” he explained. “But I wanted to play more, so I didn’t renew.” Partly due to injury, his stay at his new club hasn’t yet been a blazing success as he fights his way back into the first team.  
  
**September 2022 update** His move to Ñublense has not really worked out as both parties would have liked. After enjoying a decent spell at the start of 2022, where he came on in almost every game and even got a few starts, his performances have since faded and he is now mostly a bench player at the title-chasing Chilean team.
