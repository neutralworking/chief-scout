Bruno Iglesias is different – a No 10 with a soft touch, vision and a wide range of passing, short and long. Able to play right across the midfield, he provides continuity and assists, strikes the ball cleanly, is calm on the ball, dangerous around the edge of the area, and takes free-kicks and penalties. Born in Salamanca, Iglesias began with local club Santa Marta de Tormes at the age of six. Having won regional championships they reached national finals when Iglesias was 12, after which he was approached by a handful of clubs, including Villarreal, Atlético Madrid and Real Madrid. He chose the latter, moving into the club’s residence despite still being young, and is now in his sixth season there. A Spain international at under-16 and under-15 level, Iglesias has been promoted from the club’s Juvenil C directly to the Juvenil A (under-19) squad for this season. Iglesias has been mentored and is represented by the former Madrid youth team product and Real Oviedo, Granada and Leganés player Joyce Moreno – and also by Juan Mata’s father – and was the youngest player to join Common Goal, pledging 1% of his income to the movement, when he signed up in April at the age of 16. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Two goals and an assist while captaining the side during Real Madrid’s 4-1 rout of Sheriff Tirsapol in the Uefa Youth League in September helped fuel more speculation that Iglesias is creeping his way into Carlo Ancelotti’s first-team plans. “He’s still got to improve on the mental side of his game, but he’s got so much quality, he’s a bit like Kaká,” former Los Merengues youth coach Álvaro Benito said. “For me he’s the best player Real have in their youth setup.” No wonder Real pinned him down until 2025 with a contract extension in January.  
  
**September 2022 update**Continued to captain Real Madrid in the UEFA Youth League last season, but couldn't prevent a derby defeat against Atlético in the last-16 of the competition. He has trained a couple of times with the first team and this summer Raúl promoted Iglesias to Real Madrid Castilla, where he has already featured in three games.  
  
**October 2023 update** Took up the offer to play for third division side RS International, Real’s C-team, in January after dropping out of favour with the reserve side. With five goals in 18 games, Iglesias returned to Madrid with more minutes under his belt but, for a player once considered the jewel in Real’s academy crown, his future remained unclear. Another loan move this season to Celta Viga’s B-team, Fortuna, who hold an option to buy, suggests his career almost certainly lies beyond the club he joined aged 12.
