Hudson-Odoi caught the eye as a schoolboy last season when he was outstanding in Chelsea’s under-18s, scoring eight goals in 25 appearances and netting in the fifth round, semi-final and final of the FA Youth Cup, before starring for England at the European under-17 championship. He excelled in partnership with Jadon Sancho, who joined Borussia Dortmund from Manchester City in August, and scored his third goal of the tournament in the final, when England were beaten on penalties by Spain. At 16 he is already ensconced in Chelsea’s under-23s set-up and, with Juventus and Benfica having scouted him in the hope of luring him away, is committed to a three-year deal at Stamford Bridge. One to watch at the under-17s World Cup in India in October, Hudson-Odoi is likely to gain senior experience – most likely on loan – in the next 18 months. **[Dominic Fifield](https://twitter.com/domfifield)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The winger was a key member of the England Under-17s who won the World Cup in India last October, and a regular scorer for Chelsea in the EFL Trophy last season before breaking into Antonio Conte’s first team with Premier League and FA Cup appearances. He was the club’s outstanding performer in pre-season under Maurizio Sarri, who has resisted attempts by rivals to take the 17-year-old on loan so Hudson-Odoi could be properly integrated, and utilised, within the first-team group.  
  
**October 2019 update** The 18-year-old was heavily courted by Bayern Munich last season as he grew frustrated at life on the periphery of Maurizio Sarri’s side, and was so inclined to follow the path trodden by Jadon Sancho to the Bundesliga that he handed in a transfer request in January. Chelsea resisted and the winger was eventually granted his chance, duly excelling to the extent that he earned two caps for England in March. A ruptured achilles ended his campaign prematurely, but has now signed a new long-term contract at Stamford Bridge.  
  
**October 2020 update** A year of frustration after the bonus of recovering from his injury quickly, with Frank Lampard only selecting him to start seven Premier League matches. Could be allowed to leave on loan this season.  
  
**October 2021 update** Has stayed at Chelsea and played a fair amount at the start of the season but found himself increasingly marginalised under Thomas Tuchel. He was included in matchday squads and still is but was kept on the bench for Chelsea’s final five Champions League games without playing a minute as they won it.  
  
**September 2022 update** Another frustrating season for Hudson-Odoi, who had a run in the Chelsea team in October and November but did not get many chances after an achilles injury that kept him out for a few months in the spring. Now hoping to find a new lease of life in the Bundesliga, having joined Bayer Leverkusen on loan.
