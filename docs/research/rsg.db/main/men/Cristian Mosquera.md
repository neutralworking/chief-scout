The son of Colombian parents, Mosquera is a Spain international at youth level and who signed his first professional contract with Valencia in the summer, taking him to 2023. Regularly trains with the first team while leading the defence playing for Valencia Mestalla, the club’s B team in Spain’s third tier. Has already been included in the first team squad twice – against Sevilla and Athletic – although he is yet to make his debut. Physically imposing, accomplished in bringing the ball out, quick, and with excellent anticipation. “He’s characterised by a physical condition and good technical qualities, powerful in the air, and mature for his age,” says Miguel-Ángel Angulo, the former Valencia player who coaches the B team. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Made his debut for Valencia in January 2022 after playing the first half of a 1-1 draw against Sevilla, the Spanish youth international is now a regular in the Valencia squad. Featuring six times last season, he is expected to get much more playing time this year under Gennaro Gattuso, who used him often in pre-season. “Mosquera, a player born in 2004, has got an incredibly professional mentality,” Gattuso insisted after the 1-0 victory over Girona in August, in which Mosquera played almost the whole second half. A steady starter for Spain Under-19s, Mosquera captained the side against Israel at the start of September.  
  
**October 2023 update** Was called up to the Colombia Under-20 squad in March, but later dropped out of the reckoning and pleaded for more time to consider his options after having already represented Spain, the nation of his birth. Was officially promoted to the Valencia first-team squad in August, but is yet to firmly establish himself in manager Rubén Baraja's plans.
