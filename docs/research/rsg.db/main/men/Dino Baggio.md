CM					ITA	B	0.0	Baggio was a well-rounded, hard-working, tenacious, and tactically versatile player, who was capable of playing in various midfield positions, and he was deployed as a central midfielder and as a box-to-box midfielder throughout his career, although his primary position was in a defensive midfield role, where he excelled at breaking down the opposition's attacking plays;[3] throughout his career, he was deployed in every position on the pitch.[18] An aggressive defensive midfielder, he was gifted with strength, tackling ability, pace, positional sense, and stamina, as well as reliable technique, and good vision and distribution, he was also able to contribute offensively due to his ability to make attacking runs, and to score goals due to his proficiency in the air, with his head, [22] as well as his powerful, accurate shots and volleys from distance.		Throughout his club career, he won the UEFA Cup three times, twice with Parma and once with Juventus.[1][2] He obtained 60 caps at international level for Italy, and was part of the team that reached the final of the 1994 FIFA World Cup; he later also represented Italy at UEFA Euro 1996, and at the 1998 FIFA World Cup.
