“Szoboszlai has a great future ahead of him,” said the Hungary coach, Bernd Storck, after calling the 16-year-old up to the senior squad in June. A tall, languid, classy talent who is adept behind the striker or as a deep-lying midfielder, the Szekesfehervar-born player has enjoyed a wonderful year since turning 16. In March he dragged Hungary’s under-17s to their first European championship qualification since 2006 by scoring two stoppage-time winners before making his senior club debut for RB Salzburg’s feeder side FC Liefering in the Austrian second tier in July. He has started every game for them this season and in September he made his debut for Hungary’s under-21s in a 2-1 win over Germany in Paderborn. A series of false dawns has made Hungarian fans pessimistic over any young talent who looks special but it is hard for them not to feel at least a degree of excitement about Szoboszlai. The former MTK player, who was ineligible for RB Salzburg’s victorious Uefa Youth League campaign last season, has almost Toni Kroos-like qualities – brilliant ball-striking ability with both feet, a great range of passing, beautiful balance and a really high footballing IQ. **[Tomasz Mortimer](https://twitter.com/TMortimerFtbl)**
 
**October 2018 update** 2017-18 was a good season. He played for only half an hour for RB Salzburg’s first team but featured regularly for the feeder club Liefering in the second tier, scoring 12 goals and making eight assists in 35 games. Szoboszlai has been tipped to feature semi-regularly for RBS this season after being handed the No 14 shirt vacated by Valon Berisha and scored his first goal in his first appearance, in the cup against Schwaz in September 2018.
 
**October 2019 update** A tremendous year for the youngster as he has made an impact for club and country. He is already crucial for Hungary, scoring a trademark free-kick against Slovakia and performing solidly against Croatia and Wales, while at RB Salzburg, though he isn’t the first name on the teamsheet, he is starting to make crucial contributions and has six goals and nine assists in 28 games at the time of writing.
 
**October 2020 update** Another year of steady progression saw him score nine league goals from only 18 starts and help Salzburg qualify for the Champions League again by scoring in both legs of the playoff win over Maccabi Tel Aviv.
 
**October 2021 update** A bittersweet year for the Hungarian who completed a dream move to RB Leipzig in January but was then ruled out for the rest of the season, missing the Euros. Now back to full fitness he has started the season well at RB Leipzig, who have high hopes for him.
 
**September 2022 update** Six goals and eight assists in 31 Bundesliga games was an excellent return for Szoboszlai in his first full season, in which RB Leipzig secured the fourth and final Champions League spot. Has started this season with a red card and a stunning match-winning goal against Borussia Dortmund.
