The Indonesian has just finished the 2017 Southeast Asian under-18 championships as top scorer, with his eight goals getting the football-loving nation quite excited. His performances have earned him the nickname of ‘Egy Messi’ and his dribbling, speed and composure on the ball are truly impressive. The No10 was named as the breakthrough player at July’s Toulon tournament. He also performed well for Indonesia’s under-19s against Espanyol’s B team in a July friendly and was called “outstanding” by the Spanish team’s coach David Gallego, who told him that if he can add a little more defensive work to his game then he can go very far indeed. **[John Duerden](https://twitter.com/JohnnyDuerden?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Now a full international, the striker has moved to Lechia Gdansk in Poland.
 
**October 2019 update** Is edging his way torwards the first-team picture at Lechia and had, by late September, been included in most matchday squads in the new season and played 45 minutes against Wisla Krakow in July.
 
**October 2020 update** Still with Lechia Gdansk in Poland’s top tier. Did not get much of a look-in when it came to first-team chances in 2019-20, playing a total of 45 minutes, but his chances appear much improved this season.
 
**October 2021 update** After his contract ran out in Poland, Egy, who has become more of a feature in the Indonesian national team, signed for Slovakian club Senicav in August.
 
**September 2022 update** One of the very few Southeast Asian players in Europe and recently signed for Slovak Super League team FC Vion Zlate Moravce.
