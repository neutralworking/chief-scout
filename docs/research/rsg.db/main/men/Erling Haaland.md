The striker was born in Leeds, just before his father, Alf Inge Haaland, moved to Manchester City – and he fervently supports Leeds United. “My dream is to win the Premier League with them,” he says, knowing that is virtually impossible. The other aspiration is more likely to come true. Erling wants to surpass the 34 international caps of his dad, whose career was cut short by a knee injury. Physically strong, technically skilled and very tough mentally, Haaland was an integral part of the first-team squad at Bryne during the 2016 season, starting four games as a 15-year-old. When the team were relegated he signed for Ole Gunnar Solskjaer’s Molde, preferring them to Hoffenheim reserves. Under the former Manchester United star’s guidance, he has scored three goals in the past two months – all of them winners. The latest effort at Viking was followed by controversy, as Haaland celebrated wildly and taunted the home fans. **[Michael Yokhin](https://twitter.com/Yokhin)**
 
**October 2018 update** Made extraordinary progress and justified hopes he could develop into one of the top players of his generation. Scored nine goals for Molde in Eliteserien and four more in the Europa League qualifiers as well as becoming the undisputed leader of the team. Turned down an approach from Manchester United and signed a five-year deal at RB Salzburg, who paid €7m for him. Immediately loaned back to Molde and will move to Austria in January.
 
**October 2019 update** Scored nine goals in a single game at the Under-20 World Cup for Norway against Honduras and finished as the tournament’s top scorer even though his team were eliminated at the group stage. Made an astonishing start to the Austrian Bundesliga with Salzburg, scoring eight goals in the first six matches – and is expected to be their star man in the Champions League. Made his debut for the senior national team in September. A world-class star in the making.
 
**October 2020 update** Another incredible year in the young Norwegian’s life. He finished second (!) in the Champions League goalscoring charts behind Robert Lewandowski on 10 goals, eight of them coming for Red Bull Salzburg and another two for Borussia Dortmund, the club he joined for €20m in January. Became the first Bundesliga player ever to score five goals in his first two games – he has 19 goals in 22 games at the time of writing.
 
**October 2021 update** The numbers keep going up: at the time of writing he has an incredible 68 goals in 67 games for Dortmund and 12 in 15 for Norway. There is still room for improvement though, says his new manager Marco Rose: “He can still be a bit more resilient and he is working on his heading but we saw how good that can be against Leverkusen.”
 
**September 2022 update** Joined Manchester City for £51.5m and has started like a house on fire, with 10 goals in six Premier League games and three goals in two Champions League fixtures. Left Dortmund having scored a staggering 86 goals in 89 games for the Bundesliga club.
