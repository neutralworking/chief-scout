Internazionale signed an agreement with Boca Juniors for the striker in September, with the Italian side paying an initial €7m plus up to €3m in bonuses, including goals, appearances and featuring in the Champions League. Colidio, who will begin his career at Inter playing for Stefano Vecchi’s youth side when he moves in January, shot to prominence at this year’s South American under-17 tournament in Chile when he scored two goals against Peru. Fernando Batista, his coach with the national team, is particulary excited about Colidio’s future. “He is strong, so he can play well in Europe, but he has the technical ability you would associate with Latin American players,” he said. “I would like to compare him to Gonzalo Higuaín”. Known as ‘Coli’ to his teammates, he has also been watched by Manchester City, Atlético Madrid and Juventus and will be a big loss to Boca. **[Juan Carlos Pasman](https://twitter.com/TotiPasman?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Finished as the top scorer in the L’Alcúdia tournament – previous winners of the Golden Boot there include Raúl, Mauro Icardi and Gabigol. His goals clinched the Primavera Super Coppa and the Primavera league title for Inter in 2018.  
  
**October 2019 update** Over the summer Colidio was linked with moves to Newcastle, Leeds and Kilmarnock but after the English transfer window closed he moved instead to Sint-Truiden. He remains highly regarded at Inter and is expected to compete for a first-team place after his season in Belgium.  
  
**October 2020 update** Played very little in the first half of his debut season on loan at Sint-Truiden in 2019-20, but broke into the side in December and was sufficiently impressive thereafter for the Belgian side to extend the loan into a second campaign. “Since January his numbers are much more than good,” they explained in announcing the news. He now seems established in the first team. “Hopefully he can have a great career but for the moment, of course, he is focused only on Sint-Truiden,” they added.  
  
**October 2021 update**After starting last season well at Sint-Truiden, he struggled to keep his place during a second loan-spell in Belgium and finished the season in and out of the side. In the summer he returned to parent club Inter, but didn’t fit in with new manager Simone Inzaghi’s plans. He seems to have since fallen off the grid as a return to Argentina and a rumoured loan deal to Austria Vienna also collapsed and Inter didn’t register him for Serie A.  
  
**September 2022 update** After slumping into the Inter reserves earlier in the year, he decided to restart his career on loan at recently promoted Tigre back in his native Argentina. Quickly establishing himself as a regular in a high-flying Tigre side, he scored the winning goal in the quarter-finals of the league title race against River Plate. And this taste of success and regular football has helped him dream of bigger challenges. “I have been part of all the Argentina youth teams, but now my goal is to make the national team, the ultimate goal for any player.”
