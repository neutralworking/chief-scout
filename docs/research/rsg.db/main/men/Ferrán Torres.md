A talented attacking player that some have likened to Marco Asensio, Ferrán Torres has started well after making the step up to Valencia Mestalla, the Valencia B team that plays in segunda B (Spain’s third tier, which is split across four regionalised divisions). Although it is early still, that start has reinforced the expectations that have surrounded him for the last couple of years and, should he perform well this season, will help to resolve the doubts about whether he would be able to impose himself at a higher level against senior players. A good athlete and big for his age, the question was if he would dominate as much when he took the step up and no longer had that physical advantage. Creative, able to take players on and adept at using his body to hold off opponents and shoot. A member of the Spain Under-17 squad, although not necessarily a starter, there has reportedly been interest from Real Madrid and Barcelona with AS saying he has a buy-out clause of €8m. Not bad for a 17-year-old. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**
 
**October 2018 update** Last season was Torres’s first in Valencia’s first-team squad and in February, the day before his 18th birthday (though strictly speaking, having been born on 29 February, he didn’t have one), he made his first start, assisting his team’s goal in a 1-1 draw in Bilbao. In April he signed a new three-year contract with a €100m release clause. “I’ve had a lot of good times in my career considering how young I am,” he said. “Everything has happened really quickly, but I’ll keep my feet on the ground and keep working.”
 
**October 2019 update** Spain’s outstanding player as they won the Under-19 European championships this summer, scoring the winning penalty in the shootout in the semi-final and getting two goals in the final against Portugal. Has since won an Under-21 call-up. Got his first La Liga goal at Celta back in January and expected to play an increasing role this season.
 
**October 2020 update** After making 26 league starts for Valencia last season Torres moved to Manchester City for £24.5m in the summer, following the same path as his fellow Valencia youth-team graduate David Silva. “The club told me they weren’t counting on me,” he told Marca.
 
**October 2021 update** Quite a season for the winger, who made 36 appearances for City in his debut campaign and featured for Spain at the Euros, scoring two goals as they reached the semis. This season he is likely to play more as a striker with Pep Guardiola saying: “Ferran can play in this role, his movement is similar to that of Jamie Vardy’s.”
 
**September 2022 update** Torres was brought in as the new star signing at Xavi’s Barcelona in January where he has often played as a false nine, much like his role during his Manchester City days. Despite an outstanding performance in the 4-0 thrashing of Real Madrid at the Bernabéu in March, Ferran has nonetheless largely struggled to score regularly at the Camp Nou, which has led Xavi to explore other attacking options. This season the competition in the Barcelona attacking line is even fiercer and Torres has so far become victim of this battle for places. Still, he has remained upbeat, vowing to fight for his spot. “Yesterday’s match [against Viktoria Plzen] was a great help for the players who haven’t played much,” he admitted. “We have to look at each match and if the manager needs us, we have to give everything we can.”
