The powerful forward came to prominence in the proven talent factory of Malmo FF after moving to Sweden with his family from DR Congo as a six-year-old. He scored a lot of goals in the club’s successful youth teams and played a lot in his spare time with best friend Pavle Vagic, who is pretty much as talented. Mawana also proved himself in Sweden’s national youth teams and has scored 17 goals in 24 games. One of them sticks out, a beautiful bicycle kick versus Romania in the final of a four-nations tournament in September 2017. Since signing a four-and-half-year contract with Hoffenheim in December 2016 he has taken further steps and impressed in the youth set-up at the Bundesliga club. Teams from all of Europe’s top leagues, including Chelsea, are following this rare talent whose favourite player is Cristiano Ronaldo, and not Zlatan Ibrahimovic. **[Olof Lundh](https://twitter.com/oloflundh?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Suffered a cruciate injury in the spring but is determined to come back stronger. Has 14 goals in 21 games for the Sweden Under-17 team and three goals in three games for the Under-19s.
 
**October 2019 update** Returned to Sweden and signed for Hammarby in March 2019, signing a three-year deal with the Allsvenskan club. The sporting director, Jesper Jansson, said Mawana would be given time to continue his rehabilitation from the cruciate ligament injury and so far he has played more games for the Superettan partner club Frej than for Hammarby.
 
**October 2020 update** Returned to Frej for the 2020 season and scored three goals in seven games but has not featured since mid-July.
 
**October 2021 update** Back from a fractured leg and back at Hammarby he was recently included in a few Allsvenskan matchday squads.
 
**September 2022 update** Has moved to Åtvidaberg in the Swedish third tier and is finally playing regularly.
