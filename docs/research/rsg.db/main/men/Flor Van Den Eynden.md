Internazionale already had two young Belgians on their payroll in Xian Emmers and Zinho Vanheusden and they brought in a third in January by seeing off Racing Genk’s attempt to sign Van Den Eynden, one of the country’s brightest young talents. Van Den Eynden, a highly rated central defender who also came through the ranks at KV Mechelen, has been a mainstay of Belgium’s under-18 squad and possesses the qualities that Italians like in a defender: excellent vision and an understanding of the game. He also knows how to play out from the back. **[Kristof Terreur](https://twitter.com/HLNinEngeland)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Established as a regular for Inter’s under-19 side, Van Den Eynden was joined by yet another Belgian prospect in the summer when Tibo Persyn arrived from Club Brugge. Van Den Eynden has been tipped for a senior chance before too long.

**October 2019 update** Despite a promising start to life in Italy, Van Den Eynden moved to the Dutch second division side FC Eindhoven in September.  
  
**October 2020 update** Made six starts for FC Eindhoven in the second tier last season but has been confined to the bench so far in this campaign.  
  
**October 2021 update** Released by Eindoven in the summer and yet to find a new club.  
  
**September 2022 update** Joined Helmond Sport in the Dutch second tier and, after an injury-hit first season, he is now playing regularly at centre-back.
