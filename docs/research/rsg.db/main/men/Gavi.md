“It’s ahead of time? Maybe, yeah, I won’t deny that,” Luis Enrique said, but the Spain manager insisted he was still convinced when he called up Gavi to the senior squad to face Italy in October – even though he had only just turned 17 and played just 289 minutes in the Barcelona first team. “When you see him play it surprises you,” Luis Enrique said, “and in the three or four times he has played, he has left me with no doubt at all that he can be a very important player for the future not just for Barcelona but also the national team.” Luis Enrique was right of course and Gavi became the youngest ever Spaniard to represent La Roja at 17 years and 62 days as he not only started but impressed in the 2-1 Nations League win. Luis Enrique had watched Gavi in the Barcelona youth system, which he joined from Betis aged 11 having led them to a 5-0 win over Barça, that according to the national team manager he is the “standard bearer” at La Masia, an inside midfielder, and that one member of the technical staff at the Spanish federation says he is the star of this generation – “very much a Barcelona profile, but with the physicality of the modern game too, intense and with a tremendous character”. A mix of Iniesta and Xavi, some have said. So, no pressure kid. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**

**September 2022 update** It is incredible to think that Gavi has only just turned 18, given the astonishing progress he has made over the past year. Since Ronald Koeman handed him his debut, he hasn’t looked back, immediately becoming a regular at Barcelona. Under Xavi that hasn’t changed. It’s not just at the Camp Nou where the teenager is making a splash. For the national team, he has also started in eight out of 10 games since manager Luis Enrique gave him his first call-up. “Gavi isn’t just a player who runs a lot, battles every situation and is at a top level with his defensive skills. He is also special with the ball and going forward,” Luis Enrique explained. “He can receive the ball between the lines, attack the opponent’s defence, is capable of finding the final pass and he can score. He can shoot with both feet, is good in the air and his fitness is incredible. At only 18 years old, he is a unique player.”  
  
**October 2023 update** Failed to truly shine last season for Barcelona and Ilkay Gündogan’s arrival at the Nou Camp from Manchester City this summer seemed to indicate that his first-team place was under serious threat. But an injury to key man Pedri left the door ajar and Gavi, playing in a different role this season, has thrived. “Right now, it is really difficult to take Gavi off the pitch,” manager Xavi praised after the 19-year-old inspired Barcelona to a 5-0 romp over Betis in September. “He gives the team a soul, intensity, the proper rhythm, recoveries.”
