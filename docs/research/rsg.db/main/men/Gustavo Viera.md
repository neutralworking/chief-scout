Viera made his profesional debut for Montevideo’s Liverpool at the age of 16 in the Uruguayan first division and then became the third-youngest player to feature in the Copa Sudamericana. An explosive and strong striker, his characteristics can be compared to Luis Suárez, Edinson Cavani and Diego Forlán. Linked with a move to the other Liverpool last year, he was part of the Uruguay under-17 side that failed to qualify for the World Cup and has since reportedly attracted interest from Arsenal, Chelsea, Barcelona and Juventus. “I loved watching the Premier League when I was younger, especially because of the speed and the physicality,” he said. “Now I am playing myself, for sure I want to arrive there one day.” **[Jaime F. Macias](https://twitter.com/Jaimefmacias?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Last November France Football named Viera, who at the time had played only eight league games for Liverpool, on its list of the 12 most promising players in the game. “When I first heard about it I didn’t think it was true,” he said. “I am very proud because not everyone is in such a magazine.” Nicknamed El Inglés and represented by an English agent, Viera will be able to move to Europe after his 18th birthday in October.
 
**October 2019 update** Viera remains at Liverpool, where he has made only very occasional first-team appearances in 2019 – amounting to eight minutes over two games in the Apertura.
 
**October 2020 update** The striker gained more first-team experience in 2019-20 before making the switch to Europe, joining Portuguese Primeira Liga side Santa Clara in August. Viera said he was “proud” to be joining the club and that he was looking forward to playing in Europe.
 
**October 2021 update** Viera’s stay at Santa Clara was no success and he featured just once for the Portuguese mid-table team. He did make the news in January of this year when he fainted during a post-match training session for unused subs and was rushed to hospital. Low blood pressure was deemed the cause, as tests found nothing serious, but he subsequently dropped out of the picture. Returned to his home country in September to play on loan for Fenix, but is yet to make the first-team squad.
 
**September 2022 update** The step to Fenix, a mid-table team in his home country, seems to have helped Viera secure some much-needed first-team football. But despite making progress over the last year and managing to score his first professional goals, he has not always been a starter at Fenix. Perhaps his most memorable feat came at the end of last year in the 1-1 against Liverpool. Viera came off the bench to score the only goal for Fenix, after his older brother, Santiago, had opened the scoring for the opponents. “It was a very important goal for me because I went through the Liverpool youth system,” he claimed. “At the end of the game we took a photo on the pitch with our mum!”
