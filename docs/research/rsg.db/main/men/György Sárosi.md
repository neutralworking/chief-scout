CF					HUN	C	0.0	Most frequently playing as a striker, he was also an accomplished attacking centre-half and even played a number of games in the full-back line.		Undoubtedly the star of Hungary's 1938 World Cup final team, György Sárosi was perhaps one of the most versatile players of the pre-Second World War era. A one-club man with Ferencváros in his playing days, he also had a long coaching career in Italy after the war. Breaking into the first team in the 1930-31 season, Sárosi was initially used as a centre-half but as his skill on the ball became apparent, he was moved into ever more advanced roles either at inside-forward or centre-forward.
