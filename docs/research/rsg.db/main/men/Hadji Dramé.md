Dramé possesses all the tools to become a bona fide star. It’s no wonder Spanish and French clubs, as well as the famed Aspire Academy in Dakar, have shown interest. Hadji’s father, Djibril, who coaches Mali’s domestic national team and the Bamako club Onze Créateurs, talked him out of the Dakar move for family reasons. Dramé scored three goals during the 2017 under-17 Africa Cup of Nations, leading the Eaglets to their second continental title. He is a pacy, right-footed winger who has a propensity to cut into the centre of the pitch and attack the opposition backline. A stellar outing at the under-17 World Cup this month could expedite Dramé’s seemingly inevitable move to a European club. **[Maher Mezahi](https://twitter.com/mezahimaher)**
 
**October 2018 update** Dramé had a fantastic year at international level. He scored three goals at the Under-17 World Cup last October and got three more in Under-20 matches throughout 2018. There are reports that Dramé has signed a pre-contract with Shakhtar Donetsk after a trial.
 
**October 2019 update** It was expected that Dramé, after playing a decisive role in Mali winning the 2017 and 2019 Under-17 Afcon tournaments, would have made his move to Europe. Yet the winger has carried on with Yeelen Olympique, marking out his territory in the Malian championship for now.
 
**October 2020 update** Finally took his first steps in Europe by joining Estonian side Paide Linnameeskond in February, where he has yet to make his debut.
 
**October 2021 update** Now an integral member of Paide Linnameeskond in the Estonian first division, where he playing – and scoring – regularly.
 
**September 2022 update** Left Estonia for Georgia in January, signing with FC Dila, where he enjoys regular playing time across their attack.
