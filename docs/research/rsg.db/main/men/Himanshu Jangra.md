Indian fans have long dreaded the retirement of legendary striker Sunil Chhetri, the 37-year-old who has scored 75 goals for the country. Himanshu has a long way to go to even suggest he can be a long-term replacement but has been earmarked as “one to watch”. The striker – taller and more powerful than the diminutive Chhetri – received a solid football foundation coming through the respected Minerva Punjab academy. In 2019, he scored seven goals to win India South Asia’s Under-15 Championship and himself the Golden Boot thanks in some part to his aerial prowess. Still raw, it is hoped that his recent move to Delhi FC in the I-League’s second division will help him use the ball a little better but all the ingredients are there. **[John Duerden](https://twitter.com/JohnnyDuerden?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** The Under-20 star joined Indian giants East Bengal in August.  
  
**October 2023 update** Made his debut in the Indian Super League with East Bengal and is currently on loan with Delhi FC.
