His positional intelligence, rare composure and ability to organise and command his defence singled him out at an early age. He became the youngest player, at 16, to get a call-up to the Krasnodar under-21 side to play in the Premier League Youth League. In the country where the 38-year-old Sergey Ignashevich and the 35-year-old Berezutski brothers are still considered to be the best centre-backs, Martynov has all the qualities to make the step up and be one of the country’s best in the position for years to come. He is physically strong, reads the game well and is good at initiating attacks and finishing them, with four goals (all headers) and five assists since last November. Featured in all three of the Russia under-17 Euro 2017 qualifying round matches and scored against Norway. **[Artur Petrosyan](https://twitter.com/arturpetrosyan)**
 
**October 2018 update** A rock in the centre of defence for the Krasnodar youth side as they won promotion to the FNL (Russia’s second tier). Earned a place in the senior squad at 18 and appeared in four pre-season games this summer. Was unlucky to pick up an injury at the start of the season and has just returned to full fitness.
 
**October 2019 update** Featured in the senior team in a couple of friendlies in pre-season but is back with the youth teams. Has been switching between Krasnodar’s three teams in Russian leagues (FNL, PFL and Youth Premier League), playing in a variety of positions. He’s a regular for the Russia Under-19 team and is expected to spend the coming winter with the first team, who are still suffering from the departure of Andreas Granqvist.
 
**October 2020 update** Has featured for Krasnodar’s first team in pre-season friendlies but is yet to take part in a competitive match for the club. Is a regular starter for Krasnodar-2 in the FNL (the Russian second tier). Has become a versatile player switching between centre-back and right-back.
 
**October 2021 update** Spent the winter pre-season with his parent club, Krasnodar, before finishing the season on loan at Tambov in the Premier League. At the start of this season he joined Rotor Volgograd in Russia’s second tier on a season-long loan.
 
**September 2022 update** After spending a season at Rotor on loan from Krasnodar he signed a full contact with the Volgograd club and is now a regular centre-back for the second division side. Has played for all of Russia’s youth teams from Under-16 to Under-21 but has not yet made his senior debut.
