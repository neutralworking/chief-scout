The ‘Romanian Donnarumma’ has been training with Lazio’s first team for months and has made impossible saves his speciality since his move to Rome two seasons ago, having previously been on trial at Nottingham Forest. “I have had several offers from English, Italian and Dutch clubs but in the end I decided to sign a contract with Lazio because I liked the project,” he said. Despite being 6ft 6in, Rus is agile. In Romania he is regarded as a natural future No1 in the national team, taking over from the experienced keepers Ciprian Tatarusanu and Costel Pantilimon. **[Emanuel Rosu](https://twitter.com/Emishor)**
 
**October 2018 update** A meniscus injury stopped his progress at Lazio, leaving Rus out of contract. However, he has fully recovered and is talking to several clubs, some in Serie A.
 
**October 2019 update** The goalkeeper is under contract at the Romanian champions CFR Cluj but has been loaned to the second-tier high-flyers Turris. The 19-year-old is still regarded as the future Romania No 1, although for the time being he is with the Under-21s.
 
**October 2020 update** The goalkeeper is part of the first-team squad of Romanian champions CFR Cluj. Included this year on the list for the Golden Boy award, Rus dreams of a return to Italy, where he impressed at youth level for Lazio.
 
**October 2021 update** Rus dropped a level, hoping to become a regular after not playing too much in the past two years, leaving Romanian champions CFR Cluj to play for Ripensia Timisoara in the second tier.
 
**September 2022 update** Still under contract with Romanian champions CFR Cluj and at least got some game time last season on loan at Ripensia, where he kept eight clean sheets in 20 matches.
