PositionForward

Born10 May 2003

Goals. Lots and lots of goals. Widely considered the outstanding forward in the Real Madrid youth system, Israel Salazar has scored over 150 of them in six years since joining the club at the age of 12 – when he was the youngest player in the club’s residence alongside Bruno Iglesias. He scored 29 goals in 24 games as Real Madrid’s under-16s won the league in 2018-2019 and had 28 in 18 games with the Juvenil C before lockdown last season. Received an unexpected call-up to travel to Nyon in August for the rescheduled final rounds of the Uefa Youth League, which Madrid won, he made his debut in the quarter-final when he came off the bench against Internazionale. He has been promoted to the Juvenil A (under-19s) for this season, and may anticipate opportunities with Castilla, the club’s B team, under Raúl (who coached the Youth League team). An under-17 international with Spain, he has the speed, technical ability and dribbling to play off the striker as well as as a No 9, where he has an exceptional range of finishing and good movement off the ball. Although still slight, he has filled out and is strong too. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Now firmly established with Real Madrid’s Juvenil A team (under-19s) who are currently defending their Uefa Youth League crown, Sala’s now earning the occasional call-up to Castilla, the Madrid giant’s reserve team who are coached by Raúl. Was also picked for Spain Under-19s squad at the start of the year.  
  
**September 2022 update**Sala's development hasn’t quite continued as many might have expected over the last year. He played in one of his first matches with Real Madrid Castilla, but that turned out to be his only appearance in 2021-2022. He was thus sent back to the youth side where he played in the Uefa Youth League, but failed to score in Europe’s most important club youth tournament. This summer, Isra went out on loan to Ucam Murcia, who play in the regional Segunda Federación (fourth tier), and has come off the bench twice in their first three matches. "I came here to have a great year, gain experience and get some playing time under my belt,” he said.  
  
**October 2023 update** An uninspiring loan move to UCAM Murcia during the 2022-23 season culminated in his contract expiring at Real. Firing in just two goals in 31 appearances, Sala left for Real Valladolid’s B-team on a free in the summer where he will hook up with former Arsenal forward Júlio Baptista, who was appointed manager in 2021.
