Life keeps getting better for the teenager who was born in Fulda in central Germany to a German mother and Nigerian father. Having left Chelsea’s academy last summer, Musiala progressed swiftly through Bayern’s age-group sides and has been part of Hansi Flick’s first-team squad since the restart, becoming the youngest player to make a Bundesliga appearance for the club against Freiburg in June. The elegant attacking midfielder – who moved to England at the age of seven and attended Whitgift School in Croydon – surpassed Roque Santa Cruz as Bayern’s youngest-ever goalscorer in the 8-0 demolition of Schalke in September and has been earmarked by his manager as a potential star of the future for club and country. Whether that will be England or Germany is still yet to be resolved, however, with Musiala having represented both at youth level and struck up a close friendship with Borussia Dortmund’s Jude Bellingham on international duty. “I would certainly try to get him,” said Flick. “But it is his decision. He has clear ideas.” **[Ed Aarons](https://twitter.com/ed_aarons)**

**October 2021 update** What a year Musiala has had. Broke into the Bayern first team and decided to play senior football for Germany rather than England. “I have a heart for Germany and a heart for England,” he said upon announcing his decision. “Both hearts will keep on beating.” Made two appearances at Euro 2020 as Germany crashed out to England of all teams. Won the Bundesliga with Bayern.  
  
**September 2022 update** The sky really is the limit for Musiala, who has two Bundesliga titles at the age of 19 and was recently compared with Lionel Messi by Lothar Matthäus. “He has everything: fast, good dribbling, the last pass, he can score.” Called Bambi by his teammates he explained his aims for the new season as follows: “For me personally, it is to get a little more playing time, keep working on myself. As a team, we want to win the Bundesliga, the Champions League, and score lots of goals.” And then there is the World Cup too.  
  
**October 2023 update** Already a superstar at the age of 20, he has played 131 first-team games for Bayern at the time of writing and is one of the most influential players in the Bundesliga. "He is out of this world," said Rio Ferdinand after Bayern had played Manchester United in the Champions League. "His ceiling is not far off that of Jude Bellingham."
