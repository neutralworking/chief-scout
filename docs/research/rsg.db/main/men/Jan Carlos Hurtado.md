The Venezuelan striker is an intriguing talent. His size and strength suggest he would be a powerful hold-up striker, willing to stretch the defence and include others in the attack while looking to poach goals in the penalty area. But Hurtado is not entirely that. He is fast and loves bursting into the channels to get on the ball and charge at defenders. Defenders at youth international level have struggled to cope with his directness and often resort to fouling the imposing 17-year-old as the only means of stopping him. Hurtado has played in the Copa Libertadores for Deportivo Tachira and will look to continue enjoying success in a flourishing youth football scene in Venezuela. **[Stephen Ganavas](https://twitter.com/scoutedftbl?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** The Venezuelan spent the past year out of the professional game after a contract dispute with his former club resulted in a lengthy ban. He made his return to senior football this September with the Argentinian first division side Gimnasia.
 
**October 2019 update**
 
**October 2019 update** Strongly linked with a move to Newcastle United over the summer, Hurtado ended up staying in Argentina and signing a four-year contract at Boca Juniors, where he is a regular first-team player.
 
**October 2020 update** Was deployed mainly off the bench in 2019-20 and was loaned out to RB Bragantino in Brazil and received his first senior call-up to the Venezuelan national team for the October double header against Colombia and Paraguay.
 
**October 2021 update** Despite his loan move at RB Bragantino not quite working out as both parties wanted, his stay was extended by a year this summer. Hurtado is mostly on the bench for the ambitious Copa Sudamericana finalists, while remaining a regular in the Venezuela squad.
 
**September 2022 update** Hurtado is still mostly a fringe player at RB Bragantino. Despite the fact that last year’s loan at the Brazilian club wasn’t a great success, Bragantino still consider him a player for the future and extended his loan deal until the summer of 2023 with a minimum-release clause of $5.5m. Hurtado has not yet played for the Venezuelan national team under the new manager, José Pekerman, who took over at la Vinotinto in January this year.
