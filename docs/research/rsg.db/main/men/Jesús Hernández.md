
ClubQuerétaro

PositionForward

Born9 January 2004

In just eight months, the 17-year-old Queretaro striker went from scoring twice on his Under-20 Liga MX debut in January, to making his professional Liga MX debut in August. The most impressive thing? The promising teenager has not looked out of place in Mexico’s top-flight and demonstrated his quality hold-up play, clever distribution and knack for recovering the ball through pressuring the opposition. Currently a part of Mexico’s youth national team structure, it may be only a matter of time before he gets a a callup to the senior squad, especially if the goals start to flow. **[Cesar Hernandez](https://twitter.com/cesarhfutbol)**  
  
**September 2022 update**  
  
**September 2022 update** One of the few positives to emerge from Mexico’s Under-20 embarrassing failure to qualify for next year’s World Cup after El Tri were humbled by Guatemala in the Concacaf qualifiers. Is currently on the verge of signing for Spanish side Elche on a year’s loan.  
  
**October 2023 update** Scored 10 goals for Elche’s Under-19s last season before being invited to take the step up and train with the Spanish second division club’s first team in the summer. Was also part of the Mexico team that played at the Maurice Revello tournament held in the south of France.
