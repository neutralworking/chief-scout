Larsen was nicknamed “Peter Crouch” at Sarpsborg, and not only because he is 6ft 4in and supports Liverpool. He is also very good at scoring goals of all kinds, and was spotted by the club after netting five times on his debut for the tiny club Kvik Halden. His development has been quick, thanks to the youngster’s dedication and hard work – he is known to come first to the training sessions and leave last. By the beginning of 2017 he was invited to the senior team by Sarpsborg’s coach, Geir Bakke, and scored a majestic goal in a friendly against CSKA Moscow. “It is in his blood,” Bakke said. A prolific scorer for Norway’s youth teams, Larsen made his league debut for Sarpsborg in May. He was linked with a move to Sunderland and Freiburg but signed on loan for Milan’s youth team in August, to be coached by Gennaro Gattuso. The Italian side have an option to buy the striker, who possesses good vision and can provide assists with a deft touch, which is quite rare for a footballer of his height. **[Michael Yokhin](https://twitter.com/Yokhin)**
 
**October 2018 update** Returned to Sarpsborg in July after a year at Milan’s Primavera team and joined the first-team squad. Started his first Eliteserien match in August.
 
**October 2019 update** Has become an undisputed starter for Sarpsborg this season and had four goals in 18 matches at the time of writing. He is making good progress but can still be a little bit inconsistent.
 
**October 2020 update** Joined Groningen from Sarpsborg in the summer, and has started well, being included in the starting XI in the Eredivisie. Given the No 9 shirt by his new club. “I got up this morning with a big smile because I knew from today that I was a FC Groningen player,” he said on the day he was presented. Has four goals in five games for Norway Under-21s.
 
**October 2021 update** Had a superb 2020 and made his debut for the national team but an ankle injury slowed down his upward trajectory and he has struggled to regain the same form after it.
 
**September 2022 update** After a strong season he was expected to join Middlesbrough but the Championship side could not agree a fee with Groningen so Strand Larsen signed for Celta Vigo on deadline day for a fee believed to be in the £10m region. He made his La Liga debut against Cádiz on 2 September.
