One recurring criticism of Claude Le Roy during his stint managing the Togo national team was his misplaced trust in young players. Josué Doké is a good example of a player Le Roy may have overburdened as he awarded the youngster a debut at the age of 16. Doké already has four caps and a goal to his name. Since Le Roy’s sacking, however, Doké has struggled to find his way back into the Sparrows squad. The lanky winger remains a serious talent to monitor as he progresses with his club in Ghana, the West African Football Academy. **[Maher Mezahi](https://twitter.com/mezahimaher)**  
  
**September 2022 update**  
  
**September 2022 update** Sadly Doké has not played any football since March when he suffered a serious injury in Turkey while featuring for the Togo Under-23 national team.  
  
**October 2023 update** Charleroi did not exercise their option to sign Doké permanently after a few months on loan in Belgium, so Doké agreed a one-year deal with Lithuanian club Be1 NFA this summer, where he has got off to a promising start.
