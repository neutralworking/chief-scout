An attacking midfielder, in the style of the classic No 10s, with great passing and excellent movement between the lines. Though he has lived in Germany since he was 12, he has chosen to represent Uruguay, the country of his birth. “It was an easy decision,” he said. “I have Uruguayan blood, a Uruguayan heart. I feel Uruguayan, not German.” His style can be compared to his countryman Federico Valverde, though his idol is Diego Forlán. At 17 he has moved into Schalke’s under-19 squad. **[Jaime F Macias](https://twitter.com/Jaimefmacias?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Has made a blistering start to the 2021-22 season playing for Schalke’s under-19 team, scoring three goals in his first four starts. Despite having stated his desire to represent Uruguay and having represented La Celeste at under-17s level, he was recently called up to the Germany under-19s for the group stages of the European Championship qualifiers.  
  
**September 2022 update** Still yet to make his official debut with newly promoted Schalke, but the German-born forward has progressed to the first-team squad on several occasions after making steady progress in the youth divisions. Despite flirting with the German national set-up, he was called up to the Uruguay Under-20s team that played this summer’s eight-team Torneo L’Alcúdia friendly tournament in which they finished runners-up behind Argentina.  
  
**October 2023 update** Signed for Augsburg’s reserve team that compete in the German fourth division after playing last season in the fifth tier for Ingolstadt. Has signed a two-year deal.
