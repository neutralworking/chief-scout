Born in [Valladolid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valladolid "Valladolid"), [Castile and León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n "Castile and León"), Llorente finished his development with [La Liga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Liga "La Liga") giants [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF"), making his debut in the competition in the [1987–88 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1987%E2%80%9388_La_Liga "1987–88 La Liga") with fellow league club [RCD Mallorca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RCD_Mallorca "RCD Mallorca"), on loan. He returned to the Spanish capital upon his team's relegation, going on to play 33 competitive games during his two-year tenure and win five major titles, including two consecutive national championships. He appeared in six [European Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League "UEFA Champions League") matches with the _Merengues_, scoring in a 6–0 home win against [CA Spora Luxembourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CA_Spora_Luxembourg "CA Spora Luxembourg") in the [1989–90 edition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1989%E2%80%9390_European_Cup "1989–90 European Cup").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julio_Llorente#cite_note-1)

Llorente signed with [CD Tenerife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CD_Tenerife "CD Tenerife") in summer 1990, helping to the [Canary Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "Canary Islands") side's domestic and [European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA "UEFA") consolidation.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julio_Llorente#cite_note-2)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julio_Llorente#cite_note-3) From 1994 to 1996 he scored a total of nine goals in 75 league matches, adding five appearances in the [1996–97 UEFA Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Cup "1996–97 UEFA Cup") to help them reach the semi-finals.[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julio_Llorente#cite_note-4)

Tenerife were relegated to [Segunda División](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n "Segunda División") in [1999](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_La_Liga "1998–99 La Liga"), and 33-year-old Llorente joined another club in that level, [UD Salamanca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UD_Salamanca "UD Salamanca"), retiring after [one season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n "1999–2000 Segunda División") with top-flight totals of 280 games and 20 goals.

## Personal life

Llorente hailed from a sporting family. Aside from his uncle [Francisco Gento](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_Gento "Francisco Gento"), his three older brothers were also professionals: [José Luis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Llorente "José Luis Llorente") (1959) and [Antonio "Toñín"](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Llorente&action=edit&redlink=1 "Antonio Llorente (page does not exist)") (1963) played top-level [basketball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basketball "Basketball") for well more than one decade (including at [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_Baloncesto)), while [Francisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paco_Llorente "Paco Llorente") spent seven seasons with Real Madrid, coinciding with Julio from 1988 to 1990.
