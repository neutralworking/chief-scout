After signing his first professional contract in November 2016, Santos made his Ligue 1 debut with Saint-Étienne in February and continued to assert himself at youth level. His goals at youth level have underpinned his key qualities, especially a 40-yard chip against Clermont that demonstrated his acute sense of spatial awareness. This skill is also displayed in the Cape Verdean’s ability to navigate around tight spaces with close control. Defensively, the 17-year-old has the potential to be a pressing machine – his rapid lateral movements allow him to change direction and pressure multiple opposition players in quick succession. He has been followed by numerous clubs, notably Manchester City. **[Stephen Ganavas](https://twitter.com/scoutedftbl?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The Cape Verdean still finds himself on the fringes of the senior team. However, he did make his senior international debut in the past 12 months, appearing in a World Cup qualifier against Burkina Faso and a friendly against Andorra.  
  
**October 2019 update** After failing to make much impact at Saint-Étienne, Santos dropped down a division to join Nancy in the summer and has played his way into the Ligue 2 side’s starting XI.  
  
**October 2020 update** Made 26 league appearances for Nancy as they ended the season in midtable.  
  
**October 2021 update** After scoring three goals in 19 matches for Nancy last season, he signed for Oostende in Belgium, where he had, by the end of September, played in every match of the new season.  
  
**September 2022 update** Has grown into an established midfielder for club and country. He had a great year playing in 29 matches for Oostende in the Jupiler League and helping the Cape Verde Blue Sharks to the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations quarter-finals in January.
