KV Mechelen tried everything to convince Masangu to stay, even promising a spot in the first-team squad of 25 players. But Masangu, a profilic, modern defensive midfielder, had made up his mind. He decided to leave to join Roma, the club that had shown most interest in the previous 12 months. The Belgium youth international joined the Giallorossi’s youth squad, trained by Alberto De Rossi (Daniele’s father), during the summer. Other teams were also in for him: Fiorentina, Stoke and Standard Liège. He signed for three seasons at Roma, who paid Mechelen only €180,000 in compensation. **[Kristof Terreur](https://twitter.com/HLNinEngeland)**  
  
**October 2018 update** So far, so impressive in the Italian capital for the versatile Masangu. Performed well in last season’s Uefa Youth League and has become a regular for Belgium’s Under-18 side.  
  
**October 2019 update** A starter for Roma in the Uefa Youth League despite his tender years and now established in Belgium’s Under-19 side as well. Could be a big year for him.  
  
**October 2020 update** Signed a three-year with Beerschot back in Belgium and remains part of the national youth team set-up.  
  
**October 2021 update** Spent the second half of last season on loan at Croatian club Sibenik and joined Belgian second-tier side Virton in the summer.  
  
**September 2022 update** Was initially ruled out of action with a shoulder injury but then worked his way into the starting XI at Virton.
