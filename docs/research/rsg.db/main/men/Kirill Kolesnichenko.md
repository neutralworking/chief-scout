Kolesnichenko became the youngest Russian to be included in a professional club’s squad when he was 15 and the youngest player to feature when he came on as a substitute in the Championship-like tough and ruthless second division at 16 years and three months. A powerful but technically gifted winger who can attack from either side, he is rapidly progressing through Russia’s youth sides, scoring two goals in two games for the under-15s, three in nine for the under-16s and eight goals in 20 matches for the under-17s. Kolesnichenko, whose style is reminiscent of Borussia Dortmund’s Andriy Yarmolenko, has three Russian grassroots championship titles, the Moscow Cup and various European youth trophies to his name. Scored in all three of Russia’s under-17 matches in the 2017 Euros qualifying round to help his team win the group. **[Artur Petrosyan](https://twitter.com/arturpetrosyan)**
 
**October 2018 update** Was loaned to SKA Khabarovsk in March 2018 and made his Premier League debut aged 18 years, two months and 18 days. Failed to live up to expectations in the senior team, however, and spent the rest of the season with the SKA youth team. Now back at Chertanovo, having switched from winger to left-back.
 
**October 2019 update** Became a regular starter at Chertanovo this year as they pushed for an unlikely promotion to the Premier League. Had six goals and three assists in 17 starts before picking up an ankle injury in March. He kept playing until the end of the season, which made it worse, and then suffered a recurrence of the injury in pre-season to put him out until November.
 
**October 2020 update** Moved from his boyhood club Chertanovo to Kazakhstan big boys Kairat Almaty for €250,000 in March but was soon loaned out to Russian Premier League’s Covid-hit side Rotor Volgograd, where he made his full debut on 27 September.
 
**October 2021 update** Signed by Premier League side Ural after a season with SKA-Khabarovsk in Russia’s second tier. Now a regular in the Yekaterinburg team, left wing-back having become his default position.
 
**September 2022 update** It took him six years from becoming the youngest footballer in Russian history (had a professional debut at 16 years and four months) to being left without a club. Played for Premier League side Ural last season but after successfully avoiding relegation the Yekaterinburg club decided to release the 22-year-old who was without a goal or an assist in 18 matches.
