A right-back who excels both in defence and attack, has great pace and stamina, and models his game on Dani Alves, Aguilar is part of Peru’s under-23 squad and the first-team group at Alianza Lima. His promise, and his performances at the 2019 South American Under-17 Championships, were such that Manchester City signed him in April when he was still 16, though under the terms of the deal he will remain in Lima until he turns 18 in May 2021, and his arrival in England may be further delayed by work permit issues. He was named after Patrick Kluivert, and has brothers called Diego Armando and Rijkaard. **[Jaime F Macias](https://twitter.com/Jaimefmacias?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Loaned to City Football Group’s partner Lommel SK, the full-back made his debut in September coming off the bench for the Belgium second division side. “I want to give 100% in this new experience,” Aguilar said. “I don’t want to have to return to Peru for at least 10 years.”  
  
**September 2022 update** Despite playing just five times on loan at Belgium side Lommel last season, Manchester City chose to extend the defender’s loan until June 2023. The right-back is still part of the Peru Under-20s where he is considered the team’s biggest talent.  
  
**October 2023 update** Has played every minute of Belgian second division leaders Lommel SK’s start to the 2023-24 season under former Arsenal defender Steve Bould, scoring his first goal against Francs Borains at the end of September. Such fine form has alerted the Peruvian national team who may turn to the right-back to ignite their World Cup qualifying campaign.
