Today's result was important for a lot of united fans. 3-0 win, clean sheat and a worldie from promising young talent.

Let's not mince anything here. That goal is possibly one of the greatest we've ever seen in the Prem. I, for one, have seen a number of 'ranking premier league goals' videos, tv shows and compilations. Many of these have placed Rooney's bicycle kick against City as number one. But what about that Garnacho goal? It was a cleaner strike, from further out and, arguably, had better build up. Will it go down as the best PL goal ever? Not sure. It was only against a struggling Everton, whereas Rooney's goal had a lot more situational impact. But, let's not take away the deserved admiration, celebration and jubilation that Garnacho deserves. It was a terrific goal and should be recognised as so. He even got player of the match for it. 

However. And I say this with baited breath. Had it not been for that single spectacular goal, followed by a performance that justified the admiration of an upcoming, young goalscorer on the making, anyone playing closer attention should know who's performance we should be in awe of.

Kobbie Mainoo has, of course, been out for a while. A few speculative performances in pre season and one or two full team features left a lot of us wanting to see more. United hasn't had many impactful youth players for a few seasons now, with most fleeting and others being far and few between. Garnacho has obviously caught our attention, but since Rashford, or possibly McTominay, depending on your perspective, who have we got, what have we had?

Yet despite all this, Kobbie Mainoo has commanded attention. We know pre season doesn't always equate to performance in the Prem but, from what I've personally seen, we've all looked to this young lad as a man who could solve a situational issue and be a player we could both be proud of and hope to be effective for the team.

Not since Carrick have we had a solid, reliable holding midfielder. We've tried a few here and there, as well as shared duties via different formations. But when we bought Casemiro, let's be honest, we thought everything would finally click. It didn't quite hold up as long term as we thought.

Now, let's fast forward to this particular match. We could talk about his qualities against meagrer opponents, what skills he demonstrated, and what we think he could be capable of. But, what did we actually see to evidence such high expectations of young Kobbie?

I, for one, haven't seen a player handle a multi-man press as well as Mainoo did this match, in a very long time. We saw a number of situations where he'd be pressed and bypass two men closing him down with relative ease through pure awareness and a clever touch. We saw him slot into areas he shouldn't be and cover, but also support build up. We saw him stretch his duties by getting involved in play while advancing. Yet he was present when we needed him, he was always where we needed him to be. A lot of the time, it was low-key, and yet, he took centre stage in the passage of play. 

He bypassed lines of pressure with clever touches, got his head up early and saw what a lot of players probably wouldn't. This lad is still young, and yet he gives the impression that he is more than capable of the responsibility.

Had it not been for a fantastic performance from Garnacho, I think Kobbie would easily claim player of the match and, despite this, still the best player we saw on the pitch today. One for the future? 100%.
