Having spent three seasons at Barcelona’s La Masia academy before joining Leeds United’s under-23s on a free transfer this summer, Temenuzhkov is deservedly regarded as one of Bulgaria’s hottest football prospects. Growing up idolising Luis Suárez, the pint-sized prospect has shown the same predator’s instinct in the penalty area as the Uruguay striker. Last season his goals fired Barca’s Juvenil B team to the title, as he scored 30 goals in as many league games. Even though he received an invitation to play for Spain’s youth ranks, the Haskovo-born youngster chose to represent the country of his birth. **[Metodi Shumanov](https://twitter.com/shumansko)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The La Masia graduate continues to thrive in Yorkshire, having scored the goal that clinched the PDL North Division title for Leeds’ under-18s team in a 1-0 victory over Bolton Wanderers.  
  
**October 2019 update** Marcelo Bielsa gave Temenuzhkov his first-team debut last season but he is mainly playing for the Under-23s at the moment. Had by mid-September three caps for Bulgaria’s Under-21s at the age of 19.  
  
**October 2020 update** Leeds decided that Temenuzhkov would benefit from some first-team football and loaned him out to Real Unión in the Spanish third tier.  
  
**October 2021 update** Technically still under contract with Leeds but will stay for another season on loan at Real Unión and it looks like his Leeds adventure is all but over.  
  
**September 2022 update** His contract with Leeds having expired and after a few loan spells in Spain, he has signed a permanent deal with local fourth-tier side CDA Navalcarnero.
