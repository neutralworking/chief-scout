But hang on, who is that young player wearing the No 44 for Hertha Berlin making his Bundesliga debut against Bochum on 12 September? Most people did not have a clue who Linus Gechter was when he played 45 minutes at centre-back for an injury-hit Hertha side at the start of this season. Well, soon we will know a lot about him. He is an unruffled central defender who has a good passing game and is comfortable playing from the back. During lockdown youth football was cancelled and he was able to play against members of the pro team on a regular basis. He managed to convince Pål Dardai, now the first-team manager who previously worked at the academy, that he was worth a chance in the first team. He still needs to get a bit stronger but there is plenty of time for that. **Carsten Schröter-Lorenz, editor at [Kicker.de](https://www.kicker.de/)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Showed in 2021-22 that he belongs in the Bundesliga, making 13 appearances, scoring once and signing a contract extension until 2025 in March. “This is a great honour for me,” he said. “They have showed belief in me and I hope I can repay them for that.” Has been affected by illness at the start of the 2022-23 season.  
  
**October 2023 update** Gechter's health problems continued and he had an operation to remove his tonsils, according to Bild. Was loaned out to Eintracht Braunschweig to gain some more game time and is now back at Hertha, who have been relegated to the 2. Bundesliga. Has three appearances in the league at the time of writing in a struggling team.
