
ClubHertha Berlin

PositionLeft-back/left winger

Born15 May 2003

In summer 2020 Netz left Berlin's under-19s to join their under-23 side in Germany's fourth tier. In May he also made his debut in their full Bundesliga squad, though he did not make it off the bench, an experience he has repeated this season. Netz spends most of the week with Bruno Labbadia's highly ambitious senior team and the coach appreciates his skill set. The full-back has a fine left foot and makes dynamic runs down the flank, from where his crossing can be destructive. Compared to opponents in his age, he is physically strong and explosive. It will be interesting to see how he copes with physical challenges of the highest level. In 2020, he won the bronze Fritz Walter Medal, presented annually to Germany's most promising young players. **Carsten Schröter-Lorenz, editor at [Kicker.de](https://www.kicker.de/)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Became the most expensive 18-year-old to switch between two Bundesliga clubs when he joined Borrusia Mönchengladbach for €4m in August. “He is a highly interesting player, said the Gladbach sporting director, Max Eberl. “He is among the most talented German players in his position and we are very happy he decided to join us.”  
  
**September 2022 update** A remarkable first season at Gladbach for the youngster, playing 24 Bundesliga matches at left-back and left-midfield. Now a member of the Germany Under-21 team as well, he said this of his current club at the start of the 2022-23 season: “Borussia is a special club with its fans and amazing traditions. Mönchengladbach as a city lives for football more than anything else, which, for me, is shown by the fact that I am focused on the club and the game rather than anything outside of football.”  
  
**October 2023 update** Had to be patient as he was behind Ramy Bensebaini in the pecking order on the left-hand side in 2022-23 but things are looking up this season after the Algerian departed for Borussia Dortmund.
