When Chivas scouts spotted Puente in a regional tournament in 2014, they told his father there was no need for him to come to Guadalajara for a trial. They had already seen enough. The powerful, technical, two-footed No9 with a fierce shot has repaid that faith by going on to be part of title-winning teams at under-13, under-15 and under-17 level, scoring almost a goal per game in his time at the club. Puente cites the Brazilian Ronaldo as his idol and has impressed the Mexican national team setup with his humility and professionalism. A first-team debut in Liga MX may not be too far away for the all-Mexican club, given Puente is leading the scoring chart this season in the under-20s league. **[Tom Marshall](https://twitter.com/mexicoworldcup)**  
  
**October 2021 update** It’s the moment every footballer treasures, the day he steps out on the pitch for his first ever professional appearance. But for Puente, a dream debut turned into a nightmare. Coming on as a second-half sub for Guadalajara’s reserve side Tapatío last October, the brawny striker’s big day lasted just 20 minutes before he tore his cruciate ligaments. Since then it’s been a long and slow battle to return to fitness, yet with Chivas aiming to have 70% home-grown players in their first team in the future, Puente needn’t rush his recovery. “He’s a player who is going to stand out,” Guadalajara manager Víctor Vucetich predicted in August. “But we’ll have to give him all our support, especially at Chivas because our success is based upon them.”  
  
**September 2022 update** Almost 700 days after cruelly rupturing his cruciate ligament, the young striker is finally back kicking a football. Registered with the Guadalajara Under-20s this season, Puente made his long-awaited return to action in August 2022.  
  
**October 2023 update** In his finest form since doing his ACL in 2021, he helped Tapaito to the second division title last season with four goals and those encouraging displays have continued into the new campaign with Guadalajara fans clamouring for the striker to be promoted to the Chivas first team. “He’s a great example of what we want to achieve. I don’t want to reveal our plans too much, but this is what we want to do with Puente: boost his confidence by playing and scoring goals,” manager Veljko Paunovic observed. “We saw him in pre-season and we know he has so much potential.”
