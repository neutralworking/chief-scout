PositionWinger

Born11 February 2003

Already a regular for Super Liga also-rans Vozdovac, Cveticanin attracts attention for one particular trait, and that is his speed. The club claim they have tracked him running 35.2km/h during a competitive match, which would already make him, at 17, one of the fastest footballers around. “There is no doubt that he can still improve his speed,” Marko Kuridza, Vozdovac’s fitness coach, said. But pace is not the only interesting thing about the young winger reminiscent of Ivan Perisic or Gareth Bale, who has attracted attention from the likes of Juventus and Benfica. He is also technically sound, can play with both feet and is particularly good at crossing, while being strong and tall enough to play centrally as well if need be. **[Aleksandar Holiga](https://twitter.com/AlexHoliga)**  
  
**October 2021 update**  
  
**October 2021 update** Scored just one goal in 46 apperances for Vozdovac before transferring to La Liga’s Cádiz, who immediately loaned him out to third-tier San Fernando.  
  
**September 2022 update** His loan was cut short as Cádiz felt he had not been given enough chances, but now he is with the club’s B squad in the fourth tier and does not seem to be pulling up any trees there either.  
  
**October 2023 update** “I’m ready to play on a higher level and want to make it to the Under-21 national team,” Cveticanin said in April from the depths of the Spanish league system. Then, this summer, he was signed by Kolubara, a team newly relegated from the Serbian top flight, where he’s a starter now.
