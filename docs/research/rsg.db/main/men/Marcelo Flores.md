Flores was born in Canada, spotted in the Cayman Islands (where his father, who played professionally as a striker, coaches the women’s national team) and trained in England – originally at Ipswich, now at Arsenal – but he has chosen to represent his father’s country, Mexico. A versatile attacking midfielder, Flores compares his game to Philippe Coutinho and Eden Hazard, and had already trained with Arsenal’s first team before signing professional terms with the club in October. His older sister Silvana was part of the Mexico squad that reached the final of the 2018 Under-17 Women’s World Cup, and both she and his other sibling, 15-year-old Tatiana, left Arsenal to join Chelsea this summer. **[Tom Marshall](https://twitter.com/mexicoworldcup)**  
  
**October 2021 update** It’s been quite the year for the talented midfielder. Superb form for the Gunners’ under-18s side has seen Flores promoted to the under-23s on occasion and he was even rewarded with a call-up to first-team training before Arsenal’s Premier League game against Burnley in September. This progression from the youth ranks bodes well. Yet his international future is far from clear. Called up to the Mexico under-20s for September’s double header against Spain, he still hasn’t committed his future to El Tri, with England and Canada also wrestling over his future. A big decision looms.  
  
**September 2022 update** Was sent out on loan by Arsenal to the Spanish Second Division at the start of this season and has been a regular in the first team at Real Oviedo ever since. “We have to be patient and try to help him because he is a very young boy,” Oviedo coach Jon Pérez Bolo observed. “But I am very happy with his attitude.” Having finally nailed his colours to the Mexican national team, recent rumours suggest Tata Martino has his eyes on the 18-year-old to replace injured star Jesús Manuel Corona in this winter’s World Cup squad for Mexico.  
  
**October 2023 update** Snapped up by Mexican champions Tigres on a four-year deal in September for a reported £2m fee after making just two appearances on loan at Real Oviedo. “As soon as we knew there was a chance of signing him, we didn’t hesitate,” manager Robert Dande Siboldi enthused. “He’s not come here for the money, he’s come because he wants to play.” Now a regular on the fringes of the Mexican squad, Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta predicted a “tremendous future” for the youngster upon sanctioning a move back to his native Mexico.
