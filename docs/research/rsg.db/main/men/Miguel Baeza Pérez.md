Joined Real Madrid’s youth system five years ago. An “interior” midfielder in a creative role, who can also play wider, coming in off the right. Intelligent, left-footed, strong technically, skilful with a good shot and delivery from the wing or inside, as well as from dead balls. In addition he has a willingness to run beyond players into space between the opposition’s midfield and defence. Has been playing for Madrid’s juvenil (under-19s) despite being younger than most of his team-mates, and has scored eight times in four games this season. Extremely highly thought of. **[Sid Lowe](https://twitter.com/sidlowe)**
 
**October 2018 update** In April Zinedine Zidane invited Baeza to train with the first team, recognition for the player’s fine form in the junior ranks. Left-footed, able to play on the left or in the centre and also his team’s set-piece specialist, Baeza has also progressed into Spain’s Under-19 side.
 
**October 2019 update** Stood out with Real Madrid’s juvenil (Under-19s) in the Youth League and has made the step up to the Castilla team, playing in Spain’s Second Division B.
 
**October 2020 update** One of several young players to have left Real over the summer, Baeza joined Celta Vigo for €3m on a five-year contract. “I thought I would have another good year at Castilla and then make the leap [to La Liga], but when these opportunities come along you can’t pass them up,” he said, defining himself as “a super-hardworking and super-humble player who never gives up on a lost ball”.
 
**October 2021 update** A first season at Celta de Vigo didn’t go quite as planned for the talented winger. After a reasonable start where he was given minutes in almost every game, he saw his playing time slowly reduced, as he failed to fully convince manager Eduardo Coudet. Baeza was then set for a loan move to Spain’s Segunda División, but no deal could be arranged in time. By the start of October, he has played just one minute for Celta this season.
 
**September 2022 update** It looks like Celta have given up hope on Baeza. Last season he played exactly one minute for the team before going out on loan at Spanish second division team Ponferradina in January. With just five starts in 13 appearances, Baeza failed to make much of an impression. Unwanted at Celta, this season he again opted for a loan move at top-flight Portuguese side Rio Ave where he made a promising start with an assist in his first full 90 minutes.
