Forssell began his professional career in 1997 for [HJK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helsingin_Jalkapalloklubi "Helsingin Jalkapalloklubi"), having progressed through the team's youth teams, but he only remained at the club for a single season, making 17 league appearances, before he moved to England to join [Chelsea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelsea_F.C. "Chelsea F.C.") in 1998. He never broke into the Chelsea team, making 33 league appearances in seven years, and spent two loan spells at [Crystal Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C. "Crystal Palace F.C."), one at [Borussia Mönchengladbach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach "Borussia Mönchengladbach"), and two at [Birmingham City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C. "Birmingham City F.C."). He switched permanently to the latter club in 2005, but injury problems restricted him to 65 league appearances in three seasons. He joined German side [Hannover 96](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hannover_96 "Hannover 96") in July 2008 on a free transfer, but once again struggled with form and injury problems, and he left the club in 2011, having made 44 league appearances. This was followed by an unsuccessful season back in England, this time with [Leeds United](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C. "Leeds United F.C."); 15 league games failed to yield a single goal. Forssell returned to HJK in October 2012.

Forssell made his full international debut for Finland in 1999, and was a regular player for his country for the next 15 years. He earned 87 caps for his country, and scored 29 goals, making him Finland's sixth most capped player and second highest goalscorer.
