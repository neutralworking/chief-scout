#crosser


**José Miguel González Martín del Campo**, known as **Míchel** (Spanish pronunciation: [[ˈmitʃel]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish "Help:IPA/Spanish"); born 23 March 1963), is a Spanish former professional [footballer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_football "Association football") who played as a [right midfielder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midfielder#Wide_midfielder "Midfielder"), currently a [manager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manager_(association_football) "Manager (association football)").

He was most noted for his stellar [crossing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross_(association_football) "Cross (association football)") ability, also contributing with a fair share of goals.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-BIO-2)During his career he represented mainly [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF") – over a decade – achieving great team and individual success.

Míchel earned 66 [caps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cap_(sport) "Cap (sport)") for [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_national_football_team "Spain national football team") from 1985 to 1992, and appeared for the nation in two [World Cups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup "FIFA World Cup") (scoring four goals in the [1990 edition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup "1990 FIFA World Cup")) and one [European Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship "UEFA European Championship"). He started working as a manager in 2005, notably leading [Olympiacos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C. "Olympiacos F.C.") to two [Super League Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_League_Greece "Super League Greece") accolades.

## Playing career

### Club

The son of a footballer who had to retire from the game at 27 after a [road accident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traffic_collision "Traffic collision"), Míchel was born in [Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madrid "Madrid") and joined [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF") at the age of 13,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-FANS-3) his technique and physicality on the pitch quickly standing out.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-BIO-2) He moved quickly through the ranks to the first team, appearing – and scoring – once in the [1981–82 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1981%E2%80%9382_La_Liga "1981–82 La Liga"), in a 2–1 away win against [CD Castellón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CD_Castell%C3%B3n "CD Castellón") on 11 April 1982.

A member of the renowned _[La Quinta del Buitre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Quinta_del_Buitre "La Quinta del Buitre")_, which also featured [Emilio Butragueño](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emilio_Butrague%C3%B1o "Emilio Butragueño"), [Miguel Pardeza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_Pardeza "Miguel Pardeza"), [Rafael Martín Vázquez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rafael_Mart%C3%ADn_V%C3%A1zquez "Rafael Martín Vázquez") and [Manolo Sanchís](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manolo_Sanch%C3%ADs "Manolo Sanchís"),[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-4)[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-FANS-3) Míchel never played less than 31 [La Liga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Liga "La Liga") matches from 1985 to 1994. After helping [Castilla CF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_Castilla "Real Madrid Castilla") to the [second division](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n "Segunda División") title in [1984](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1983%E2%80%9384_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n "1983–84 Segunda División"), he was instrumental in the capital club's conquests, which included six leagues and two consecutive [UEFA Cups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League "UEFA Europa League"); he opened the score in the [1985 final of the latter competition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1985_UEFA_Cup_Final "1985 UEFA Cup Final"), against [Videoton FC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Videoton_FC "Videoton FC").[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-FANS-3)

In 1989, Míchel announced he would leave Real Madrid after having signed with an Italian side, but this never came to pass and he ended up staying until 1996. In [his penultimate season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_La_Liga "1994–95 La Liga"), he suffered a severe [knee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knee "Knee") injury which rendered him unavailable for several months,[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-FANS-3) but still bounced back for a [final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_La_Liga "1995–96 La Liga") solid year, after which he left for [Atlético Celaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Celaya "Atlético Celaya") in [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico") – where Butragueño was also playing – shortly after the arrival at Madrid of president [Lorenzo Sanz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorenzo_Sanz "Lorenzo Sanz"). He retired from football in 1997.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-BIO-2)

Míchel's career was not without incident: in 1988, he was hit by a bottle while on the pitch and, three years later, he was sanctioned by [UEFA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA "UEFA") for using an unorthodox method of disrupting the concentration of [Carlos Valderrama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlos_Valderrama_(soccer_player) "Carlos Valderrama (soccer player)"), in a game against [Real Valladolid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Valladolid "Real Valladolid"). The presiding judge in the case noted that "manipulating in public that of your neighbour which is a gift given exclusively to males by nature" violated a federation rule protecting a player's dignity.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-5)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-6)

### International

Míchel made his debut for the [Spain national team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_national_football_team "Spain national football team") on 20 November 1985 against [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria_national_football_team "Austria national football team"),[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-7) going on to appear in a further 65 internationals and score 21 goals (only missed a callup due to injury).[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-RSSSF-8) He played in the [1986](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup "1986 FIFA World Cup")[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-9) and the [1990 FIFA World Cups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup "1990 FIFA World Cup"), netting a [hat-trick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hat-trick "Hat-trick") against [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team "South Korea national football team")in Spain's second fixture during the latter tournament (3–1)[[10]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-10) and also a [penalty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football) "Penalty kick (association football)") against [Belgium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team "Belgium national football team")in the country's final group match.[[11]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-11)

Shortly after [Javier Clemente](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Javier_Clemente "Javier Clemente")'s arrival as national boss, Míchel was deemed surplus to requirements and never called again, although only 29.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-12) All national categories comprised, he received exactly 100 caps.[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADchel_(footballer,_born_1963)#cite_note-BIO-2)
