After a string of stunning performances for Danubio’s youth teams, the talented forward took the step up to the first team. Having made his professional debut, he went on to score a perfect hat-trick at the age of 16. “He is a beast,” said Danubio’s manager, Leo Ramos, after that performance. An explosive centre-forward, defenders struggle to cope with his pace. He’s able to score with both his feet as well as his head and his performances led to a transfer to Montevideo City Torque, part of the City Football Group, this summer. **[Jaime F Macias](https://twitter.com/Jaimefmacias?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Returned to action after a long-term injury at the start of the year, but then tore muscles in both legs two months later and has been on the treatment table ever since. Specialists from the City Group in Manchester are monitoring his recovery carefully.  
  
**October 2023 update** Won the Under-20 World Cup with Uruguay this summer, where he was used three times from the bench. Has since started to show form at Uruguayan City Football Group club, Montevideo City Torque, where he’s banged in three goals in recent weeks. “This last year has been complicated, but I knew how to remain patient,” the €3.1m striker told Uruguayan paper El País. “And I’m now starting to show my best form.”
