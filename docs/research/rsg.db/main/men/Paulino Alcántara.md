CF					PHI	B	0.0			He spent most of his playing career at Barcelona and was the first Filipino and Asian player to play for a European club.[1] He also played for Catalonia, the Philippines and Spain.[2] Alcántara made his debut for Barcelona at the age of 15 and remains the youngest player to play or score for the club. He also scored 100 goals in 69 matches, making him the club's highest goalscorer (counting goals scored in both official games and friendlies).[3] After retiring as a player in 1927 at the age of 31, he became a doctor. On 3 July 1927, Barcelona played against Spain in a testimonial match in his honour (Spain won 2–1[4]) and he later served as a club director between 1931 and 1934. In 1951, Alcántara became a coach and managed Spain for three games.
