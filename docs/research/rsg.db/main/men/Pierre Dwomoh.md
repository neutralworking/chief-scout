Every top talent comes with a manual – but not all of them are easy to read. One and a half years ago Pierre Dwomoh was promoted to Genk’s first-team squad, with the announcement that he was one of the biggest talents in their youth ranks. Last season he made his official debut, but then things went wrong. His ambition is his worst sin it seems. The gifted midfielder constantly needs challenges. He is fast, technically strong, tall, but also has an explosive edge. A feisty row with under-21 manager Hans Somers was the beginning of the end at Genk. He was sent to the B-squad but it didn’t work out. He pushed for a move to Milan but finally signed for Antwerp last summer. The ambitious Belgian club paid €2m in the hope they will sell him on for a bigger fee in a few years. In his manual there is a warning sign and no guarantee that he will deliver but all the tools are there. **[Kristof Terreur](https://twitter.com/HLNinEngeland)**  
  
  
**September 2022 update** His first season at Antwerp did not go quite according to plan and he has been loaned out to Braga in Portugal for the 2022-23 campaign.  
  
**October 2023 update** The temporary deal with Braga was made even more temporary when his loan was cut short in January. A move to Oostende followed, where he saw more action and he is now on loan at RWD Molenbeek, where he has began this season in a central midfield position. Could this be his breakthrough year?
