**Rafael Gordillo Vázquez** (born 24 February 1957) is a Spanish retired [footballer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_football "Association football"). A tremendously attacking [left wing-back](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Wing-back "Defender (association football)"), equally at ease as [defender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back "Defender (association football)") and [midfielder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midfielder#Wide_midfielder "Midfielder") and with a good effort rate, he had an unmistakable style of playing with his socks down.[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rafael_Gordillo#cite_note-BIO-1)

He represented mainly [Betis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Betis "Real Betis") and [Real Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Madrid_CF "Real Madrid CF") during his career, appearing in 428 [La Liga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Liga "La Liga") games and scoring 38 goals over 16 seasons. He won ten major titles with the latter club, including five national championships.

Gordillo was a mainstay for the [Spain national team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_national_football_team "Spain national football team") in the 80s, appearing in 75 matches and representing the nation in five international tournaments.
