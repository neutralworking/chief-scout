Few players can say they played a full season at the age of 16. Ravil is one of them. The left-footed centre-back managed to break into the Altinordu first team last season, starting all but three games for the Izmir-based side in the Turkish second division and earning a move to reigning champions Basaksehir. Altinordu have been behind the development a many of Turkey’s most promising young footballers over recent years and Tagir looks like he can be the next big thing, following in the footsteps of another defensive Altinordu graduate, Caglar Söyüncü. **[Emre Sarigul](https://twitter.com/Turkish_Futbol1?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Ravil is looking more and more like the real deal. In his first season at Basaksehir he made seven league appearances and started the last three games of the season. “God willing my goal is to play in Europe. If I keep developing I will attain my dreams,” he said back in May. It would not be a surprise if he made a high-profile move soon.  
  
**September 2022 update** Ravil broke into the Basaksehir first-team last season making a total of eight appearances. He has always said his goal is to play in Europe and he got his move. The Super Lig outfit loaned the 19-year-old to Westerlo for two seasons this summer. At the time of writing he has already made four appearances, including one start, and says: “Belgium is a great place for a young player to develop. I am working hard and have adapted well, the manager believes in me and I believe I will play regularly soon.”  
  
**October 2023 update** Continues to develop without the hype and fanfare he would have received being a young centre-back playing regularly in the Super Lig. Established himself as a first choice centre-back at Westerlo last season and is back in Belgium for the 2023-24 campaign.
