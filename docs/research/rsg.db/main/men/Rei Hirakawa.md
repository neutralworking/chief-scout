A deep-lying midfielder who is also capable of playing at No10 or in central defence, Hirakawa helped Japan to the semi-finals of the AFC under-16 championship in 2016 and will go to the under-17 World Cup in India this month as one of his country’s key players. He has amassed a wealth of experience in various national team competitions and demonstrates great maturity in his all-round play and in articulating his views on the game. Hirakawa has earned particular praise for his vision and superb positioning, and having established himself in the FC Tokyo under-18 team since the age of 16, he has since worked to bring greater defensive solidity and ball-winning skills to his play. He was fast-tracked on to FC Tokyo’s official player registration list in September 2016, made his debut for the club’s under-23 side in Japan’s third division a month later and has made regular starts there throughout the 2017 season in between spells on international duty. **[Ben Mabley](https://twitter.com/BenMabley?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Rei Hirakawa was a key player at the Under-17 World Cup in 2017 before Japan lost on penalties in the last 16. He made his J1 League debut for FC Tokyo that November but ill fortune struck days later when a metatarsal fracture sidelined him for several months. He returned with typical maturity, speaking of an experience “that will stand [him] in good stead for the future”. Hirakawa has resumed his position as regular starting midfielder for his club’s under-23 team in the Japanese third division, intertwined with occasional first-team call-ups and international duties for Japan’s Under-18s and Under-19s.
 
**October 2019 update** Despite the exploits of Takefusa Kubo, who joined Real Madrid in June 2019, FC Tokyo’s charge towards the J1 League title has not made it any easier for other younger talents such as Hirakawa to make the step up from third division games with the club’s Under-23 team. After a handful of Levain Cup appearances in the first half of this season, the 19-year-old has been sent on loan for regular football in Japan’s second tier with Kagoshima United. He has broken into the Under-20 national team but was not selected for the World Cup.
 
**October 2020 update** After a tough couple of seasons, with injuries and a difficult loan spell unsuccessfully battling relegation from J2 with Kagoshima United, Rei Hirakawa is finally regaining confidence, form, and his place in the Japanese top flight with FC Tokyo. The manager, Kenta Hasegawa, has praised his improved patience and maturity, rewarding the 20-year-old with a substitute appearance away to Vissel Kobe on 12 September. It is now time for Hirakawa to start justifying the old comparisons with his former teammate, Takefusa Kubo.
 
**October 2021 update** Appeared set for regular action in the Japanese second division this year with a loan to Matsumoto Yamaga, but his poor luck with injuries continued early in the season when a broken metatarsal ruled him out of action for three months. He was eased back into action this summer and has now established himself as a regular in the struggling side’s midfield.
 
**September 2022 update** Hirakawa was finally able to put injuries behind him for a sustained spell in the Matsumoto Yamaga team in the second half of 2021 but couldn’t prevent them finishing bottom of the J2 League. His appearances upon returning to parent club FC Tokyo this year were restricted to cup games and, in August, he made the decision to transfer permanently to J2 promotion chasers Roasso Kumamoto.
