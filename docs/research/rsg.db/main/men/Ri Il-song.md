After Ri impressed in the AFC Under-16 Championships back in 2019, there were whispers that North Korea had found their own Son Heung-min. This winger can play on both sides of attack, with pace that causes problems, and loves to run at defenders. With a good first touch and an eye for goal, there was talk, pre-pandemic of course, of a move to Spain where Ri could hone his skills and get what was felt to be the missing ingredient – more international exposure and experience. It has yet to happen but hopes are high that it still will. If a lack of football over the last 12 months hasn’t set the 17-year-old back, the future is bright indeed. **[John Duerden](https://twitter.com/JohnnyDuerden?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** There’s been so little football in North Korea this year that Ri has fallen off the radar a little.  
  
**October 2023 update** Has struggled to get any playing time with North Korea shut off due to Covid but the football scene is starting to run again and Ri is currently with the Under-23 team at the Asian Games.
