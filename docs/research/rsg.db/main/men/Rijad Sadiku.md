Sadiku idolises Paolo Maldini, although he is too young to remember the great Italian at his peak. Sadiku is an elite prospect and everything you want from a modern ball-playing defender. It is hard to pinpoint any significant weaknesses because he seems to have it all: natural fitness, elegance, calmness, composure, aerial power, anticipation, positional sense, vision and a professional attitude. He accomplished the rare feat of taking part in two European under-17 championships, but already plays for the under-19 side and has been with the senior FK Sarajevo squad since the beginning of this season, although he is yet to make his league debut. Turns 18 in the winter transfer window, which probably means he won’t be staying there much longer. **[Aleksandar Holiga](https://twitter.com/AlexHoliga)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Signed by the Belgian top-flight club Mouscron in January without making a senior appearance for FK Sarajevo, but soon found himself out for months with a serious ankle injury.  
  
**October 2019 update** After recovering from injury, he was given a chance in three league play-off games for Mouscron but had not featured in the new season by mid-September.  
  
**October 2020 update** Released by Mouscron in February and signed by Bosnian top-division club Mladost Kakanj. Moved to title-contenders Zrinjski Mostar this summer.  
  
**October 2021 update** Now with the league’s also-rans Rudar Prijedor, his career is not moving in the direction he would have hoped for a couple of years ago.  
  
**September 2022 update** Another year, another club for him: joined FC Botosani in the Romanian top-tier this summer but, as of mid-September, is yet to make an appearance for them.
