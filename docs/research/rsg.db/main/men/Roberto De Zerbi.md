It's the classic Brazilian [[DB/Docs 1/Tactics/Formations/4-2-2-2]]. Was Nagelsmann doing this as well?
The full backs actually do underlaps. The wingers stay super wide and stretch the pitch. The front four all come off the line and play in the half-space. It's impossible to defend.
The central double box keeps it really tight and allows you to play possession.
This is the counter to a lot of modern footballing problems. Arsenal should switch to this when we have a low block team.
You beat the initial press with the possession and numerical advantage of 6 v 7 at the back. You transition quickly and hit a forward, who lays it off, breaking several lines. You play a long ball in behind or continue to play between the lines.
Everyone needs to be good on the ball and be able to dribble and pass. The forwards need to work hard and move really well. Walcott would be perfect in this team.
I can model the Brighton team statistically and also look to bring some comparisons from the other teams I mentioned above.
This is one of about 10 archetypes though. 4-2-2-2 Movement. DZ. De Zerbi. Drop Zone/Danger Zone.

Brighton minimise the opposition's defensive transition period to almost 0 seconds, initiating a numerical advantage and mobile defensive backline with ease.
