- Sensini was a consistent, decisive, and intelligent player.
- He was also extremely tactically versatile, and was capable of playing in any defensive or midfield position.
- Throughout his career, he was deployed as a full-back, an attacking wing-back, a centre-back, or even as a central or defensive midfielder.
- Primarily excelled defensively as a ball winner, due to his tackling ability.
- He was also a player with good technique, who was capable of making attacking runs and contributing offensively for his team with occasional goals
- His professional playing career started in 1986 for [[Newell's Old Boys]]; where he won his first title
- Relocated to Italy in 1989, joining [[dev/main/clubs/Udinese]] alongside compatriot [[dev/main/men/Abel Balbo]].
- Sensini played four seasons for the Bianconeri, before moving to [[Parma]] in 1993, where he remained for a further six seasons.
- During this period Sensini sometimes played in midfield.
- Won two UEFA Cups, two Italian Cups, and the UEFA Super Cup with the gialloblu, also reaching the final of the European Cup Winners' Cup in 1994.
- He would then have a brief stint with S.S. Lazio starting from the 1999-2000 season
- Won the scudetto, the Italian Cup, the UEFA Super Cup, and the Supercoppa Italiana in his first season.
- After two years with the club, he moved back to Parma for a further year, winning another Coppa Italia in 2002
- Returned to his first Italian club, Udinese, in 2002.
- In 2005, he was one of the most consistent performers in Udinese's surprise qualification for the UEFA Champions League.
- Sensini was one of the oldest players of the 2005–06 Serie A, aged of 39 years, two months and 26 days.
- He set a record as the oldest foreign player to score a goal in first division.
- With over 380 matches at the top level, he was considered as one of the most experienced defenders of the Italian league
- Often nicknamed nonno (grandfather) by Italian sports journalists. He retired at the end of the season.
- "Boquita" Sensini debuted for his national team in 1987, and played his last match in 2000.
- He played in the World Cup 1990, 1994 and 1998.
- In the 1990 FIFA World Cup Final Sensini conceded the disputed penalty kick from which West Germany scored their winning goal.
- He helped his country to a third place finish at the 1989 Copa América
- He was also part of the championship teams in the 1991 Copa America and 1993 Copa America tournaments.
- One of the three over-23 players allowed per squad in the silver medal-winning team at the 1996 Summer Olympics in Atlanta, Georgia.
