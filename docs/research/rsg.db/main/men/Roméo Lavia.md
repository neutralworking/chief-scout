At Anderlecht they were sad and no wonder why. It was the summer of 2020 and they had lost their biggest talent, Roméo Lavia, on a free to Manchester City. In the Belgian Pro League he would already have been a regular, but Lavia chose the difficult way. “As a young player you have one chance out of a million to break through at City”, says Vincent Kompany, the former Manchester City captain and now Anderlecht manager. “But Romeo can do it. He’s a rare talent.” Lavia is a strong, mature and ball-playing defensive midfielder. His role models are Fernandinho and Sergio Busquets. He likes defending, playing the ball out with both feet and making forward runs. Two years ago he played in the KDB Cup, Kevin De Bruyne’s tournament in his hometown Drongen and last month he made his City debut alongside Phil Foden and De Bruyne. **[Kristof Terreur](https://twitter.com/HLNinEngeland)**  
  
**September 2022 update** Got further minutes for City in the FA and EFL Cups and was on the bench for several Premier League games before being signed by Southampton for a whopping £10.5m. Began the season in Ralph Hassenhüttl’s starting XI and was linked with a move to Chelsea on deadline day before he suffered a hamstring injury.  
  
**October 2023 update** After a hugely impressive season at Southampton, without being able to prevent them from being relegated, Lavia became the subject of a tug-of-war between Chelsea and Liverpool in the summer of 2023. The west London paid an initial fee of £53m for the 19-year-old and co-sporting directors Laurence Stewart and Paul Winstanley said: "He demonstrated his quality in the Premier League last season at Southampton, showing maturity despite his young age, and is a player we have monitored for some time. We believe he is ready to make an impact at Chelsea throughout the current campaign, and in the coming years."
