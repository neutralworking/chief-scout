Born in Guinea-Bissau, Ronaldo Camará was eight years old when he moved to Portugal, where his father already worked. He joined Sporting in 2012 after being spotted playing in a park round the corner from Sporting’s José Alvalade Stadium by Aurélio Pereira, the scout who also discovered Paulo Futre, Luís Figo and Cristiano Ronaldo. Three years later Camará decided to switch Sporting for rival Benfica, where he was a member of the team that in August lost the Uefa Youth League final to Real Madrid. He prefers to play as a No10, but has grown into a true box-to-box player, with a lot of technical quality and excellent passing, although he can increase his impact in the last third of the field. **[Nuno Travassos](https://twitter.com/nunotravassos?lang=en)**

**October 2021 update** Played very little at the start of 2021 because of an impasse in contract negotiations but a new deal was finally sorted in July and he is back performing for Benfica’s B side.  
  
**September 2022 update** Decided to leave Benfica in January 2022, joining Silvio Berlusconi’s and Adriano Galliani’s Monza, but he is yet to make his first-team debut at the time of writing.  
  
**October 2023 update** It never happened for Camerá at Monza – he never played for the first team – before a move back to Portugal in January to join Feirense in the second division. He is still there but not yet a regular in the first team.
