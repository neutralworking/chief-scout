When Bazdar scored five goals in a single youth league game last summer, he did not even seem particularly enthused: “I’m a midfielder and my primary task is to create, not score,” he said. But he did keep scoring at an incredible rate of more than one goal per game and was soon moved to the senior squad, getting his debut in May. Whether he will end up playing up front or behind the striker is still uncertain because he has the skills for both roles: pace, acceleration, dribbling and, obviously, finishing, but also passing and vision. He likes to move deeper into midfield, uses both feet and is very intelligent in his movement off the ball; high work rate and team play mean he is good at the pressing game as well. This young man just seems to have it all. **[Aleksandar Holiga](https://twitter.com/AlexHoliga)**

**September 2022 update** A regular off-the-bench option for Partizan this season, he has already scored twice in 125 minutes spread on eight league appearances. European powerhouses such as the two Madrid giants are rumoured to be interested but Bazdar said he “wasn’t ready to leave” and signed a new contract with the club. He is firmly committed to his goals: “There are no big players without discipline and iron will,” he says. “That’s my path as well.”  
  
**October 2023 update** Operating mainly from the bench, he has 48 appearances for Partizan, scoring four goals (none this season though, at the time of writing). “I’m always hungry for goals;” he says. There have been plenty of rumours of a transfer abroad, although smaller clubs are now being mentioned.
