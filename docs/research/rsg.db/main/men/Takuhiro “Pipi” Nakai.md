PositionMidfielder

Born24 October 2003

Like Takefusa Kubo, the 19-year-old forward currently on loan from Real Madrid to Villarreal, Takuhiro Nakai’s precocious ball skills came to the attention of the internet, Japanese television, and one of La Liga’s grandest clubs when he was still at elementary school. Videos of Nakai bewildering his contemporaries with quicksilver dribbling skills went viral when he was just seven, and he was then invited to take part in a Japanese training camp hosted by Real in 2012, and invited for trials a year later. His technique, ball retention, vision, and all-round quality impressed coaches enough for the young midfielder to officially join the club shortly before his 11th birthday in 2014. Nakai struggled to adapt to his new surroundings initially – the nickname “Pipi” stemmed from his tendency for tears – but his progression through the ranks since has impressed. At 16, he was the youngest player to be called into the Real Madrid under-19 squad by club legend Raúl for the resumption of the 2019-20 Uefa Youth League this August. **[Ben Mabley](https://twitter.com/BenMabley?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** Has enjoyed a year of rapid progression, gaining the opportunity to train with the Real Madrid first team in October 2020 and being promoted ahead of time to the U19 Juvenil A squad midway through the 2020-21season. He spent the 2021 pre-season as part of the Real Madrid Castilla side, appearing in friendly matches and making the bench for the second Primera División RFEF (third tier) match of the 2021-22 campaign.  
  
**September 2022 update** Expectations surrounding Nakai remain particularly high. He crowned his 2021-22 season with the winning goal for Real Madrid in the Copa del Rey Juvenil final against Espanyol and was officially promoted from the U19 Juvenil A squad to become a full time member of Real Madrid Castilla for the current season. In February he signed a new three-year contract with the club, while in July, it was announced that he had signed a management contract with Rakuten Sports.  
  
**October 2023 update** Nakai didn’t end up getting much game time for Real Madrid Castilla after promotion from the Under-19 squad and has been loaned out this season to another team in the Spanish third tier, Rayo Majadahonda, to continue his development.
