Thill is one of the best direct free-kick takers emerging in this generation. But his proficiency at dead-ball situations belies the type of player the 11-times Luxembourg international is developing into. He is proficient at holding up the ball and drawing fouls, while also confident taking on defenders. The 17-year-old is rated extremely highly internally at the French side Metz, where he has been likened to their former midfielder Miralem Pjanic because of his footballing intelligence and free-kick expertise. While an excellent attacker, Thill is also disciplined defensively; a skill he will continue to develop while playing with Luxembourg. He played 59 minutes in Luxembourg’s historic 0-0 draw with France in World Cup qualifying in September. **[Stephen Ganavas](https://twitter.com/scoutedftbl?lang=en)**
 
**October 2018 update** Thill found himself restricted to cup appearances last season as Metz were relegated after finishing last in Ligue 1. He has been loaned to Pau this season and is getting regular minutes in France’s third division, while also featuring regularly for Luxembourg.
 
**October 2019 update** Thill enjoyed an excellent loan season with Pau, scoring 12 times, and has stepped up to Ligue 2 with Orléans for this season. He was linked with Norwich during the summer and hopes remain high that he will have a top-level career.
 
**October 2020 update** Could not prevent Orleans from being relegated last season and joined Madeira-based Nacional in September on a permanent deal.
 
**October 2021 update** Has now made his way to Ukraine, joining Vorskla Poltava on a free transfer in August.
 
**September 2022 update** The war having stopped all football in Ukraine he joined first Örebro in the Swedish second tier on loan and then AIK in the top-flight, where he has been playing across midfield.
