It wasn’t too long ago when Karim Benzema couldn't stand the thought of Vinicius Junior receiving possession. "Don't play with him, on my mother's life he is playing against us," the French forward told Ferland Mendy during a Champions League group stage clash in 2020.   
  
Oh, how the tables have turned!  
  
Vinicius' evolution from a spritely but indecisive winger into final third machine played a key role in Real Madrid’s Champions League victory in 2021/22. He also notched 27 La Liga goal contributions that season and struck up a deadly partnership with the once critical Benzema.  
  
He's now the leading light at the Bernabeu in light of Benzema's departure to Saudi Arabia. The Brazilian has copped terrible abuse in Spain but keeps rising above the hate and is arguably the best out-and-out winger in the world.




ClubFlamengo (on loan from Real Madrid)

PositionAttacking midfielder

Born12 July 2000

He had never played as professional, but, at 16, was sold to Real Madrid for €45m – the second most expensive Brazilian transfer of all-time, behind Neymar. Vinicius Júnior’s numbers are impressive, as is his ability. Having joined Flamengo at the age of 10, he has always been seen as a brilliant prospect and is showing that now in the senior team. Proof of his ability was the goal he scored against Atlético Goianiense in August, when his finish reminded many people of Romário’s effort against Uruguay in 1993. In March, before his professional debut, he was the top scorer and elected the best player at South American under-17 championship. He is due to move to the Spanish capital on his 18th birthday. **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2018 update** Missed out on a place in Brazil’s World Cup squad but impressed throughout the season for Flamengo before his move to Real Madrid, where he has started well for Castilla, the reserve team.  
  
**October 2019 update** A tremendous year for the youngster, who played – and shone – for Real Madrid in La Liga and Champions League. Was called up to the national team for the first time in Feburary but suffered an injury and could not play. Made his debut for the Seleção seven months later, against Peru. Still only 19.  
  
**October 2020 update** This was his second season at Real and you could see him growing in confidence, aided by the trust given to him by Zinedine Zidane and the fans. Has made a remarkable 79 appearances for Real Madrid already and won his first La Liga title at the end of the 2019-20 season.  
  
**October 2021 update** A household name now around the world, he is continuing to progress. Last season, he played 49 games at Real Madrid and scored six goals and has started this campaign in inspirational form, scoring five goals in five games and being voted Real Madrid fans’ player of the month for August. For the Seleção, however, it is a different matter and failed to make an impresstion at the Copa América, playing fewer than 70 minutes in a tournament that was won by arch-rivals Argentina.  
  
**September 2022 update** At the age of 22 he has become a world super star and one of Real Madrid’s most important players. Scored the only goal of the Champions League final against Liverpool to give Real their 14th European Cup triumph.
