Vinicius Tobias has a typical background of a Brazilian footballer from a poor background who saw in sports a way to have a better life. Born in São Paulo and raised in Buraco Quente Favela with five brothers, he did not have an easy upbringing, as Renato Silva, who has run a social project called RSoccer in the city since 2006, helping more than 600 children, tells the Guardian. “He was playing normally and suddenly fainted. So I asked him what happened? He answered he was hungry because hadn’t eaten anything.” One year later, in 2016, he moved to Internacional and his life changed immediately. In the first season, he put on eight kilos and the development in his football was clear to see. “He is a No 10 playing as right-back,” said Jos Boto, the scout at Shakhtar Donetsk who was responsible for the club signing the player in July for €5m. Many experts believe he is a Brazil international in the making. **[Thiago Rabelo](https://twitter.com/thiagorabelo1?lang=en)**  
  
**September 2022 update** The war in Ukraine stopped Vinicius Tobias’ progress and after a period of not playing he was loaned to Real Madrid Castilla. His first few months in Spain were impressive and Carlo Ancelloti, said that Vinicius may well be the future at Real Madrid. His deal expires next summer, when Real have to decide whether to sign him permanently.  
  
**October 2023 update** The loan contract with Real Madrid was renewed for one more season, but he is still waiting for a chance with the first team. There is hope though as he was included in the squad for the recent Champions League game against Hertha Berlin and sat on the bench, but did not come on.
