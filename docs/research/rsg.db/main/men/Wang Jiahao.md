The world is waiting for the first Chinese superstar and although that label is too heavy to put on Wang’s shoulders, there are high hopes that he can make it big. For a start, the speedy winger – who can play in pretty much any attacking position – was born in Spain. Last year he starred for Real Murcia’s youth team and has been getting plenty of minutes in academies around the country. According to excited observers in Beijing, a whole host of clubs in La Liga are keeping tabs on the player who is more Spanish in style than Chinese. **[John Duerden](https://twitter.com/JohnnyDuerden?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2018 update** The forward has joined the Spanish third-tier team Jumilla, who are Chinese-owned and a partner of Wolverhampton Wanderers.  
  
**October 2019 update** Was transferred to Wolves in January 2019 and, having spent some time with Sporting Under-23s, he is on loan at another Portuguese side, FC Famalicão and their Under-23 side.  
  
**October 2020 update** Another season and another loan away from Wolves, this time back in China with Nantong Zhiyun, who play in the second tier. Has started most games on the bench so far.  
  
**October 2021 update** Not exactly setting the world alight spending most of his time on Nantong Zhiyun’s bench in the Chinese second division after making his loan move permanent.  
  
**September 2022 update** Playing for Nantong Zhiyun, who are looking good for promotion to the Chinese Super League.
