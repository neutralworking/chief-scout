PositionForward

Born5 November 2003

A breakout star at last year’s Under-17 World Cup, Gnonto was only added to the Italy squad as a late replacement for Sebastiano Esposito, but went on to top score for the Azzurrini with three goals. Unfortunately for Inter, such efforts also put him in the shop window, and he was signed out of their academy by FC Zurich in April. A confident dribbler, his impressive close control while moving at top speed allows him to weave nimbly through defences. Despite standing only around 5ft 7in, his muscular frame means he is not easily bullied off the ball either. **[Nicky Bandini](https://twitter.com/nickybandini?lang=en)**  
  
**October 2021 update** The move to Switzerland appears to have worked out with the forward having made 34 Swiss Super League appearances despite being still only 17. He has gone from being just a talented player around the first team to a proper, important squad member.  
  
**September 2022 update** After another impressive season in the Swiss Super League, Leeds pounced and signed the forward/winger for a fee of around £4m. “Leeds is an incredible club with an incredible history and the Premier League is something I wanted,” he said. Roberto Mancini is a fan too and gave the youngster his international debut against Germany in June 2022.  
  
**October 2023 update** Had to wait patiently for his debut but soon grew into a key player for Jesse Marsch. Sadly, he could not avoid Leeds getting relegated and it looked like he could be on the move in the summer but stayed put in the end. Out injured at the moment but expected back soon.
