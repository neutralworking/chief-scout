Czech Republic was successful at the turn of 20th and 21st centuries with the generation of top players performing in best competitions and clubs. Such names as Pavel Nedvěd, Karel Poborsky, Vladimír Šmicer, Patrik Berger, Jan Koller, Marek Jankulovski are known by everyone across the world. Some guys from that generation as Tomas Rosický and Petr Čech are still playing at the highest level. After that generation Czechs were playing in Euro final tournament, but without any success. 

In that time they decided to change their approach to developing talents and started systematic changes in the structure of competitions. Local clubs has become more modern and followed best European practices. As a result, there started attracting very young and promising footballers in their teams who can become new stars of Czech football. In 2011 Czech U19 was 2nd in the final tournament. From that squad Pavel Kaderabek moved to Hoffenheim, Tomáš Kalas is in Middlesbrough FC and many other players are waiting for their transfers to more solid competitions. Current main national team has a lot of Synot Liga representa- tives. It was one of the first countries who qualified for Euro 2016, beating Netherlands on their way. Some of these players are still in their current clubs, so we provided detailed information on them. Czech national team has all prerequisites for a successful campaign this summer. 

According to Martin Riha, Czech agent, the league is sometimes underrated power. From his point of view it is very demanding and has own specifics. Players need to show their dueling behavior and be good tactically. If a player from abroad comes here and adapts, he can attract clubs from best European competitions. All he needs is to show constant progress and improvement of his football skills. Our manager Jakub Andrýsek can prove his words: “When we see Fitness reports from Czech league, we understand that this is one of the most challenging leagues in Europe. Apart from good tactical level, players have to be also very well prepared physically”. 

Moreover, competition in Synot Liga has increased drastically in the recent years. Now not only Sparta and Viktoria Plzen seem to be powerful, but also other clubs such as Jablonec, Slovan Liberec and Mlada Boleslav are quite hard to be beaten. It also contributes to development of local football. When our scouts 

analyzed the country, all of them noticed good education of players. Most of the attackers are very fast and move wisely without ball, while defenders show proper positioning and necessary tenacity. Czech goalkeeping school continues to provide high-quality leaders, with which teams can be confident and relaxed.

Ideals
Quality in the final third (skill) - Attack/Assault
Hard work and consistency in the middle (engine) - Gain territory/Defend territory
Robust protection at the back (walls) - Defend/Guard
