The Counter/Defence-To-Attack Transition
The key to this tactic
Possession is not important, as they control the game off theball
They invite teams to push up onto them while they stay deep and compact, then move them into areas they want them to pounce on the ball
When they get the ball they want the ball and players forward ASAP
Pace and power up front
Pace to stretch defences
Power in 10 position to hold up
Inverted wings with good runs in half-spaces (the creators)
Unpredictable movement from inverted wingers makes it a nightmare for defences on the counter
Offensive setup
Outside lane fullbacks
Two number 6s/double pivot
Inverted wingers that come into half spaces/inside lanes
One stretch forward and one deeper target man
Defensive
Immediate pressure on ball carrier and surrounding space (Counter pressing)
Push forwards when losing ball, with 2 3 between holding midfielders and fullbacks
