
### 1. **Agent Offers Page**

#### **Agent Offers Overview**
- **Centralized Offer Management**: All agent offers should be consolidated on a single page, making it easy for the player to manage multiple offers at once.
- **Hyperbolic Reports**: Agents tend to oversell their clients, offering glowing reports filled with hyperbole. However, each agent has a **Trustworthiness Rating** (1-5 stars), which influences how much weight the player should give to their claims.
- **Trustworthiness and Relationships**: The more deals the player completes with an agent, the more trust they build, potentially leading to more honest evaluations. Conversely, an agent who feels slighted might exaggerate even more or become difficult to deal with.
- **Actionable Options**:
  - **Negotiate**: Enter negotiations directly from this page.
  - **Request More Info**: Ask for additional details, like video clips, stats, or a scouting report (with associated costs or delays).
  - **Mark for Follow-Up**: Add the player to your shortlist or radar for future consideration.

### 2. **Club Secretary's Role**

#### **Key Functions**
- **Contact Information Management**: The club secretary is responsible for finding and managing contact information for players, agents, and other clubs. Their effectiveness is determined by their **Competency Rating**:
  - **High Competency**: Can find contact information for most targets quickly, reducing delays in negotiations.
  - **Low Competency**: Might struggle to find contacts, leading to delays or even missed opportunities.
- **Match Attendance Coordination**: Better secretaries can organize travel and match attendance more efficiently, allowing the player to attend more games and gather better information on potential targets.
- **Information Gathering**: Secretaries can sometimes gather basic information about potential transfer targets, such as recent injuries, contract status, or general player mood, giving the player a slight edge in negotiations.

### 3. **News Section**

#### **Transfer News Feed**
- **Transfer Listings**: Notifications when players are officially listed for transfer by other clubs.
- **Rumours**: Speculative reports from various sources. The accuracy of these rumours can vary, and the player can choose to investigate further or ignore them.
- **Contract Updates**: Information on players signing new contracts, which could either indicate their availability (if it's a tactic to increase value) or their commitment to their current club.
- **Market Trends**: Occasional updates on market trends, such as which positions are in high demand or which leagues are seeing the most transfer activity.

### 4. **Shortlists**

#### **Director's Shopping List**
- **Customizable Lists**: The player can create and manage multiple shortlists based on different criteria (e.g., "Immediate Needs," "Future Stars," "Bargains").
- **Prioritization**: Players on the shortlist can be prioritized, with higher priority targets receiving more frequent updates and attention from the scouting team.
- **Alerts**: The player can set alerts for specific players, so they are notified if any significant changes occur (e.g., the player being listed for transfer, new contract terms, or injury).

### 5. **Radar Screen**

#### **Interactive Radar Interface**
- **Visual Representation**: The radar screen provides a visual representation of the player’s scouting radar. The closer a player is to the center, the better they match the club’s needs.
- **Customizable Range**: The radar’s range can be adjusted based on the player’s preferences or needs. For example:
  - **Close Range**: Focuses on players who perfectly match the club's current tactical and financial criteria.
  - **Extended Range**: Includes players who might be slightly outside the preferred criteria but are still of interest.
- **Player Attributes**: Hovering over a player on the radar provides a quick summary of their key attributes and recent form. Clicking on them brings up more detailed reports.
- **Radar Updates**: The radar is updated dynamically as scouts return with new information or as the transfer market evolves. Players can appear or disappear from the radar based on real-time events (e.g., a new scout report or a sudden transfer listing).

### 6. **Search Functionality**

#### **Advanced Search**
- **Filter by Criteria**: Players can search for players using various filters such as age, position, contract status, transfer status, market value, and recent performance.
- **Saved Searches**: Frequently used searches can be saved for quick access, allowing the player to revisit them as needed.
- **Cross-Referencing**: Search results can be cross-referenced with current shortlists or radar findings, ensuring that no potential target is overlooked.

### 7. **Reports Archive**

#### **Recent Reports**
- **Default View**: The most recent reports are shown by default, providing the latest insights from scouts and analysts.
- **Search and Filters**: The player can search through all past reports using filters (e.g., by player name, report date, scout, or specific attribute focus).
- **Detailed Reports**: Clicking on a report opens a detailed view, showing all the gathered information, including scout notes, videos, and statistical breakdowns.

### User Interface and Experience

#### **Engaging and Intuitive UI**
- **Unified Dashboard**: The transfer market should have a unified dashboard where the player can easily navigate between News, Shortlists, Radar, Search, and Reports. This ensures that the player feels in control and can make informed decisions without feeling overwhelmed.
- **Dynamic and Responsive**: The UI should be dynamic, with elements like the radar and news feed updating in real-time to reflect ongoing market changes. Notifications and alerts should be visually distinct, drawing the player’s attention to important developments.
- **Immersive Interaction**: The radar screen, in particular, should be a visually engaging and interactive experience. Players should enjoy exploring it, tweaking the range, and discovering new targets as they appear on the radar.
